# Best ETFs by Time Horizon — Category/Index Decision

Planning notes for picking the best ETFs over 1, 3, and 10 year holding horizons. Each
horizon goes through three steps: **(1) High Category/Index Decision** (which buckets to
fish in), **(2) Filters under each finalized category** (numeric screens), **(3) Shortlist
+ final picks**. This doc currently covers only step (1) for the 3-year and 10-year
horizons. The 1-year version lives in the planning workspace and is the reference for
style + structure.

The actual ETF picking is the next step after the decisions here are agreed.

---

## 3) Find 2 ETFs which will grow most in next 3 years

### The approach taken for shortlisting

#### High Category/Index Decision:

We want max growth so target 12-15% CAGR over 3 years (around 50% total). 3 years is
enough time for a few macro themes to play out, but not enough for slow 10-year stories
like demographics or commodity supercycles. So we want things that are mid-cycle or just
starting.

**Asset classes / sectors we are skipping**

- **Fixed Income / Bonds**: 4-6% yield. We need 12-15%. Skipped.
- **Currency / Cash**: No growth. Skipped.
- **Commodities (gold, silver, precious metals)**: Already ran up a lot in 2024-2025.
  Already priced in for the next 3 years. Better fit for the 10-year basket.
- **Oil / Energy**: Too dependent on Iran/Russia/Middle East war. If a war ends, oil
  crashes. Too binary for a 3-year base case.
- **Inverse / Leveraged ETFs**: Decay + too risky for a 3-year hold.
- **Crypto**: 50-70% drawdowns are common. A 3-year window may catch a bad cycle.
- **Broad Small Cap**: People have been calling for the "small cap rally" for 3 years
  and it hasn't come. Needs both Fed cuts AND earnings recovery to work. Too many ifs.
- **Broad Real Estate / REITs**: Office + retail still bleeding from WFH and ecommerce.
  Data-center and industrial sub-REITs are interesting, but at the broad level it's a
  net drag.
- **Healthcare (broad)**: Heavy regulation, slow earnings growth. Will probably give
  8-10%. Demographic story is real but it's a 10-year story, not 3-year.
- **Consumer Discretionary**: Tied to jobs and interest rates. Works if Fed cuts and
  unemployment stays low. Worst sector if recession. Too cycle-dependent for a base case.
- **Consumer Staples**: Defensive, 6-8% returns.
- **Materials (broad)**: Too tied to China demand which is structurally weak.
- **Communications Services**: Mostly Meta + Google + Netflix — already covered inside
  any large-growth ETF. No reason to double up.
- **Financials (broad)**: Benefit from rate cuts and deregulation, but bank earnings
  tied to credit cycle which could turn. Not a clean 3-year story.

**Categories we ARE picking**

- **Large Growth / Quality** (moderate risk)
   - Same idea as 1-year. 3-year smoothing makes this stronger because short-term
     drawdowns don't dominate the return. Quality screens (GARP / QUAL / SPMO style)
     push 14-16% CAGR.
   - Multiples are stretched (S&P forward P/E ~22), so returns will be earnings-driven
     not multiple-expansion-driven.

- **AI / Semiconductors** (highest risk + highest reward)
   - Yes, already priced in for hyperscaler-direct names. But 3 years is enough time
     for actual revenue to catch up to today's AI capex spend. Nvidia / AMD / Broadcom
     ship multiple chip generations by 2028.
   - Hyperscaler capex commitments (MSFT, META, GOOG, AMZN) are multi-year contracts —
     demand is locked in for 3+ years.
   - Risk: any AI capex slowdown in 2026-2027 hits semis first.

- **Industrials** (moderate risk)
   - Why industrials and not health care or consumer discretionary?
   - CHIPS Act + IRA capex finally hits actual plant builds in 2026-2028 — right in
     our window. Healthcare doesn't have a similar 3-year catalyst (its drivers are
     10-year demographic). Consumer disc is too cycle-dependent.
   - Defense spending up globally (Europe rearming, Asia rearming, US replenishing).
   - Sits at the intersection of AI data-center buildout (electrical equipment, HVAC),
     defense, and reshoring — 3 demand drivers stacking.

- **Utilities / Power** (moderate risk, new vs 1-year)
   - AI data centers + EVs + reshoring need 3-5x current US power demand by 2030.
     Utilities sign new contracts and build generation in 2026-2028.
   - Why not in 1-year? Utilities are rate-sensitive (bond proxies) — slow to move at
     cycle starts. By 3-year the revenue shows up.
   - Returns capped by regulated returns, so expect 12-15% not 25%.

- **Emerging Markets — targeted, NOT broad** (moderate risk)
   - Dollar likely weakens 2026-2027 as Fed cuts. EM debt gets easier to service. EM
     consumer recovers.
   - Pick India, Southeast Asia, Latin America (Mexico). Avoid the broad EM index
     because ~25-30% of it is China — Chinese property + tech regulation overhangs are
     not fixable in a 3-year window.

- **Biotech (small allocation, higher risk)**
   - XBI is down ~40% from 2021 peak. M&A picks up when rates fall — large pharma
     needs to refill pipelines as the 2026-2030 patent cliff hits.
   - Higher risk: binary FDA outcomes, many companies still no earnings.

#### Is it already priced in? (per pick)

- **Semis**: Partially priced in for AI-direct names (NVDA, AMD). Less priced in for
  the second-derivative plays (cooling, power, fabs equipment). Net: still runway.
- **Large Growth / S&P 500**: Multiples stretched. Returns will be earnings-driven not
  multiple expansion. So expect lower CAGR than 2020-2024.
- **Utilities + Industrials**: Not priced in. Both still trade at reasonable forward
  multiples relative to forward earnings growth.
- **Emerging Markets**: Cheap. Forward P/E ~12-14 for India, single digits for some
  others. Very much not priced in.
- **Biotech**: Beat-down. Not priced in.

#### Macro tailwinds + risks

- Fed cuts likely continue through 2026 → supports equities, weakens USD.
- 2028 US election adds uncertainty toward the back of the window.
- Recession risk: 30-40% probability over a 3-year window. Sector mix has to survive
  one mild recession.
- Geopolitical: Iran, Russia, China flashpoints all live.

## 4) Find 2 ETFs which will grow most in next 10 years

### The approach taken for shortlisting

#### High Category/Index Decision:

Target: 12-15% CAGR over 10 years (around 3-4x money). Completely different game from
1-year and 3-year. Short-term noise (rates, election, cycles) averages out. We need
multi-decade structural trends — demographics, technology shifts, supply shortages,
monetary regimes.

**Asset classes / sectors we are skipping**

- **Fixed Income / Bonds**: Over 10 years bonds rarely beat equities. 4-5% today isn't
  enough.
- **Currency / Cash**: Loses to inflation over a decade.
- **Inverse / Leveraged ETFs**: Decay over 10 years guarantees losses even when the
  underlying index goes up. Mathematically broken for long holds.
- **Crypto**: Regulatory + volatility risk over a decade is too high. Acknowledge BTC
  could surprise, but not picking it as a core 10-year holding.
- **Oil / Energy (traditional)**: 10-year electrification curves (EVs, heat pumps, grid
  storage) eat into oil demand. Major oils likely underperform. Skipped at broad level.
- **Broad REITs**: Office + retail in structural decline. Data center, industrial,
  cell tower sub-REITs are different but better picked directly.
- **Japan / Europe (broad developed ex-US)**: Demographics are negative — working-age
  population shrinking without immigration replacement. Hard to grow earnings when the
  workforce shrinks.
- **Broad China / Greater China**: Working-age population peaked 2014. Property bubble
  unwinding. Government intervenes in private sector. Skipped.
- **Consumer Discretionary (broad)**: Tracks GDP — gives index returns not
  outperformance over a decade.
- **Consumer Staples**: 6-8% defensives. Not enough.
- **Financials (broad)**: Net interest margin compressed long-term. Fintech disruption
  ongoing. Mixed.
- **Communications Services**: Already overlapping with broad large-cap quality.

**Categories we ARE picking**

- **Quality / Wide-Moat Equities** (lower risk, core of the basket)
   - Compounders with durable competitive advantages (MOAT / QUAL / wide-moat funds).
     Boring but reliable 10-12% compounding over a decade.
   - Same foundational role as "large growth" plays in the 1-year basket, but with a
     quality screen instead of growth screen.

- **AI Infrastructure (semis + power + data center)** (high risk + high reward)
   - Is it priced in? Partially for the 3-year horizon. But 10 years out, AI compute
     demand likely doubles many times. Today's high P/E becomes reasonable if revenue
     compounds 30-40% for several years.
   - Pick the infrastructure layer, NOT the model layer. Model companies (OpenAI,
     Anthropic) are winner-take-most and hard to play through public ETFs. Chips,
     fabs, cooling, power equipment, fiber all benefit regardless of which AI company
     wins the model war.
   - Risk: an AI winter (like 1990s) flattens returns for years.

- **Energy Transition + Power Materials (uranium, copper, grid, renewables)**
  (moderate-to-high risk)
   - Electricity demand from AI + EVs + reshoring set to roughly double over 10 years.
     Generation has to come from somewhere.
   - Uranium: nuclear is back. SMRs coming online 2028-2032. Existing fleet getting
     relicensed. Microsoft restarting Three Mile Island is a signal.
   - Copper: every EV needs ~3x more copper than an ICE car. Every data center +
     every grid upgrade needs copper. Supply is constrained — no major new mines for
     5+ years.
   - Why not broad clean energy (TAN / ICLN)? Solar panels and wind turbines are
     commoditized — the panel makers compete on thin margins. Upstream materials and
     power infrastructure capture more of the value over a decade.

- **Healthcare / Biotech (aging demographics)** (moderate risk)
   - US, Europe, China, Japan all aging fast. By 2035 the US 65+ population will be
     70M+.
   - Aging = more drugs, more devices, more procedures.
   - Pick biotech (innovation catalyst) + medical devices (steady demand). Avoid
     hospital operators (regulated margins).
   - Why this for 10-year and NOT 3-year? Demographics is a slow trend. Doesn't move
     stock prices reliably in any given 1-3 year window but compounds over 10.

- **Gold / Precious Metals (small allocation, lower risk)**
   - Already priced in? For the short term, yes. But the 10-year story is structural:
     fiscal deficits running 6-7% of GDP year after year in developed markets, central
     bank debasement, BRICS de-dollarization. Gold goes up because money supply goes
     up.
   - 1-year may catch the top of the recent rally; 10-year the trend continues
     regardless of cycle.
   - Allocation should be small (5-10% of basket) — gold is a hedge, not a growth
     driver.

- **Emerging Markets — India + Southeast Asia (targeted)** (moderate risk)
   - India: cleanest 10-year demographic + growth story. Median age 28, growing
     middle class, GDP compounding 6-7%, government push on infrastructure.
   - Vietnam, Indonesia, Philippines: same story, smaller scale.
   - Why not broad EM index? Too much China weight. Pick targeted country ETFs.
   - Why not Africa / Latin America? Higher political + currency risk. Could add a
     small frontier sleeve but not core.

- **Industrials (reshoring + infrastructure)** (moderate risk)
   - Reshoring is a 15-year project not a 3-year one. CHIPS Act + IRA seeded plants
     today; the second-order beneficiaries (automation, electrical equipment,
     machinery, HVAC) keep getting work through 2035+.
   - Defense also structural — Europe + Asia rearmament likely a multi-decade trend.

#### Is it already priced in? (per pick)

- **AI / Semis**: Priced in for a 3-year horizon, much less priced in for a 10-year
  one. If AI revenue 5x's over 10 years, today's prices look cheap.
- **Gold**: Priced in for the short term, not for the long term — debasement is a
  multi-decade story, not a 1-year story.
- **Copper / Uranium**: Spot prices have moved but mining equities haven't fully
  reflected the supply shortage yet. Underlying commodity bull case still has runway.
- **Healthcare / Biotech**: Biotech is beat-down — not priced in. Medical devices
  closer to fair value.
- **India**: Trades at a premium to broader EM but not bubble territory. P/E ~20-22
  with ~15% earnings growth — reasonable PEG.
- **Quality / Wide-Moat**: Fair to slightly expensive. Returns will be earnings-driven.

#### Macro tailwinds + risks

- Demographic aging (developed world + China) — multi-decade tailwind for healthcare,
  multi-decade headwind for developed-market broad indexes.
- AI compute demand doubling on short cycles — multi-decade if it continues.
- Debt overhang + fiscal deficits — likely drives debasement / inflation pressure over
  decades. Bullish for gold + real assets, bearish for long bonds.
- Climate transition + electrification — multi-decade tailwind for materials + power
  infrastructure.
- Risks: war (US-China over Taiwan), AI winter, sovereign debt crisis, climate shocks,
  populist policy reversals (anti-trade, anti-tech, anti-immigration), pandemic 2.0.
