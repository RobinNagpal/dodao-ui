# Best ETF Picks — by Group

[koalagains.com/etfs/groups](https://koalagains.com/etfs/groups). One pure-passive
pick + one actively-managed pick per group. Each pick evaluated on the data points
that actually matter before buying any ETF.

## 1. Broad Equity

| Metric | **QQQ** (passive pick) | **FBCG** (active pick) |
|---|---|---|
| Style | Passive index | Actively managed (Sonu Kalra since 2009 on the underlying FBGRX mutual fund) |
| Benchmark | Nasdaq-100 Index (NDX) | Russell 1000 Growth |
| Top index to compare active to | n/a — IS an index tracker | Has beaten Nasdaq-100 by ~0 pp/yr over 10y (matches) and Russell 1000 Growth by ~+3 pp/yr over 10y net of fees (via FBGRX) |
| **Expense ratio** | 0.20% | 0.59% |
| Expense vs same-index competitors | QQQM tracks the same Nasdaq-100 at **0.15%** (5 bps cheaper) — better for pure buy-and-hold. QQQ's edge is liquidity + the deepest equity-options chain on Earth, useful for hedging or income overlays | VONG (Russell 1000 Growth passive) at **0.07%** — FBCG must clear a ~52 bps/yr active hurdle. FBGRX has cleared it by ~200–300 bps/yr historically |
| Tracking diff vs index (1y / 3y / 5y / 10y / 15y) | < 5 bps / yr across every window — near-perfect tracking, all five horizons | n/a (active, no tracking mandate). vs Russell 1000 Growth via FBGRX: 1y ~+2 pp, 3y ~+2 pp, 5y ~+3 pp, 10y ~+1 pp, 15y ~+2 pp |
| AUM | ~$320B (top-3 ETF in the world) | ~$3B in the ETF + ~$80B in the FBGRX mutual fund share class — same strategy, no capacity concern |
| **Portfolio turnover** | ~10–15% / yr (annual NDX reconstitution + quarterly weight rebalances) | ~30–40% / yr (discretionary stock-picking, but turnover is moderate by active-fund standards) |
| Dividend distribution | ~0.5% annual yield, paid quarterly. Distributions are taxed at qualified-dividend rate | ~0.2% annual yield, paid once annually (December). Growth strategy → most return is capital appreciation, not yield. Tax-efficient by design |
| Max drawdown + recovery vs index | 2022: −33% vs S&P 500 −25%; recovered in ~14 months. Dot-com −83% vs S&P −50%; recovered in ~15y (worst-ever case, capped by NDX concentration in 2000) | 2022: −35% vs Russell 1000 Growth −29%; recovered in ~13 months. Generally tracks RUG drawdowns ±3 pp |
| Forward outlook on current holdings | Mag 7 ≈ 50% of fund — concentrated bet on AI / cloud / semis / platform-compounding. Aligned with the macro trend driving the last 15y. If that thesis continues, QQQ wins; if leadership rotates, concentration hurts | Heavy mega-cap tech overlap with QQQ + Eli Lilly, Mastercard, Visa, off-index growth names (recent IPOs, foreign-listed-US-revenue). PM has discretion to trim into froth that QQQ structurally cannot — a real edge **if** the PM continues to use it well |
| Securities-lending revenue | ~1–3 bps / yr returned to fund, partially offsetting the 0.20% fee — Invesco standard program | ~2–4 bps / yr returned to fund — Fidelity standard program. Slightly higher than QQQ because active funds hold more lendable smaller-cap names |
| Bid-ask spread | ~0.5–1 bp during market hours — among the tightest globally | ~5–10 bps during market hours — wider than QQQ but trivial for retail-size positions |
| **Verdict** | Cheapest, most liquid expression of the 15y-winning innovation-mega-cap trade. Bet: tech keeps leading | Best active growth ETF in the market with a multi-cycle PM track record. Bet: Kalra keeps doing what he's done since 2009. Falls back to VONG (0.07%) if the trailing-5y excess vs VONG drops below ~50 bps/yr |

**Why this pair, not VTI or any all-passive total-market pick:** the koalagains
"Broad Equity" group is about US large-cap broad-market exposure, and **every single
large-cap index has lagged the Nasdaq-100 and Russell 1000 Growth over 10y and 20y**.
Owning the average is owning the laggards. QQQ gives you the rules-based
high-conviction version; FBCG gives you the discretionary high-conviction version.
Together they are two different active alphas on the same future-growth universe.
