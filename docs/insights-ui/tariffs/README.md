### Beginner-Friendly Tariff Reduction Guide (April 2026)

A plain-English series explaining how U.S. import tariffs work and how importers legally reduce them. Five worked $10,000 product examples.

- **[basic-concepts.md](basic-concepts.md)** — Plain-English glossary of tariff terms: HTS code, MFN, Section 301/232/122, IEEPA, reciprocal tariffs, AD/CVD, USMCA, TRQ, MPF/HMF, ART, GSP, country of origin, transshipment, etc.
- **[common-strategies.md](common-strategies.md)** — Detailed how-to guide for the legal tariff-reduction methods used across all five product reports: First Sale Rule, FTZ, Bonded Warehouse, Drawback, Tariff Engineering, Country Switching, and the Re-Export Reality framework. Each strategy includes when it works, savings math, setup cost, and common mistakes.
- **[toys-china.md](toys-china.md)** — Toys from China: 7+ reduction strategies including sourcing alternatives, FTZ, bonded warehouse, first sale, tariff engineering, drawback, and the re-export reality check.
- **[sneakers-vietnam.md](sneakers-vietnam.md)** — Athletic sneakers from Vietnam: includes the leather-upper trick, Converse-style felt-sole tariff engineering, USMCA alternatives, and component-import strategies.
- **[t-shirts-bangladesh.md](t-shirts-bangladesh.md)** — Cotton T-shirts from Bangladesh: includes the new U.S.–Bangladesh ART zero-tariff TRQ for U.S.-cotton garments, fiber-blend pitfalls, and Mexico/CAFTA alternatives.
- **[bedsheets-india.md](bedsheets-india.md)** — Cotton bedsheets and terry towels from India: includes the Section 122 expiry-timing strategy, AD/CVD checks, and Mexico/Honduras alternatives.
- **[wooden-furniture-indonesia.md](wooden-furniture-indonesia.md)** — Wooden furniture from Indonesia: includes the Section 232 25% tariff, the bamboo/rattan workaround, knock-down + U.S. assembly approach, and Mexico USMCA option.
