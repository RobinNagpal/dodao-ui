## 2. Sector & Thematic Equity — **ITA + GDOC**

The KoalaGains [stock-scenarios](https://koalagains.com/stock-scenarios) page lists
**7 active US scenarios**. Five of them cluster cleanly into two sector themes:

**Defense (2 scenarios):**
- US Defense Industrial Supercycle
- US Missile Stockpile Rebuild After Iran War

**Healthcare innovation (3 scenarios):**
- GLP-1 TAM Expansion Beyond Obesity
- Eldercare & Long-Term Care Boom
- Mental Health Demand Surge

(Two more — Medicare Most-Favored-Nation Drug Price Caps and US Power Demand
Supercycle — are macroeconomic crosswinds that shape, but don't define, a sector
bet.)

If KoalaGains' own forward scenarios are the future-growth thesis we're trying to
own, the right Sector & Thematic picks are **the cleanest passive vehicle for
defense + the best active vehicle for future-healthcare**.

### Passive pick — **ITA (iShares US Aerospace & Defense ETF)**

Why ITA, not PPA or XAR:

- **ITA** — market-cap-weighted Dow Jones US Select Aerospace & Defense Index;
  $8B AUM; 0.40% fee; concentrated in the prime contractors (RTX, Lockheed,
  Northrop, GE Aerospace, Boeing, L3Harris, TransDigm). This *is* the defense
  supercycle in ETF form — when the Pentagon writes a $1B procurement check, it
  flows to these names.
- **PPA** — broader (~50 holdings, includes space and government services); 0.58%
  fee; messier exposure. Higher fee for less-pure thematic exposure.
- **XAR** — equal-weighted; 0.35% fee; tilts mid-cap (more on smaller suppliers
  and electronics). Cheaper but loses the concentration that makes the supercycle
  thesis work.

Why passive, not active: there is no actively-managed defense ETF with a
$1B+ AUM and a multi-year track record. Defense holdings are concentrated enough
(top 10 ≈ 75% of any reasonable index) that an active manager would essentially
be picking among the same 15 names. Passive captures it cleanly.

Decision rule: hold ITA as long as **US defense procurement spending CAGR > 5%**.
If procurement growth rolls over or geopolitics de-escalate enough to remove the
tailwind, the supercycle thesis breaks and ITA becomes just another sector fund.

### Active pick — **GDOC (Goldman Sachs Future Health Care Equity ETF)**

Why GDOC, not XLV or IBB:

- **GDOC** — actively managed by Goldman Sachs' healthcare team; 0.50% fee; built
  to overweight the *future* of healthcare (GLP-1, AI-enabled diagnostics,
  longevity, precision medicine, eldercare beneficiaries). Holdings include Eli
  Lilly, Novo Nordisk, Vertex, Boston Scientific, plus AI-healthcare names. This
  directly maps to 3 of KoalaGains' active healthcare scenarios.
- **XLV** — passive Health Care Select SPDR; 0.09% fee; heavyweight in UnitedHealth
  + traditional pharma + medical devices. Cheap and broad — but you get *all* of
  healthcare, including the legacy pharma names most exposed to **Medicare
  Most-Favored-Nation Drug Price Caps**. That's the wrong end of the scenario.
- **IBB** — passive biotech (NASDAQ Biotech Index); 0.45% fee; very volatile,
  small-cap-heavy, and biotech-only. Misses the GLP-1 mega-caps (Lilly, Novo).

Why active matters here: the healthcare scenarios are not a sector-wide tailwind
— they're a within-sector rotation. GLP-1 winners take share from legacy diabetes
care. Eldercare service operators benefit; legacy pharma exposed to MFN pricing
loses. An active manager can overweight the winners and underweight the losers in
a way that a passive sector ETF, by construction, cannot. **This is the right
place to pay the active hurdle** — assuming the team continues to make the call.

**Caveats — honestly:**
- AUM is ~$300–500M, below the $1B safety threshold. Not a deal-breaker, but
  flag it.
- Launched 2022, so only ~3y of live track record. Has roughly matched-to-slightly-
  beat XLV; not yet decisively proven across a full cycle.
- 41 bps active hurdle vs XLV. GDOC has cleared it so far. If trailing-3y excess
  vs XLV drops below 25 bps/yr, switch to **XLV** as the fallback and accept the
  blunter exposure.

### Why not the obvious alternatives

- **XLK (Tech) or SOXX (Semis)?** Already inside QQQ at >50% weight. Buying a
  tech sector ETF on top of QQQ doubles up on the same risk.
- **PAVE (Infrastructure) for US Power Demand Supercycle?** Reasonable, but the
  scenario is more of an *energy + utilities + industrials* crosscurrent than a
  clean thematic bet. Single-theme dollars are better deployed on defense
  (cleaner) or healthcare (more scenario coverage).
- **ARKK (innovation)?** Active, but the post-2021 track record vs QQQ is bad
  enough that the active hurdle is not being earned. Defense and healthcare are
  better-targeted thematic bets than the everything-disruptive bucket.
