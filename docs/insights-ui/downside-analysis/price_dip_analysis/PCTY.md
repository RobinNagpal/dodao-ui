# Paylocity Holding Corporation (PCTY) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Paylocity is a cloud-based human capital management (HCM) and payroll software provider targeting mid-market employers (20-5,000 employees). Payroll processing accounts for roughly 50% of revenue, HCM/benefits administration roughly 30%, and talent management/engagement roughly 20%. The company holds approximately 10% of the US payroll market with a modern cloud platform, and has excellent unit economics: 69% gross margin, 34% EBITDA margin, and 20%+ ROE with a net cash balance sheet.

PCTY posted FY2025 (ended June 2025) revenue of approximately $1.58B with recurring revenue growth of 14% YoY. FY2026 is guided for revenue of $1.73-1.74B (+10% growth), with recurring revenue of $1.62-1.63B (+10-11%). Q2 FY2026 total revenue was $416.1M (+10% YoY) with adjusted EBITDA of $142.7M (34.3% margin). FCF reached approximately $375M (+26% YoY).

The stock has fallen approximately 48% from its 52-week high of $201.62 to around $105 (and 67% from ATH of $314.50 in November 2021). The decline is a classic growth-to-value transition: the market paid 50-60x PE when growth was 20%+, but with recurring revenue growth decelerating every year (25% to 20% to 14% to 11%), the appropriate multiple is much lower. The Paychex/Paycor merger creates a larger competitor, and Rippling is disrupting from below with a modern all-in-one platform. AI-driven product adoption (PCTY's AI assistant for HR workflows) is a potential reacceleration catalyst but has not shown up in the numbers yet.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $95, implying roughly 10% further downside from $105. PCTY's valuation is driven almost entirely by the growth narrative, not the absolute level of earnings. At $105, the stock is priced for "boring 10% grower" status. If growth reaccelerates to 15%+, the multiple expands and the stock could double. If growth decelerates to 5-7%, the multiple compresses to ADP-like levels and the stock drops 25-30%.

The bull case requires AI-driven product adoption to reaccelerate recurring revenue growth to 15%+ with margin expansion — producing a re-rating to $152-173. The severe bear case envisions a US recession with SMB/mid-market layoffs compressing per-employee-per-month revenue, recurring growth dropping to 3-5%, and PCTY being re-rated as a cyclical SaaS company at 12-14x EV/EBITDA — producing a price of $53-68. The underlying business is excellent (69% gross margin, strong FCF, net cash) — this is a "what multiple does it deserve?" stock, not a "will the business survive?" stock.

## Competitive Position & Moat

PCTY has approximately 10% penetration of the addressable US mid-market payroll TAM with a modern cloud platform that is technologically superior to legacy systems from ADP and Paychex. Client retention is 92%+, and the cross-selling model (talent management, benefits, time tracking, expense management) increases wallet share over time. The $375M+ FCF and net cash balance sheet provide financial flexibility for M&A and capital returns.

However, competition is intensifying from both above and below. The Paychex/Paycor merger creates a larger mid-market competitor. Workday and ADP are moving downmarket into PCTY's core segment. Rippling is disrupting from below with a modern all-in-one platform at lower price points. Every HCM competitor is launching AI-powered HR assistants, so PCTY's AI features are incremental rather than transformative. The core payroll business is inherently slow-growth (tied to employment growth plus pricing), and the law of large numbers is working against PCTY — at $1.6B recurring revenue, each percentage point of growth requires $16M in incremental ARR.

PCTY's -67% from ATH is in line with HCM sector-wide de-rating (Paycom -60%, Ceridian -65%). The Paycom comparison is most concerning — PAYC experienced a similar growth deceleration and never recovered to its ATH, permanently re-rating to a lower multiple. Precedent-implied further downside: -10 to -25%.

## What to Watch

FY2027 initial guidance (August 2026) is the defining moment: 10%+ guide equals stability, sub-10% equals permanent de-rate. Net revenue retention above 110% confirms healthy cross-selling; below 105% signals a churn problem. AI product adoption metrics (module attach rates, ARPU lift) determine whether AI is a real growth driver or marketing. US employment data directly affects PCTY's per-employee revenue model — SMB/mid-market companies cut headcount faster than large enterprises in downturns.
