# e.l.f. Beauty (ELF) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

e.l.f. Beauty is the #1 value cosmetics brand in the US, disrupting prestige beauty at drugstore prices. Top revenue products are: color cosmetics including face, lip, and eye products (approximately 70% of revenue, the core franchise that undercuts prestige competitors by 50-70%), skin care products (approximately 20% of revenue, growing category), and the acquired Naturium brand (approximately 10% of revenue, clean beauty positioning). Products are primarily manufactured in China (approximately 75% of sourcing) and sold through mass retail (Target, Walmart, Amazon) with accelerating international expansion (UK, Canada, Italy). FY2025 revenue was $1.3B (+28.3% YoY), net income $112M (EPS $1.92), gross margin approximately 71%.

The stock has fallen approximately 61% from its 52-week high of $150.99 to around $59.35. The crash resulted from a 55% China tariff rate hitting 75% of production (approximately $50M annualized cost impact), gross margin compression of 165 basis points, a PEG ratio of 2.89 too rich for tariff headwinds, and a broader consumer discretionary selloff.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $52, implying roughly 12% further downside from $59. ELF's downside is fundamentally tariff-margin driven — with 75% China sourcing concentration, tariff changes flow almost directly to EBITDA margin.

ELF's 2017-2018 crash of -73% during a strong economy shows the stock can fall much further if the growth narrative breaks entirely.

## Competitive Position & Moat

ELF holds the fastest-growing position in mass cosmetics with a prestige-at-drugstore-prices value proposition that undercuts competitors by 50-70%. The brand has demonstrated genuine pricing power, and its digital-first approach resonates with younger consumers. International expansion (UK, Canada, Italy) provides a less tariff-exposed growth vector.

The top 2 direct competitors are NYX Professional Makeup (L'Oreal, accelerating growth in the same value cosmetics segment) and ultra-cheap imports/private-label brands (which threaten if tariff-driven price increases erode ELF's value positioning). Revenue at risk is modest: NYX acceleration could take 2-3% of market share ($35-60M, 2.7-4.6% of revenue), but the ultra-cheap import threat is diminishing as tariffs make imports more expensive too.

ELF's moat (3/5 — brand positioning + digital-first + value proposition) is moderate: the prestige-at-drugstore positioning is powerful but beauty is a fickle category where consumer preferences shift rapidly. The 75% China manufacturing concentration is the most acute vulnerability among major beauty companies.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 16x EV/EBITDA. Beauty/cosmetics peers (Estee Lauder 14-18x, Coty 10-14x, Ulta 14-16x) bracket this range. ELF's growth premium justifies the upper end. Current price of $59 implies approximately $1.50B revenue at 18% margin at 16x.

**Valuation regime check:** ELF's EV/EBITDA averaged approximately 15-20x in 2020-2022 and expanded to approximately 25-35x in 2023-2025 (hypergrowth premium). The compression from 35x to 20x reflects the tariff-driven margin concern. For the severe case, 10x EV/EBITDA (distressed consumer brand) is appropriate. The pre-expansion average (15-20x) is the fair mid-cycle range.

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue x EBITDA Margin. Assumes 16x EV/EBITDA, net debt $400M, ~58M shares diluted.
