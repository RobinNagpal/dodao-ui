# Progressive Corporation (PGR) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Progressive is the #1-2 US auto insurer, neck-and-neck with State Farm at 18.1% market share. Revenue comes from three product lines: personal auto via direct channels (online/phone, approximately 50% of net premiums written), personal auto via independent agents (approximately 35% of NPW), and commercial auto plus specialty lines (approximately 15% of NPW). FY2025 produced a combined ratio of 87.4%, ROE of 34-40%, and EPS of $19.29.

The stock is down 31% from its ATH of $289.88 to around $199 because PGR is transitioning from an earnings upgrade cycle to earnings normalization. FY2025's combined ratio of 87.4% was extraordinary but unsustainable, driven by low accident frequency and favorable reserve development. As the hard market matures, competitors are catching up on rate adequacy, and PGR's relative pricing advantage is narrowing. Consensus expects FY2026 EPS of approximately $15.40 versus $19.29 in FY2025 (a 20% decline). A $950M Florida regulatory charge in Q3 2025 added political risk to the narrative. This is cyclical earnings normalization, not structural business deterioration.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

## Competitive Position & Moat

Progressive's competitive advantages are structural rather than cyclical. Its Snapshot telematics platform provides a data asset that competitors cannot replicate quickly — GEICO is recovering from years of underinvestment but remains years behind on usage-based pricing data. The dual distribution model (direct + agency) gives PGR access to both price-sensitive shoppers and advice-seeking customers, a combination no pure-direct or pure-agency competitor matches. PGR's combined ratio of 87.4% vs. the industry average of 99%+ demonstrates a structural underwriting advantage.

The top 2 direct competitors are GEICO (Berkshire Hathaway, approximately 14% market share) and Allstate (approximately 10% market share). GEICO is recovering from years of underinvestment and technology gaps but remains years behind on telematics. Allstate is pivoting from agent-heavy to direct distribution but has lower underwriting proficiency. Competitive displacement risk is minimal due to PGR's telematics moat and dual distribution advantage. The key risk is not market share loss — it is margin compression as the industry cycle turns from hard to soft market.

PGR's moat (4/5 — data/telematics advantage + dual distribution) is strong: #1-2 market share with best-in-class underwriting. The risk is purely cyclical, not competitive.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 14x PE. PGR's 5-year median PE is approximately 13-15x. Insurance peers (GEICO is not public; Allstate 10-12x, Travelers 12-14x) bracket this range. Current price of $199 implies approximately $14 EPS at 14x — meaningful normalization is already priced.

**Valuation regime check:** PGR's PE averaged ~12-14x in 2016-2020 and ~13-16x in 2021-2025, with brief spikes to 18x+ during peak hard-market earnings. The expansion was partially justified by superior telematics data and market share gains. For the severe case, 10x PE (below the pre-expansion average, reflecting loss of compounder premium) is appropriate.
