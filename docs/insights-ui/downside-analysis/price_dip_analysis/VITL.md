# Vital Farms (VITL) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Vital Farms is the #1 pasture-raised egg brand in the United States and the #2 egg brand overall, partnering with 600+ family farms built over 15+ years. The top revenue products are: pasture-raised shell eggs (approximately 95% of revenue, $720M+ in FY2025, the dominant US pasture-raised brand), butter and other dairy (approximately 5% of revenue, growing), and the Vital Crossroads processing facility (not yet revenue-generating, but $140-150M capex investment for future capacity toward $2B 2030 revenue target). FY2025 delivered revenue of $759.4M (+25.3% YoY), adjusted EBITDA of $114M (15% margin), zero debt, $113M in cash, and ROIC of 30%.

The stock has collapsed approximately 75% from its 52-week high of $53.13 to around $13.40 (and 56% YTD in 2026). Four forces converged: egg price deflation as conventional supply recovered from avian flu, widening the premium gap back to $4+/dozen. Q4 EPS missed ($0.35 vs $0.39 expected) and FY2026 guidance came in below consensus ($910M midpoint vs $938M). Founder Matt O'Hayer retired as executive chairperson in February 2026. Scanner data weakness in January-February 2026 suggested consumers are trading down from premium eggs. Meanwhile, VITL is mid-build on the $140-150M Vital Crossroads processing facility, making FCF deeply negative despite a profitable P&L.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $12.30, implying roughly 8% further downside from $13.40. At $13.40, the stock trades at approximately 9.3x PE, 0.81x PS, and 4.4x EV/EBITDA — distressed multiples for a company growing 20%+ with zero debt.

- Bull midpoint $19.00 x 20% = $3.80
- Base midpoint $11.50 x 30% = $3.45
- Bear midpoint $8.58 x 35% = $3.00

Note: Scenario 4 (Avian Flu Resurges) is the "wildcard" upside scenario. It carries 15% probability and would push the stock to $20-23. The weighted expected price of approximately $13.48 reflects the unusual dynamic of a stock where the most likely negative outcome (consumer trade-down) has limited incremental downside because the multiple is already at distressed levels, while a supply shock event could drive significant upside.

The key insight from stress testing: VITL is a pure re-rating bet. Moving revenue or margins within the realistic range only swings the price +/-$2-3 at a fixed multiple. But moving from the current 6x EV/EBITDA to a fair 10x on the same fundamentals swings the price +75%. The multiple is doing almost all the damage. The true floor is approximately $7.90 (revenue miss + margin collapse + 4x distressed multiple) but reaching that requires genuine financial stress — difficult for a zero-debt company.

## Competitive Position & Moat

VITL's moat rests on its 600+ family farm partnership network, built over 15+ years, with third-party certifications (Certified Humane, USDA Organic where applicable). This supply chain is extremely difficult to replicate. Current household penetration is only approximately 2%, implying massive remaining runway. The company's distributed farm network (600+ small farms, lower density) is structurally more resilient to avian flu than concentrated conventional operations — none have had positive HPAI cases.

The top 2 direct competitors are Cal-Maine Foods (largest US egg producer, beginning to launch pasture-raised product lines) and Happy Egg/Pete and Gerry's (smaller premium egg brands in adjacent segments). Revenue at risk from Cal-Maine entering pasture-raised is modest near-term because the farm partnerships and certifications create a structural barrier that takes years to replicate. The bigger risk is consumer trade-down to conventional eggs during economic stress rather than competitive share loss within premium.

VITL's moat (4/5 — supply chain relationships + certification barriers + brand) is strong. The primary threat is not competition but the widening price gap between conventional and pasture-raised eggs during supply recovery periods.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 6x EV/EBITDA (current market-implied multiple). VITL's current 4.4x EV/EBITDA is well below food company peers (Hershey 15-18x, Lamb Weston 10-12x, general food companies 10-14x). The market is pricing in both fundamental deterioration and capex risk simultaneously. A "fair" multiple for a growing food company with 30% ROIC would be 10-12x; 6x represents the market's current skepticism.

**Valuation regime check:** VITL went public in 2020 and its EV/EBITDA has ranged widely — from 20x+ during the avian flu tailwind period (2023-2024) to the current 4.4x. The compression reflects the loss of the avian flu-driven growth premium. For the severe (bear) case, 4x EV/EBITDA (distressed food company level) is the floor. The pre-pandemic-era multiple is not relevant because VITL's business scale was fundamentally different.

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue x EBITDA Margin. Assumes net cash $30M by end of 2026, ~45.6M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = ((Revenue x Margin x Multiple) + $0.03B) / 45.6M shares
