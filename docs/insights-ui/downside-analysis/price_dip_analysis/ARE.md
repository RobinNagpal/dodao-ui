# Alexandria Real Estate Equities (ARE) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Alexandria Real Estate Equities is the #1 life science lab REIT, owning 35.9M rentable square feet of operating lab properties in AAA innovation clusters (Boston, San Francisco Bay Area, San Diego, Seattle, Maryland). Lab/office space in these premier clusters accounts for roughly 70% of revenue, the development pipeline roughly 20%, and venture investments roughly 10%. The company operates "Megacampus" ecosystems for pharma/biotech tenants with $200-500/sqft switching costs, and 53% of revenue comes from investment-grade or large-cap tenants including Bristol-Myers Squibb, Moderna, and Eli Lilly.

ARE posted FY2025 adjusted FFO/share of $9.01 and revenue of $3.03B (down 2.9% YoY). The company guided FY2026 FFO of $6.25-$6.55/share, a 29% decline. Occupancy has fallen to 90.9% (versus a 10-year average of 95%) and is guided to dip into the high-80s% in 2026. Same-property NOI is guided to decline 7.5-9.5%.

The stock has fallen approximately 75% from its all-time high of $185.92 to around $46.88, and approximately 50% from its 52-week high of $93.54. The crash resulted from a historic life science lab oversupply crisis (lab space grew 7.5x since 2021 while demand dropped 60%), a 45% dividend cut, an S&P BBB+ negative outlook, a securities fraud lawsuit, removal from the FTSE All-World Index in March 2026, and Boston vacancy hitting a record 29.7%. Total debt stands at $13.96B.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $42, implying roughly 10% further downside from $46.88. ARE is already matching its own GFC trough percentage decline of -70 to -75%. For the stock to fall significantly further, the lab oversupply would need to be classified as "worse than the GFC" for this specific REIT — possible given the 7.5x supply increase, but not the base case.

The bull case requires the lab oversupply to clear faster than expected, with JLL's forecast of 18.7M SF being converted to other uses front-loading in 2026-2027, occupancy stabilizing at 89-90%, and the S&P negative outlook being revised to stable. The severe bear case envisions a CRE credit crisis ($936B in 2026 loan maturities), an S&P downgrade to BBB or worse triggering forced institutional selling, a second dividend cut, and potential equity dilution to pay down debt — producing a price of $23-30. The GFC low P/FFO of 3-4x would imply $19-26 on 2026 FFO as the absolute floor.

## Competitive Position & Moat

ARE holds the dominant #1 position in life science lab REITs, operating mega-campus ecosystems in AAA innovation clusters with $200-500/sqft tenant switching costs. The mission-critical nature of lab space (specialized ventilation, biosafety, floor load requirements) creates barriers that generic office REITs cannot replicate. Tenant quality is high — 53% investment-grade or large-cap — and the 45% dividend cut actually stabilized the payout at a comfortable 33% ratio.

However, the biotech funding winter has created a historic oversupply that overwhelms ARE's moat advantages. Lab space grew 7.5x since 2021 while demand dropped 60%. Boston vacancy stands at a record 29.7%. The oversupply is structural, not cyclical — it will take 2-3 years to normalize even with the 18.7M SF conversion forecast. ARE's operating leverage works in reverse on empty space: fixed costs on vacant labs directly compress NOI. The $13.96B debt load with S&P BBB+ negative outlook creates credit risk, and the securities fraud lawsuit adds headline risk that deters new institutional buyers. The stock is now lumped with office REITs in investor psychology ("lab is the new office").

ARE's current -75% from ATH matches its own GFC trough almost exactly. The only comparable precedent at this severity is Medical Properties Trust (also -75%), which involved actual tenant bankruptcy. The 45% dividend cut, while painful, removed the risk of a "surprise" cut and provides a 6.1% yield floor that attracts income investors.

## What to Watch

The key variable is the P/FFO multiple, which is driven by S&P rating actions, interest rate trajectory, and lab-sector sentiment more than quarterly occupancy data. ARE could be $22 or $84 on the same fundamental trajectory — the difference is what multiple the market assigns. Monitor the $2.9B asset sales program closely: cap rates achieved will signal whether the market values lab space or treats it like distressed office. Boston lab vacancy peaking and turning would be the clearest inflection signal.
