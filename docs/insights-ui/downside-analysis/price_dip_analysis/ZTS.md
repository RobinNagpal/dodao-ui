# Zoetis Inc. (ZTS) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Zoetis is the world's largest animal health company, spun off from Pfizer in 2013. It develops and sells medicines, vaccines, and diagnostics for companion animals (~65% of revenue) and livestock (~35%). The top revenue-generating products are: Apoquel/Cytopoint dermatology franchise (approximately $2B, the dominant treatment for canine allergic/atopic dermatitis since 2013), Simparica Trio parasiticide (growing steadily, less at risk from current headwinds), and Librela (osteoarthritis pain mAb for dogs/cats, $500M+, projected peak sales $1B+). FY2025 revenue was $9.5B (+6% organic), adjusted EPS $6.41, with 2026 guidance of $9.83-10.03B revenue and $7.00-7.10 adjusted EPS.

The stock has fallen approximately 54% from its ATH of $249 (November 2021) to around $115, and 33% from its 52-week high of $172.23. Four reinforcing headwinds hit simultaneously: Librela (the flagship growth driver) saw US sales decline 16% after FDA-documented neurological adverse events led to label changes and "Dear Veterinarian" warnings. Broader companion animal market weakness emerged as vet visits declined, particularly among Gen Z/Millennial pet owners under inflationary pressure. Merck's Numelvi gained FDA approval in February 2026, creating real competition for the $2B dermatology franchise for the first time since Apoquel launched in 2013. Management cut Q3 2025 revenue guidance, confirming the slowdown.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $99, implying roughly 14% further downside from $115. The 60% combined probability of bear and severe scenarios reflects Librela's FDA-documented safety issues (which rarely fully resolve), real competitive pressure from Numelvi, and structurally declining vet visits.

The bull case (only 10%) requires Librela safety concerns to fade with stable post-marketing surveillance, vet visits to recover, and Numelvi to have a slow launch — unlikely because recovering from a "Dear Veterinarian" warning takes years, not quarters. The severe bear case envisions a US recession accelerating vet visit decline, companion animal revenue declining 3-5% (the first decline in ZTS's public history), and Librela facing class-action litigation from pet owners.

The fundamental question is whether ZTS is still a 25x+ premium compounder or has become a 15-18x pharma company with pipeline risk. The stress test shows PE multiple matters far more than EPS — the spread between 15x and 25x on guidance EPS is $106 vs $176.

## Competitive Position & Moat

Zoetis holds the #1 global position in companion animal health with 25-31% market share, operating in a duopoly with Elanco. The moat rests on $600M/year R&D spend, FDA approval timelines of 5-7 years per species (creating enormous barriers to entry), and relationships with 100,000+ veterinarians. The company generates 72% gross margins, $2.3B FCF, 45% ROE, and 25.5% ROIC — franchise-quality economics.

The top 2 direct competitors are Merck Animal Health (division of Merck, launched Numelvi in February 2026) and Elanco (ELAN, the #2 standalone animal health company). Merck's Numelvi is the first real challenger to the dermatology franchise since 2013, with revenue at risk estimated at $200-300M (10-15% of the $2B Apoquel/Cytopoint franchise, or 2-3% of total ZTS revenue). Elanco competes broadly but lacks ZTS's margin profile and R&D scale. Librela's safety issues compound competitive pressure by removing the growth driver that was supposed to offset Numelvi's market entry.

ZTS's moat (4/5 — regulatory barriers + R&D scale + vet relationships) is strong but under pressure from simultaneous safety and competitive headwinds.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 19x PE. ZTS's 5-year median PE is approximately 30-35x, but the current 19x reflects a structural de-rating from premium compounder to a company with pipeline/safety risk. Elanco, the closest comparable, trades at 13x PE with similar growth. Current price of $115 implies approximately $7.05 EPS at 16x, or $6.50 at 18x — modest skepticism baked in.

**Valuation regime check:** ZTS's PE averaged ~30-38x in 2017-2022 (premium compounder status, post-Pfizer spinoff growth phase) and ~18-25x in 2023-2026 (de-rating on Librela safety, vet visit decline, Numelvi competition). The compression is partially justified by genuine headwinds (safety issues, competitive pressure). For the severe case, 12x PE (the animal health sector floor for 3-5% growers) is appropriate. The pre-expansion 30x+ average is not relevant because ZTS's growth profile has structurally changed.

**Key variables:** FY2026 Adjusted EPS x PE Multiple. ~430M shares diluted.
