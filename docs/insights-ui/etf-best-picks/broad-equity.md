# Best ETF Picks — Broad Equity

Two picks. Together they own the entire global stock market with almost no overlap.

- **Pick 1 — VTI** for the US market.
- **Pick 2 — VXUS** for everything outside the US.

A typical pairing is roughly 60% VTI / 40% VXUS, or 50/50 if you want true global
market-cap weighting. Combined weighted fee is about 0.04% per year.

---

## Pick 1: VTI — Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF

**What it owns:** Around 4,000 US stocks — large, mid, and small cap. Essentially
every US company that matters.
**Benchmark index:** CRSP US Total Market Index.

### Why this index is the right one

- The **S&P 500** only covers 500 large companies. The **Russell 1000** covers 1,000
  large/mid. The **CRSP US Total Market** covers them all *plus* small caps —
  about 8x more names.
- Small and mid caps historically grow faster than large caps over long horizons.
  Owning the total market means you already hold tomorrow's winners while they're
  still small, instead of buying them after they get promoted into the S&P 500 (and
  paying the price bump that comes with promotion).
- The US has been the best-performing major equity market for 15+ years. Deep capital
  markets, the world's strongest tech sector, AI-driven productivity gains, and
  shareholder-friendly corporate culture make a sudden reversal unlikely.

### How VTI "beats" its index

The CRSP US Total Market price index doesn't include dividends. VTI holds the real
stocks, collects the dividends, and pays them out to you — roughly **1.3% yield per
year**, distributed quarterly. So VTI's total return = price-index return + dividends
− fees. That's how an honest passive ETF "beats" the headline index.

### Why VTI over competitors that own the same thing

| ETF      | Issuer    | Fee   | AUM       |
| -------- | --------- | ----- | --------- |
| **VTI**  | Vanguard  | 0.03% | ~$1.8T    |
| ITOT     | iShares   | 0.03% | ~$70B     |
| SCHB     | Schwab    | 0.03% | ~$35B     |

All three track US total-market indexes at the same headline fee. VTI wins on:

- **Liquidity** — by far the largest AUM, which means tighter bid-ask spreads (you
  lose less on every trade).
- **Tax efficiency** — Vanguard's unique ETF-share-class-of-the-mutual-fund structure
  has historically meant near-zero year-end capital-gains distributions, while
  competitors occasionally pay them. In a taxable brokerage account this is real money.
- **Track record** — VTI has been live since 2001 with consistently tight tracking
  error vs its index.

## Pick 2: VXUS — Vanguard Total International Stock ETF

**What it owns:** Around 8,500 stocks across developed and emerging markets outside
the US — Europe, Japan, UK, Canada, China, India, Brazil, plus small caps in all of
those.
**Benchmark index:** FTSE Global All Cap ex US Index.

### Why this index is the right one

- The most common alternatives — **MSCI EAFE** and **MSCI ACWI ex-US** — leave out
  big pieces. EAFE has only ~800 *developed-market large caps*; it misses China,
  India, Brazil, and every small cap. ACWI ex-US adds emerging markets but still
  skips small caps.
- FTSE Global All Cap ex US is the most complete non-US benchmark — developed +
  emerging + small. The international growth story plays out as much in
  emerging-market consumers and small caps as in European megacaps, and you don't
  want to miss it.
- Valuation gap: international stocks trade at a multi-decade discount to US stocks.
  That gap doesn't have to close, but if it does even partially, international wins
  for a long stretch.

### How VXUS "beats" its index

International companies pay much higher dividends than US ones — VXUS distributes
about **3% per year** in dividends, more than 2x VTI's yield. European and Japanese
companies are required by both culture and regulation to return more cash to
shareholders. The ETF passes that through to you on top of any price appreciation.

### Why VXUS over competitors that own non-US stocks

| ETF       | Issuer   | Fee   | What it covers                                                    |
| --------- | -------- | ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **VXUS**  | Vanguard | 0.05% | Developed + emerging + small caps (most complete non-US exposure) |
| IXUS      | iShares  | 0.07% | Same coverage as VXUS, slightly higher fee                        |
| EFA       | iShares  | 0.32% | Developed-market large caps only — no emerging, no small caps     |
| ACWX      | iShares  | 0.32% | Developed + emerging large caps — still no small caps             |

VXUS owns more of the world for less money. EFA and ACWX are popular legacy products
charging 6x the fee while owning materially less of the market. There's no real
reason to pick them today.

---

## Why pair VTI + VXUS together

- **No overlap.** VTI is US-only; VXUS is everything-but-US. Together they hold
  ~12,500 stocks across every developed and emerging market on the planet.
- **Lowest combined fee.** Weighted average of ~0.04% per year. A $100,000 portfolio
  pays about $40 a year in fees for global equity exposure.
- **Built-in rebalancing.** When the US outperforms (like the last decade), VTI's
  share grows and you can trim into VXUS at relatively cheaper valuations — and vice
  versa.

If you only want **one** ETF instead of two, the single-ticker equivalent is **VT**
(Vanguard Total World Stock ETF, fee 0.06%). VT holds VTI + VXUS in market-cap
weights inside a single wrapper — slightly higher fee, but you never have to
rebalance.
