## FSV-specific checklist you should monitor

### 1) **FirstService Brands organic revenue**

**Watch for:** organic growth below **+2%**, and especially **negative** for 2 quarters.
**Why this matters for FSV:** Brands is the main earnings driver, and this is where the weakness showed up first.
**Expected pricing impact:** **-8% to -15%**.

### 2) **Restoration claims + roofing activity + housing/news backdrop**

**Watch for:** mild weather, fewer large-loss claims, weak roofing demand, weak housing market, weak consumer confidence, or management mentioning these together.
**Why this matters for FSV:** these were exactly the reasons management gave for Brands weakness.
**Expected pricing impact:** **-6% to -12%**.

### 3) **Brands margin trend**

**Watch for:** Brands EBITDA or operating margin down **50–100 bps+** year over year.
**Why this matters for FSV:** in Q3 and Q4, weaker revenue caused negative operating leverage and margin pressure.
**Expected pricing impact:** **-5% to -10%**.

### 4) **Organic growth vs acquisition growth**

**Watch for:** total growth looking fine, but organic growth staying flat or negative while acquisitions do the heavy lifting.
**Why this matters for FSV:** in 2025, Brands revenue was up **4%** for the year, but **down 3% organically**. The market pays a lower multiple when growth is more acquisition-led than demand-led.
**Expected pricing impact:** **-6% to -12%**.

### 5) **Residential as the stabilizer**

**Watch for:** Residential organic growth dropping below about **3%-4%**, weaker contract wins, or margin slippage.
**Why this matters for FSV:** Residential held up well in 2025. If this segment also softens, then the “Brands is weak but the rest is fine” support disappears.
**Expected pricing impact:** **-4% to -8%** by itself, more if it happens with Brands weakness.

### 6) **Valuation discipline**

**Watch for:** buying FSV at a premium multiple when growth is slowing.
**Why this matters for FSV:** this stock has historically been treated like a quality compounder, so even decent results can lead to a big drop when growth slips.
**Expected pricing impact:** **-10% to -20%** if growth disappoints while valuation is still rich.
