## Generic Downside Analysis Framework

Checklist items extracted from analyzing SE, ACM, OBDC, CRH, AGM, PGR, and ZTS. Each item appeared as a real driver of 15–35% declines in stocks where the underlying business was still fundamentally okay.

---

### 1) Revenue growth vs operating expense growth

**Metric:** (OpEx growth rate) minus (Revenue growth rate), measured quarterly YoY.
**Red flag:** OpEx growth exceeds revenue growth for 2 consecutive quarters.
**One-line:** If costs grow as fast as revenue, the market stops paying a growth premium.
**Seen in:** SE (cost of revenue +40% vs revenue +38%), PGR (expense ratio +1.8pts from ad spend).
**Expected pricing impact:** 5–10% for 1 quarter, 10–20% if sustained.

---

### 2) EPS vs consensus estimate

**Metric:** Reported EPS minus consensus EPS, as a percentage.
**Red flag:** Miss by any amount at a premium valuation; miss 2 quarters in a row at any valuation.
**One-line:** At peak multiples, even a 1% miss signals the growth story may be peaking.
**Seen in:** CRH (missed by 1.3%, stock dropped 3.7% premarket), SE (revenue beat but profit missed repeatedly).
**Expected pricing impact:** 3–8% for a small miss, 10–20% for consecutive misses.

---

### 3) Forward guidance growth deceleration

**Metric:** Next-year guidance growth rate vs current-year actual growth rate.
**Red flag:** Guidance growth rate is meaningfully below the current year's actual growth, especially at elevated valuation.
**One-line:** Stocks are priced on future expectations — when the next year looks weaker, the multiple compresses now.
**Seen in:** ACM (FCF guided -42% YoY), CRH (EBITDA guidance +8% vs +11% actual), SE (GMV guidance 25% vs 26.8% actual), ZTS (revenue guide cut in Q3).
**Expected pricing impact:** 5–10% for mild deceleration, 15–30% for sharp step-downs.

---

### 4) Free cash flow trend (quarterly and annual)

**Metric:** Quarterly FCF YoY change; annual FCF guidance vs prior-year actual.
**Red flag:** Quarterly FCF drops >25% YoY, or next-year FCF guided >20% below current year.
**One-line:** Cash flow is the proof that earnings are real — when it breaks, the valuation story breaks with it.
**Seen in:** ACM (Q4 FCF -51% YoY, FY26 guide -42% vs FY25).
**Expected pricing impact:** 8–15% for one bad quarter, 15–30% if guidance confirms it.

---

### 5) Net debt / EBITDA (leverage)

**Metric:** Net debt divided by trailing-twelve-month adjusted EBITDA.
**Red flag:** Rises above 2.0x, or increases >0.5x in a single year, especially during peak earnings.
**One-line:** Rising leverage during a peak cycle means less flexibility when the cycle turns.
**Seen in:** CRH (1.5x → 1.8x from $4.1B in M&A), ACM (leverage fine at 1.0x — this is why the business held up).
**Expected pricing impact:** 3–8% for watch-level increase, 10–20% if combined with earnings weakness.

---

### 6) Forward P/E vs 5-year historical range

**Metric:** Current forward P/E vs median and upper-bound of the stock's own 5-year range.
**Red flag:** Trading >1.5x the 5-year median P/E or at all-time-high multiples.
**One-line:** Elevated valuations leave no margin for error — any stumble gets punished disproportionately.
**Seen in:** CRH (22x+ vs ~15x historical), ZTS (premium multiple with slowing growth).
**Expected pricing impact:** 10–20% reversion to mean, 20–35% if multiple compresses during an earnings miss.

---

### 7) Profit quality (revenue beat + earnings miss)

**Metric:** Count of quarters where revenue beat consensus but EPS missed.
**Red flag:** This pattern occurs 2+ times in a row.
**One-line:** Revenue beats that don't translate to profit beats signal deteriorating unit economics or rising reinvestment costs.
**Seen in:** SE (multiple quarters of revenue beat / profit miss), ZTS (EPS held up by cost control while revenue guide was cut).
**Expected pricing impact:** 5–10% first occurrence, 15–25% if it becomes a pattern.

---

### 8) Dividend / distribution coverage

**Metric:** Earnings (or NII for BDCs/financials) divided by the dividend per share.
**Red flag:** Coverage drops below 100% for 1 quarter; supplemental/special dividends eliminated.
**One-line:** For income stocks, undercovered dividends signal a cut is coming — yield investors sell before the announcement.
**Seen in:** OBDC (NII $0.36 vs $0.37 base dividend, supplemental went to $0.00), AGM (provision spike threatened earnings stability).
**Expected pricing impact:** 5–10% when coverage weakens, 10–20% on actual cut.

---

### 9) Credit quality trend (for financials / lenders)

**Metric:** Non-accrual rate at fair value; provision for credit losses as % of revenue; 90-day delinquency rate.
**Red flag:** Non-accruals double YoY, or provisions grow faster than the loan book for 2 quarters.
**One-line:** Rising credit costs directly reduce earnings and signal hidden portfolio risk — the market prices in worst-case scenarios fast.
**Seen in:** OBDC (non-accruals tripled from 0.4% to 1.3%), AGM (provision hit $16M / 16% of revenue), SE Monee (provisions +66% in Q4).
**Expected pricing impact:** 5–12% for trend deterioration, 15–30% for a sharp spike.

---

### 10) Key segment concentration risk

**Metric:** Revenue or profit contribution of the top 1–2 products/segments as % of total.
**Red flag:** The dominant segment (>30% of revenue) shows slowing growth or margin pressure.
**One-line:** When the engine that drives the stock stumbles, the whole thesis gets re-evaluated.
**Seen in:** ZTS (parasiticides + dermatology = 42% of mix, both slowed), SE (Shopee efficiency questioned), PGR (Personal Lines combined ratio — any weakening matters because it is the core).
**Expected pricing impact:** 5–10% for mild slowdown, 10–20% for clear deceleration in the key segment.

---

### 11) Acquisition spend discipline

**Metric:** Annual M&A spend as % of EBITDA; organic growth rate ex-acquisitions.
**Red flag:** M&A spend >50% of EBITDA in a single year, or organic growth below 2% while acquisitions accelerate.
**One-line:** Heavy M&A at peak valuations raises integration risk and masks whether the core business is still growing.
**Seen in:** CRH (53% of EBITDA on 38 deals, net debt +$4.5B).
**Expected pricing impact:** 3–8% for concern, 10–20% if combined with leverage increase or integration issues.

---

### 12) Corporate action / governance risk

**Metric:** Qualitative — any merger with a related party, strategic review, or management action that raises alignment concerns.
**Red flag:** Related-party transaction at a discount; strategic review with vague timeline; lawsuits filed after a corporate action.
**One-line:** Governance missteps destroy trust faster than bad earnings — the discount can persist for quarters.
**Seen in:** OBDC (failed OBDC II merger at 0.80x NAV + 6 lawsuits = 17–22% drop), ACM (Construction Management strategic review added uncertainty).
**Expected pricing impact:** 8–15% on announcement, additional 5–10% if lawsuits or prolonged uncertainty follow.

---

### 13) Market reaction pattern (post-earnings)

**Metric:** Day-1 price change on earnings + price action over the next 5 trading days + analyst actions within 2 weeks.
**Red flag:** Stock drops >3% on day 1, continues falling over 5 days, and 1+ analyst downgrades follow.
**One-line:** When the stock keeps falling after earnings and analysts pile on, expectations were too high — the re-rating has begun.
**Seen in:** ACM (-3.6% day 1, -21.3% over 5 days, Baird/Barclays downgrades), CRH (-3.7% premarket on Q4 miss).
**Expected pricing impact:** 10–20% total if the pattern completes (drop + continuation + downgrades).

---

### 14) One-time items or timing benefits in reported numbers

**Metric:** Management disclosure of timing shifts, accounting changes, or non-recurring items that boosted the quarter.
**Red flag:** >2% of reported growth attributed to timing or one-time items.
**One-line:** Reported growth that includes one-time boosts will reverse next quarter — if you don't adjust, you'll be surprised by the "deceleration."
**Seen in:** ZTS (Q4 international growth had 2.5–3.5% timing benefit), PGR (favorable reserve development that may not repeat).
**Expected pricing impact:** 3–8% when the reversal shows up in the next quarter.
