### 1) Combined ratio

**What to track**

- Companywide combined ratio
- Personal Lines combined ratio
- Change vs last quarter / last year

**Why this matters for PGR**
This is the most important number for Progressive because it shows whether underwriting is still highly profitable. Progressive manages the business with a goal of a **calendar-year combined ratio of 96 or better**, but in 2025 it performed far better than that, with **87.5** in Personal Lines and **87.0** in Commercial Lines. That is why the stock can react badly even to a small weakening from very strong levels.

**How to read it**

- **Below 88** → very strong
- **88 to 90** → still good, but less special
- **90 to 93** → warning
- **Above 93** → bigger red flag

**Expected pricing impact**

- Improving or staying below 88: **positive / supportive**
- Moving into 90–93: **moderate negative**
- Sustained move above 93: **strong negative**

### 2) Policies in force growth + net premiums written growth

**What to track**

- Policies in force (PIF) growth
- Net premiums written (NPW) growth
- Whether growth is slowing

**Why this matters for PGR**
PGR is not just a margin story. It is also a **market-share gain** story. In 2025, Personal Lines NPW grew **14%** and PIF grew **11%**. But in early 2026, monthly NPW growth slowed to **4% in January** and **5% in February**, while total PIF growth was still **10%**. That kind of slowdown matters because the stock was used to seeing both strong growth and great underwriting at the same time.

**How to read it**

- **PIF and NPW both healthy** → growth engine still fine
- **PIF strong but NPW slows** → pricing/mix may be weakening
- **Both slow together** → bigger warning

**Expected pricing impact**

- Strong PIF + strong NPW: **positive**
- PIF okay but NPW slowing: **mild to moderate negative**
- Both slowing clearly: **stronger negative**

### 3) Expense ratio / acquisition spend

**What to track**

- Underwriting expense ratio
- Aggregate expense ratio
- Advertising spend
- Whether growth is becoming more expensive

**Why this matters for PGR**
Progressive invested hard to grow. In 2025, its **aggregate expense ratio increased 1.8 points**, partly because of **$1.1 billion more advertising expense** to drive growth. Management also said it **invested heavily in advertising and agent incentives**. So for PGR, strong growth is good only if it is not becoming too expensive to maintain.

**How to read it**

- Growth with stable expense ratio → healthy
- Growth but expense ratio rising → weaker quality
- Slower growth + higher spend → real red flag

**Expected pricing impact**

- Stable expense ratio: **neutral / positive**
- Expense ratio drifting up: **moderate negative**
- Higher spend with weaker growth: **strong negative**

## Other measurable metrics you should add

### 4) Loss and loss-adjustment expense ratio

**What to track**

- Loss & LAE ratio
- Whether claim costs are rising faster than premiums

**Why this matters for PGR**
This tells you if claims pressure is coming back. Progressive said 2025 benefited from **lower accident frequency**, and that helped profitability. If that tailwind fades, the stock can reset fast because investors know recent profits were helped by favorable loss trends.

**Expected pricing impact**

- Stable / improving: **positive**
- Gradual worsening: **moderate negative**
- Sharp worsening: **strong negative**

---

### 5) Reserve development

**What to track**

- Favorable or unfavorable prior-year reserve development
- Any reserve strengthening

**Why this matters for PGR**
In 2025, profitability benefited from **favorable prior-year development**. That is good, but it is also a tailwind that may not repeat every year. If reserve development turns weaker, investors may treat earnings quality as lower.

**Expected pricing impact**

- Favorable development continues: **positive**
- Tailwind fades: **mild negative**
- Unfavorable development / reserve strengthening: **strong negative**

---

### 6) Written premium per policy / rate adequacy

**What to track**

- Written premium per policy
- Rate increases or decreases
- Whether pricing still covers risk

**Why this matters for PGR**
Progressive said **rates remained relatively stable during 2025** and it even **lowered rates in some states**. The annual report also shows that changes in premiums are driven by **new business applications, business mix, premium per policy, and retention**. For this stock, slower premium per policy growth can be an early sign that the pricing cycle is getting softer.

**Expected pricing impact**

- Healthy premium per policy: **supportive**
- Flat / weaker premium per policy: **moderate negative**
- Falling premium per policy with rising loss costs: **strong negative**
