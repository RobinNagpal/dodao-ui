## Checklist for PCTY

**1) Recurring revenue growth**
**Why it matters:** this is the cleanest measure of Paylocity’s core software engine.
**Safe:** **above 12%**
**Watch:** **10%–12%**
**Red flag:** **below 10%**
**Expected pricing impact:** **High**
For PCTY, this is the first thing to watch because the stock got hit when recurring growth moved from **15% in FY2025** toward **10%–11% in FY2026 guidance**.

**2) Full-year and next-quarter guidance**
**Why it matters:** this stock trades on forward growth, not just last quarter’s beat.
**Safe:** guidance stable or improving
**Watch:** growth guide down by **1–2 points**
**Red flag:** growth guide down by **3+ points**, or total revenue guide falls below **10%**
**Expected pricing impact:** **Very high**
This was the main warning sign here: FY2026 initial guidance came in far below FY2025 growth, and that is the kind of reset that can knock **15%–30%** off a premium software stock.

**3) Underlying margin excluding client-funds interest**
**Why it matters:** payroll companies can look stronger when interest on client funds is helping profits, so you need to check the underlying software margin too.
**Safe:** stable or rising, and around **30%+** of recurring revenue on a full-year basis
**Watch:** **28%–30%**
**Red flag:** below **28%** or clearly falling
**Expected pricing impact:** **Medium**
Paylocity itself highlights **Adjusted EBITDA excluding interest income on funds held for clients**. In FY2025 that figure was **31.2% of recurring revenue**, which is healthy.

**4) Client growth + revenue per client**
**Why it matters:** Paylocity wins by adding clients and selling more modules into each client.
**Safe:** client growth **above 6%** and average revenue per client still expanding
**Watch:** client growth **4%–6%**
**Red flag:** client growth below **4%** or weak cross-sell
**Expected pricing impact:** **Medium to high**
Management said FY2025 growth was helped by a **7% increase in client base** plus higher revenue per client, so this is directly relevant to PCTY’s model.

**5) Client employee counts / payroll activity / hiring tone**
**Why it matters:** Paylocity’s results depend partly on how many employees its clients have and how active payroll volumes are.
**Safe:** stable hiring and payroll activity
**Watch:** mixed hiring commentary
**Red flag:** broad hiring slowdown, layoffs, or tighter client budgets
**Expected pricing impact:** **Medium to high**
Paylocity’s 10-K explicitly says quarterly results can fluctuate with **the number of clients’ employees**, **client renewal rates**, and **changes in client budgets and procurement policies**.

**6) Industry-news check: payroll/HCM peer resets**
**Why it matters:** PCTY gets revalued with the group, not in isolation.
**Safe:** peers are holding guidance / sector sentiment stable
**Watch:** mixed peer outlooks
**Red flag:** peer guidance cuts, broad payroll/HCM selloff
**Expected pricing impact:** **Medium**
This matters because the sector was already weak, and peer pressure likely amplified Paylocity’s fall.
