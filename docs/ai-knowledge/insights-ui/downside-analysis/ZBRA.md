## Zebra Technologies (ZBRA) — Financial Ratios Analysis

ZBRA posted FY 2025 revenue of $5.4B (+8.3% YoY), net income of $419M, GAAP EPS of $8.18, and free cash flow of $831M. The company guided FY 2026 revenue growth of 9–13% (~$5.9–6.1B) and adjusted EPS of $17.70–$18.30. Memory cost headwinds may pressure gross margins in 2026, but the sales pipeline and acquisitions provide tailwinds.

---

## Valuation Ratios

### PE Ratio — 24.36
**Verdict: Neutral.** A PE of ~24x is reasonable for a mid-growth industrial-tech company. It is below the S&P 500 average (~26x) and not stretched, but not cheap either. ZBRA's GAAP EPS of $8.18 reflects one-time charges; adjusted EPS was significantly higher.

**2026 Prediction: ~18–19x.** With consensus EPS of ~$18.14 and assuming the stock stays near $330, the forward PE compresses to ~18x. This is attractive if the company delivers on guidance.

### Forward PE — 11.05
**Verdict: Good.** A forward PE of ~11x signals the market expects significant earnings growth, which aligns with the jump from GAAP $8.18 to guided adjusted $17.70–$18.30. This is cheap relative to peers.

**2026 Prediction: ~14–16x.** As 2026 earnings materialize, the forward PE (looking into 2027) will likely normalize upward unless the company signals continued strong growth.

### PEG Ratio — 0.77
**Verdict: Good.** A PEG below 1.0 suggests the stock is undervalued relative to its growth rate. ZBRA is growing earnings faster than its PE multiple implies.

**2026 Prediction: ~0.8–1.0.** As earnings growth moderates from the recovery phase, PEG will drift toward fair value (~1.0).

### PS Ratio — 1.91
**Verdict: Neutral.** A P/S of ~1.9x is modest for a company with 48% gross margins and recurring software/services revenue. Not expensive but not deep value.

**2026 Prediction: ~1.7–1.8x.** Revenue growth of 9–13% should outpace stock appreciation slightly, compressing P/S.

### PB Ratio — 2.75
**Verdict: Neutral.** A P/B of ~2.75x reflects goodwill from acquisitions (Matrox, etc.). For an asset-light tech company, this is reasonable.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.5–2.7x.** Book value will grow with retained earnings, slightly compressing P/B.

### P/TBV Ratio — 2.99
**Verdict: Neutral.** Slightly higher than P/B due to intangibles. Not a concern for a tech company.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.7–2.9x.**

### P/FCF Ratio — 12.40
**Verdict: Good.** A P/FCF of ~12x is attractive. ZBRA generates strong free cash flow ($831M in FY 2025), and 2026 guidance is $900M+. Anything below 15x for a quality compounder is appealing.

**2026 Prediction: ~10–11x.** With FCF guided to $900M+, this ratio improves further assuming stable stock price.

### P/OCF Ratio — 11.23
**Verdict: Good.** Similar story to P/FCF. Strong cash generation relative to market cap.

**2026 Prediction: ~10–11x.**

## Enterprise Value Ratios

### EV/Sales — 2.39
**Verdict: Neutral.** Reasonable for ZBRA's margin profile. Not expensive vs. peers in enterprise hardware/software.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.1–2.3x.** Revenue growth and potential debt paydown reduce EV/Sales.

### EV/EBITDA — 13.07
**Verdict: Neutral.** An EV/EBITDA of ~13x is fair for an industrial-tech company. The historical 5-year average is ~15–17x, so ZBRA is trading below its historical range.

**2026 Prediction: ~11–12x.** EBITDA should grow with revenue, compressing this ratio. Attractive if achieved.

### EV/EBIT — 16.09
**Verdict: Neutral.** Slightly elevated vs. EV/EBITDA due to depreciation/amortization from acquisitions. Reasonable for the sector.

**2026 Prediction: ~13–14x.**

### EV/FCF — 15.49
**Verdict: Neutral to Good.** Below 15x is generally attractive; at ~15.5x ZBRA is on the cusp. With FCF guided to $900M+, this improves in 2026.

**2026 Prediction: ~13–14x.**

## Profitability Ratios

### ROE — 11.68%
**Verdict: Below average.** ROE of ~12% is mediocre. Quality companies typically deliver 15%+. ZBRA's ROE is depressed by GAAP charges and goodwill-heavy equity base.

**2026 Prediction: ~16–18%.** With adjusted net income expected near $900M+ on ~$5B equity, ROE should improve materially as earnings normalize.

### ROA — 6.36%
**Verdict: Below average.** ROA of ~6% reflects the asset-heavy balance sheet (goodwill, intangibles from acquisitions). For a tech company, this is low.

**2026 Prediction: ~8–9%.** Higher earnings on a similar asset base will lift ROA.

### ROIC — 8.02%
**Verdict: Below average.** ROIC of ~8% barely exceeds typical cost of capital (~8–10%). ZBRA needs to push this above 10% to create meaningful shareholder value.

**2026 Prediction: ~10–12%.** Guided earnings growth should push ROIC above the cost of capital hurdle.

### ROCE — 10.84%
**Verdict: Neutral.** ROCE above 10% is acceptable but not impressive. Better than ROIC due to excluding excess cash and non-operating items.

**2026 Prediction: ~13–15%.**

## Leverage Ratios

### Debt/Equity — 0.70
**Verdict: Good.** D/E of 0.70 is conservative and manageable. ZBRA has been deleveraging from higher levels post-acquisitions.

**2026 Prediction: ~0.55–0.65.** With $900M+ FCF and buyback plans, net debt should decline further.

### Debt/EBITDA — 3.01
**Verdict: Neutral (Watch).** A leverage ratio above 3.0x is a yellow flag. Lenders typically prefer below 3.0x. However, with EBITDA growing, this is improving.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.3–2.5x.** EBITDA growth and potential debt paydown should bring this comfortably below 3.0x.

### Debt/FCF — 3.20
**Verdict: Neutral (Watch).** Similar to Debt/EBITDA — slightly elevated but improving. ZBRA could pay off all debt in ~3 years from FCF alone.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.5–2.8x.**

### Net Debt/Equity — 0.71
**Verdict: Good.** Very close to gross D/E, meaning ZBRA holds minimal cash relative to debt. Not a concern given strong FCF generation.

**2026 Prediction: ~0.50–0.60.**

### Net Debt/EBITDA — 2.86
**Verdict: Neutral.** Below 3.0x is the threshold for comfort. ZBRA is just under.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.0–2.3x.**

### Net Debt/FCF — 3.05
**Verdict: Neutral.** Slightly above 3.0x but improving with guided FCF of $900M+.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.3–2.6x.**

## Efficiency Ratios

### Asset Turnover — 0.66
**Verdict: Below average.** Asset turnover below 1.0 means ZBRA generates less than $1 of revenue per $1 of assets. This is typical for acquisition-heavy companies with large goodwill balances inflating the asset base.

**2026 Prediction: ~0.70–0.73.** Revenue growth (~10%) on a similar asset base should modestly improve turnover.

### Inventory Turnover — 3.94
**Verdict: Neutral.** Turning inventory ~4x per year (i.e., ~90 days of inventory) is acceptable for a hardware manufacturer. Not best-in-class but not a concern.

**2026 Prediction: ~4.0–4.3.** Improving demand and supply chain normalization should slightly improve turnover.

---

## Liquidity Ratios

### Quick Ratio — 0.52
**Verdict: Poor.** A quick ratio below 1.0 means ZBRA cannot cover current liabilities with liquid assets alone. At 0.52, this is concerning and suggests reliance on inventory conversion or credit facilities to meet short-term obligations.

**2026 Prediction: ~0.6–0.7.** FCF generation should improve cash balances, but this ratio may stay below 1.0 unless ZBRA holds more cash on the balance sheet. The company has $1.5B in available credit capacity, which mitigates the risk.

### Current Ratio — 0.97
**Verdict: Below average (Watch).** A current ratio below 1.0 is a warning sign for liquidity. However, ZBRA's strong FCF ($831M in 2025) and $1.5B credit facility provide a safety net. The ratio improved to 1.61 intra-year (Q3 2025), so the year-end figure may reflect seasonal working capital timing.

**2026 Prediction: ~1.1–1.3.** With better cash flow and potential debt refinancing, the current ratio should improve above 1.0.

## Yield Ratios

### Earnings Yield — 4.07%
**Verdict: Below average.** An earnings yield of ~4% (inverse of PE ~24x) is below the 10-year Treasury yield (~4.5%). On a GAAP basis, ZBRA does not offer a compelling earnings yield vs. risk-free alternatives.

**2026 Prediction: ~5.5–6.0%.** As GAAP earnings normalize toward ~$18/share, earnings yield improves significantly.

### FCF Yield — 8.07%
**Verdict: Good.** An FCF yield above 8% is attractive. This is the more relevant metric for ZBRA since FCF exceeds GAAP earnings substantially (no goodwill amortization in cash flow).

**2026 Prediction: ~8.5–9.5%.** Guided FCF of $900M+ on a ~$17B market cap implies improving FCF yield.

### Buyback Yield — 1.29%
**Verdict: Neutral.** ZBRA repurchases shares modestly. The company announced an expanded $1.5B buyback authorization. At 1.3% yield, it is supplementing returns but not aggressively shrinking the share count.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.0–2.5%.** With the expanded authorization and strong FCF, buyback yield should increase.

### Total Shareholder Return — 1.29%
**Verdict: Below average.** ZBRA pays no dividend, so total shareholder return equals buyback yield at 1.3%. For a company generating $800M+ FCF, returning only 1.3% to shareholders is conservative.

**2026 Prediction: ~2.0–2.5%.** Should improve with expanded buybacks. ZBRA reinvests heavily in growth, which is appropriate if ROIC improves above cost of capital.
