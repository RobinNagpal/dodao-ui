## Tecnoglass (TGLS) — Downside Analysis

TGLS hit a 52-week high of $90.34 in June 2025 and fell to $39.53 by March 2026 — roughly **56% down**. The business posted record FY 2025 revenue of $983.6M (+10.5%) and record backlog of $1.3B (+16%). **The stock collapsed because margins deteriorated sharply in Q3–Q4 while costs spiked from multiple directions simultaneously — Colombian peso, aluminum, tariffs, and mix shift — turning an earnings-beat story into a consecutive-miss story.**

---

## 1) Gross margin trend (quarterly YoY change)

**Metric:** Quarterly gross margin vs same quarter prior year.

**Threshold:**
- Green: stable or expanding YoY
- Watch: contracts 100–200 bps YoY
- Red: contracts >200 bps YoY for 2 consecutive quarters

**Why this applies to TGLS:** Tecnoglass manufactures in Colombia and sells into the U.S. Its gross margin is the single most sensitive metric because it absorbs every cost headwind — peso appreciation, aluminum prices, tariffs, and mix shift — all at once. Q2 2025 gross margin was 44.7% (+400 bps YoY). By Q3 it was 42.7% (-310 bps). By Q4 it was 40.0% (-450 bps). That 470 bps swing from Q2 to Q4 is what destroyed earnings.

**Effect:** Gross margin is where all of TGLS's risks show up first. When it contracts, EPS misses follow within the same quarter. Investors in building-products stocks watch margins more than revenue because revenue can grow while profits shrink if costs are out of control.

**Expected pricing impact:**
- Gross margin contracts 100–200 bps for 1 quarter: **5–10% downside**
- Contracts >200 bps for 2 consecutive quarters: **15–25% downside**
- Contracts >400 bps with no clear reversal path: **25–40% downside**

---

## 2) Adjusted EBITDA margin trend

**Metric:** Quarterly adjusted EBITDA margin vs prior year.

**Threshold:**
- Green: above 30%
- Watch: 27–30%
- Red: below 27%

**Why this applies to TGLS:** EBITDA margin captures both gross margin pressure and SG&A efficiency. TGLS went from 31.6% (Q1) → 31.2% (Q2) → declining in Q3 → 25.4% (Q4, vs 33.1% prior year). That Q4 number was a 770 bps YoY collapse. The 2026 EBITDA guidance midpoint of $285M on ~$1.1B revenue implies ~26% margin — below 2025's 29.6%. When the forward margin outlook is worse than the current year, the market re-rates aggressively.

**Effect:** EBITDA margin below 27% signals that TGLS has lost its cost advantage. For a company that was valued on 30%+ margins, dropping to mid-20s changes the earnings power and the multiple investors are willing to pay.

**Expected pricing impact:**
- EBITDA margin dips to 27–30% for 1 quarter: **5–10% downside**
- EBITDA margin below 27% for 2 quarters: **12–20% downside**
- Forward guidance implies margin below current year: **15–25% downside**

---

## 3) Colombian peso vs USD (quarterly average)

**Metric:** COP/USD quarterly average vs prior year; directional trend.

**Threshold:**
- Green: peso stable or weakening (COP/USD above 4,200)
- Watch: peso strengthening 3–5% YoY (COP/USD 4,000–4,200)
- Red: peso strengthening >5% YoY (COP/USD below 4,000)

**Why this applies to TGLS:** ~25% of TGLS's cost base is denominated in Colombian pesos (labor, local materials). Revenue is mostly in USD. When the peso appreciates, costs rise in dollar terms but revenue stays flat. In Q4 2025, the peso appreciated 9.5% YoY, and for the full year ~12%. Management's 2026 guidance ranges hinge on this: high-end assumes peso toward 4,000/USD; low-end assumes below 3,800/USD. This is the single largest uncontrollable cost variable.

**Effect:** Peso appreciation directly compresses gross margin with no lag. Unlike aluminum (which can be partially hedged or passed through), FX impact on labor costs is immediate and hard to offset. A 5% peso move can swing EBITDA by $10–15M.

**Expected pricing impact:**
- Peso appreciates 3–5% YoY: **5–8% downside**
- Peso appreciates 5–10% YoY: **10–18% downside**
- Peso appreciates >10% YoY sustained: **18–30% downside**

---

## 4) Aluminum cost (US Midwest premium trend)

**Metric:** US Midwest aluminum premium, quarterly average.

**Threshold:**
- Green: declining or stable QoQ
- Watch: rising 10–20% QoQ
- Red: rising >20% QoQ or premiums at multi-year highs

**Why this applies to TGLS:** Aluminum is a primary raw material for TGLS's window and curtain-wall products. In Q4 2025, US Midwest premiums doubled, creating what management called "industry-wide margin pressure." TGLS passed through $25M of tariff-related costs via pricing, but spot aluminum spikes hit margins before pass-throughs take effect (typically 1–2 quarter lag).

**Effect:** Aluminum cost spikes compress gross margin immediately. Price pass-throughs take 1–2 quarters to flow into backlog-based revenue. So there is always a margin squeeze period during rapid aluminum inflation, even if TGLS eventually recovers through pricing.

**Expected pricing impact:**
- Aluminum premiums up 10–20% QoQ: **3–7% downside**
- Premiums double or hit record highs: **8–15% downside**
- Sustained high aluminum + peso appreciation (combined): **15–25% downside**

---

## 5) Revenue growth deceleration (quarterly YoY)

**Metric:** Quarterly revenue YoY growth rate, tracked sequentially.

**Threshold:**
- Green: above 10% YoY
- Watch: 5–10% YoY
- Red: below 5% YoY, especially if decelerating from >10%

**Why this applies to TGLS:** Revenue growth decelerated sharply through 2025: Q1 +15.4%, Q2 +16.3%, Q3 +9.3%, Q4 +2.4%. That trajectory — from mid-teens to low single digits in two quarters — signals either demand softening or tough comps. At $90 the stock was priced for sustained double-digit growth. When Q3 showed single digits and Q4 barely grew, the growth premium evaporated.

**Effect:** Decelerating revenue combined with falling margins is the worst combination. Revenue growth was the reason investors tolerated a premium multiple. When it fades, the stock re-rates from "growth compounder" to "cyclical building products."

**Expected pricing impact:**
- Growth slows from >15% to 8–10%: **5–10% downside**
- Growth slows to 3–5% with margin compression: **12–20% downside**
- Growth below 3% with no margin recovery: **20–30% downside**

---

## 6) Consecutive EPS misses

**Metric:** Number of consecutive quarters where EPS misses consensus.

**Threshold:**
- Green: beating or in-line
- Watch: 1 miss
- Red: 2+ consecutive misses

**Why this applies to TGLS:** TGLS missed EPS in Q3 ($1.01 vs $1.11 est, -9%) and Q4 ($0.63 vs $0.85 est, -26%). Two consecutive misses, with the second miss larger than the first, signals the problem is worsening, not stabilizing. Q4 EPS of $0.63 vs $1.05 prior year is a 40% YoY decline. Analysts then cut 2026 targets — DA Davidson from $80 to $70, B. Riley from $70 to $65.

**Effect:** The first miss gets explained away. The second miss, especially a bigger one, triggers analyst downgrades, target cuts, and institutional selling. TGLS went from being a consistent beat-and-raise stock to a miss-and-cut stock in two quarters.

**Expected pricing impact:**
- 1 miss by <10%: **5–10% downside**
- 2 consecutive misses: **15–25% downside**
- 2 misses + analyst target cuts + forward guidance cut: **25–40% downside**

---

## 7) Installation revenue as % of total (mix shift)

**Metric:** Installation revenue share of total revenue.

**Threshold:**
- Green: below 15%
- Watch: 15–20%
- Red: above 20% or hitting "record high" levels

**Why this applies to TGLS:** Installation revenue carries lower margins than product-only sales. In Q4 2025, installation hit a "record high" proportion of revenue, which directly compressed gross margin. As TGLS's backlog mix shifts toward larger commercial projects that include installation, the margin profile structurally changes — higher revenue, lower margins.

**Effect:** Mix shift is subtle but persistent. It makes headline revenue growth look better while making margin trends worse. Investors who focus on revenue miss this until it shows up in gross margin, which is exactly what happened in Q3–Q4.

**Expected pricing impact:**
- Installation share rises modestly: **2–5% downside** (indirect, through margin)
- Installation at record share + gross margin declining: **5–12% downside**
- Structural mix shift with no margin offset: **10–18% downside**

## 8) Industry and macro risk factors

These are not single-number metrics but structural risks specific to TGLS that should be monitored as a group. **If 2+ turn red, treat as a major warning.**

**a) U.S. tariff policy on Colombian imports**
TGLS manufactures in Colombia. Any new or increased tariffs on Colombian goods directly hits margins. The current $25M tariff impact was offset via pricing, but escalation risk remains. A 50% aluminum tariff is already active.
- Watch: new tariff proposals on glass/aluminum from Colombia
- Red: tariffs imposed with no lead time for pricing adjustments

**b) U.S. housing starts and multifamily permits**
Single-family residential was $403M in 2025 (41% of revenue). Housing weakness directly reduces demand. Management said low-end 2026 guidance assumes "flattish single-family revenues."
- Watch: housing starts down 5–10% YoY
- Red: starts down >10% or multifamily permits declining

**c) U.S. commercial construction pipeline**
Commercial/multifamily backlog is the other half. If office/mixed-use construction slows due to rates or macro uncertainty, new orders decline.
- Watch: book-to-bill drops below 1.0x (currently 1.1x for 20 straight quarters)
- Red: book-to-bill below 1.0x for 2 quarters or backlog growth turns negative

**d) Redomiciliation execution**
TGLS is planning to redomicile to the U.S. — this could reduce tariff exposure but adds execution risk, potential tax changes, and transition costs.
- Watch: timeline delays or unexpected costs
- Red: regulatory complications or material tax impact

**Effect:** These risks compound. Peso appreciation + aluminum spike + tariff escalation hit simultaneously in Q4 2025 — that is why margins collapsed 770 bps in one quarter.

**Expected pricing impact (combined):**
- 1 factor turns red: **5–10% downside**
- 2 factors red simultaneously: **12–20% downside**
- 3+ factors red (full cost storm): **20–35% downside**

**Was the ~56% decline justified?**

Partially. The margin collapse was real — gross margin dropped 470 bps from Q2 to Q4, and Q4 EBITDA margin (25.4%) was dramatically below prior year (33.1%). Two consecutive EPS misses with the second one worse confirmed it was not a one-off. However, the magnitude of the decline looks overdone given: record backlog ($1.3B, +16%), near-zero cancellation rate, net debt/EBITDA of only 0.24x, insider buying ($13.1M by Energy Holdings at ~$43), P/E at a multi-year low of 12x, and analyst consensus still at Strong Buy ($63–84 targets). The key question is whether the cost headwinds (peso, aluminum, tariffs) are cyclical and reversible, or whether the margin profile has structurally changed.
