# Final checklist for AZO

## 1) Gross margin + LIFO / tariff cost pressure

**What to check**

- Gross margin %
- Change in gross margin vs last year
- LIFO charge / benefit
- Tariff or inflation commentary

**Why this applies to AZO**
This was the main reason profits disappointed. In Q1, gross margin fell **203 bps** from a **212 bps LIFO hit**. In Q2, it fell **137 bps** from a **138 bps LIFO hit**. AutoZone’s filings also explicitly flagged tariff-driven inflation on merchandise purchases. For this stock, cost pressure on parts goes straight into market reaction.

**Expected pricing impact**

- Margin stable or improving: **positive**
- Margin down modestly: **mild negative**
- Margin down **100+ bps** for repeated quarters: **strong negative**

---

## 2) Domestic same-store sales

**What to check**

- Domestic same-store sales %
- Trend vs last quarter
- Whether growth is accelerating or slowing

**Why this applies to AZO**
AZO is still mainly a mature U.S. retail story, so domestic same-store sales are the clearest signal of underlying demand. Domestic same-store sales improved to **4.8%** in Q1, then slowed to **3.4%** in Q2. That slowdown did not mean the business was bad, but it mattered because margins were already under pressure.

**Expected pricing impact**

- **4%+**: supportive
- **2% to 4%**: okay, but less exciting
- **0% to 2%**: warning
- **Negative**: strong negative

---

## 3) Domestic commercial sales growth

**What to check**

- Domestic commercial sales growth
- Average sales per program per week
- Whether commercial is still stronger than DIY

**Why this applies to AZO**
Commercial is one of AZO’s most important growth engines. In fiscal 2025, domestic commercial sales were **31.7% of total domestic sales**. In Q1 FY2026, domestic commercial sales rose **14.5%**; in Q2 they rose **9.8%**. Average sales per program per week also slowed from **10.1% growth** in Q1 to **4.8%** in Q2. So if commercial growth cools, one of AutoZone’s best support pillars weakens.

**Expected pricing impact**

- **10%+** commercial growth: positive
- **High single digits**: still fine
- **Low single digits**: warning
- Weak commercial plus weak comps: **strong negative**

---

## 4) Inventory efficiency

**What to check**

- Inventory per store
- Net inventory per store
- Inventory turns
- Accounts payable / inventory

**Why this applies to AZO**
AutoZone invests heavily in inventory availability, but if inventory rises faster than demand, returns and cash efficiency weaken. Inventory per store rose from **$849k** in Q1 FY2025 to **$927k** in Q1 FY2026 and then **$958k** in Q2 FY2026, while inventory turns slipped from **1.4x** to **1.3x** in Q2. That is not a disaster, but it is a measurable sign that the model was getting heavier.

**Expected pricing impact**

- Inventory controlled, turns stable: **neutral / positive**
- Inventory up but turns flat: **moderate negative**
- Inventory up and turns down: **stronger negative**

---

## 5) Operating profit trend vs sales growth

**What to check**

- Operating profit growth
- Operating expense % of sales
- Whether sales growth is translating into EBIT growth

**Why this applies to AZO**
For AZO, the market wants to see not just revenue growth, but **efficient growth**. In Q1, operating expenses rose to **34.0%** of sales from **33.3%**, and operating profit fell **6.8%**. In Q2, operating expenses were **36.1%** vs **36.0%**, and operating profit still fell **1.2%**. If sales go up but EBIT goes down, the stock usually struggles.

**Expected pricing impact**

- Sales up and EBIT up: **positive**
- Sales up but EBIT flat: **mild negative**
- Sales up but EBIT down: **strong negative**

---

## 6) Business / industry pressure checklist

**What to check**

- Tariff-related merchandise inflation
- Severe weather disruptions
- Vehicle market weakness
- Currency pressure on international results

**Why this applies to AZO**
These were directly tied to the recent drawdown. Reuters pointed to **tariffs, winter storms, and a bumpy vehicle market**, while the company said Q2 international sales in constant currency were **slightly below expectations**. This checklist matters because AZO’s business can look fine on sales but still disappoint if these external pressures squeeze margins or delay demand.

**Expected pricing impact**

- Short-lived weather issue: **mild negative**
- Ongoing tariff/inflation pressure: **moderate to strong negative**
- Multiple pressures at once: **strong negative**
