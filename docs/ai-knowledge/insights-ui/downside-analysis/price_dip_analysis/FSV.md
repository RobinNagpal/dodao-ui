# FirstService Corp (FSV) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

FirstService is North America's largest property services company operating through two segments. Top revenue products are: FirstService Residential / HOA community management (approximately $2.3B revenue, overseeing 9,000+ communities under multi-year HOA contracts — the moat anchor with high board switching costs and durable recurring revenue, organic growth +4% through FY2025), restoration services via Paul Davis / First Onsite (weather-dependent insurance-funded property restoration following storms, fires, and floods — inherently lumpy revenue), and fire protection/roofing via Century Fire Protection and roofing operations (tied to commercial/residential construction cycles, smaller contributors). FY2025 revenue was $5.5B (+5% YoY, acquisition-driven), adjusted EBITDA $563M (+10%), adjusted EPS $5.75 (+15%), and operating cash flow $445M+ (+56%).

The stock has fallen approximately 28% from its ATH of $209.66 to around $151. The decline was driven by Brands segment weakness: Q4 2025 restoration revenue fell 13% YoY due to below-average storm activity, roofing and fire protection were down 5%+ as housing and remodeling slowed, and Brands segment margin compressed from 11.9% to 10.8%. FY2025 total revenue growth of 5% was entirely acquisition-driven — Brands organic revenue actually declined 3%. The market is questioning whether FSV is a "premium compounder" or an "acquisition roll-up."

## Competitive Position & Moat

FSV holds the #1 position in North American residential property management with 9,000+ community contracts. Multi-year HOA agreements carry high board switching inertia. Cross-selling restoration and fire protection into the managed community base deepens relationships. Scale advantages in procurement, insurance, and staffing are difficult for smaller operators to replicate. Competitive displacement risk is minimal due to switching costs and institutional relationships (4/5 moat).

The top 2 direct competitors are Associa (large privately held community management company) and RealManage (growing regional community manager). Both compete in community management but FSV's scale and cross-selling across property services segments creates a structural advantage.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 26x Forward PE. FSV's 5-year median forward PE is approximately 25-30x, reflecting the premium for Residential recurring revenue. Property services peers (Rollins 30-35x, ABM Industries 15-18x) bracket this range. Current price of $151 implies approximately $6.50 EPS at 23x or $5.75 at 26x.

**Valuation regime check:** FSV's forward PE averaged approximately 20-25x in 2017-2020 and expanded to approximately 25-35x in 2021-2025 (premium compounder narrative). The expansion was partially justified by Residential segment organic growth consistency and cross-selling strategy. For the severe case, 15x forward PE (pre-expansion trough) is appropriate.

**Key variables:** FY2026 Adjusted EPS x PE Multiple.
