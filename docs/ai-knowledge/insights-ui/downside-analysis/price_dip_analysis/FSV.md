## FirstService Corp (FSV) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$151 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $209.66 / $209.66
**52-week low:** $147.23
**Already down:** ~28% from 52-week high

FSV is North America's largest property services company: FirstService Residential (HOA/community management, ~$2.3B revenue) and FirstService Brands (restoration via Paul Davis/First Onsite, fire protection via Century Fire, roofing). FY2025 revenue $5.5B (+5%), adjusted EBITDA $563M (+10%), adjusted EPS $5.75 (+15%). Operating cash flow up 56% to $445M+. Net debt/EBITDA improved from 2.0x to 1.6x.

**Business context:** FSV is a premium-multiple compounder facing a de-rating. Trailing PE of ~51x reflects historical compounding premium, but Brands segment organic revenue was **down 3% for FY2025** (Q4 restoration down 13%, roofing down 5%+). Total 5% growth was entirely acquisition-driven. The market is re-rating because acquisition-led growth deserves a lower multiple than organic growth. Residential held up well (+4% organic) but can't offset Brands weakness. Brands margin compressed from 11.9% to 10.8%.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Storm Activity Normalizes + Organic Growth Returns (20% probability)
**What happens:** Average storm season in 2026 reverses the restoration decline. Roofing and housing stabilize. Brands organic growth returns to 5–8% (historical average). The compounder premium restores.
**Price target: ~$190–210 (+26 to +39%)**

### Scenario 2: Muddle Through — Slow Recovery (30% probability)
**What happens:** Brands organic revenue flat to low-single-digit growth. Residential segment stable at +4%. Acquisitions drive overall 5–7% revenue growth. Margins expand slightly. Stock trades at 22–25x forward EPS.
**Price target: ~$145–160 (-4 to +6%)**

### Scenario 3: Brands Weakness Persists + Housing Slows (35% probability)
**What happens:** Below-average storm activity for second consecutive year. Roofing and housing remain weak. Brands organic revenue down again. The market de-rates FSV from "compounder" to "roll-up acquirer." Forward PE compresses to 18–20x.
**Price target: ~$110–130 (-14 to -27%)**

### Scenario 4: Recession + Brands Structural Decline (15% probability)
**What happens:** Economic downturn reduces property services spending. Restoration budgets cut. Roofing/remodeling collapses. Brands revenue drops 10%+. The goodwill-heavy balance sheet faces impairment risk. Stock reprices at 15x forward PE.
**Price target: ~$85–100 (-34 to -44%)**

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$138** (approximately 9% further downside).

## Framework 4: Precedent Drawdown + Catalyst Mapping

FSV fell 37% in 2022 (rate hike cycle) and ~35% during COVID. The current −28% is between these two precedents. Premium compounders that miss on organic growth typically see 30–40% corrections before stabilizing. **Precedent-implied further downside: −5 to −15%.** Recovery depends on storm activity and housing — both cyclical factors that normalize over 12–24 months.
