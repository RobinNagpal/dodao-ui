## CRH plc (CRH) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$102 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $131.55 / $131.55
**52-week low:** $76.75
**Already down:** ~23% from ATH

CRH is the world's largest building materials company. FY2025 revenue $37.4B (+5%), adjusted EBITDA $7.7B (+11%, record 20.5% margin, 12th consecutive year of expansion), FCF $5.0B (+18%). FY2026 EBITDA guided $8.1–8.5B (+5–10%). However, EPS guidance of $5.60–6.05 was slightly below consensus ($6.09). Total debt $19.7B with net debt/EBITDA of 2.29x. Completed 38 acquisitions for $4.1B in FY2025.

**Business context:** CRH's decline is a classic valuation reset. The stock hit $131 at 22x+ forward PE — stretched for a building materials cyclical. The market then repriced on: (1) slight EPS guidance miss, (2) tariff risk ($150–200M estimated operating profit impact from 10% tariffs), (3) rising debt from aggressive M&A pace, (4) slower organic growth (3–4% estimated vs. acquisition-driven total growth). S&P 500 inclusion broadened the investor base but also brought higher scrutiny.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Infrastructure Boom + Margin Expansion (15% probability)
**What happens:** IIJA/IRA spending accelerates. EBITDA hits $8.5B (high end). Margin expands toward 22–24% target. Tariffs moderate or are absorbed. Stock re-rates to 14–15x EV/EBITDA.
**Price target: ~$130–145 (+28 to +42%)**

### Scenario 2: Guidance Met — Steady Execution (30% probability)
**What happens:** EBITDA comes in at $8.3B (midpoint). Margins stable at 20–21%. M&A pace slows to $2–3B. Net debt/EBITDA stays at 2.0–2.3x. The stock trades at 11–12x EV/EBITDA.
**Price target: ~$100–115 (-2 to +13%)**

### Scenario 3: Tariff Hit + Construction Slowdown (35% probability)
**What happens:** 10–25% tariffs add $200–400M in costs. Non-residential construction weakens as rates stay elevated. EBITDA comes in at $7.8–8.0B (below guide). Organic growth stalls. Net debt/EBITDA rises toward 2.5x.
**Price target: ~$78–90 (-12 to -24%)**

### Scenario 4: Recession + Acquisition Writedowns (20% probability)
**What happens:** Construction spending contracts 10–15%. EBITDA drops to $6.5–7.0B. The $4.1B in acquisitions face impairment. Net debt/EBITDA spikes to 3x+. Rating agencies downgrade outlook.
**Price target: ~$58–70 (-31 to -43%)**

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$92** (approximately 10% further downside).
