## Paylocity Holding Corporation (PCTY) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$105 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $201.62 / $314.50 (Nov 2021)
**52-week low:** $100.50
**Already down:** ~67% from ATH, ~48% from 52-week high

Paylocity is a cloud-based human capital management (HCM) and payroll software provider targeting mid-market employers (20–5,000 employees). FY2025 (ended Jun 2025) revenue ~$1.58B with recurring revenue growth of ~14% YoY. FY2026 guided revenue $1.73–1.74B (~10% growth), with recurring revenue of $1.62–1.63B (+10–11%). Q2 FY2026 total revenue $416.1M (+10% YoY), adjusted EBITDA $142.7M (34.3% margin).

**Business context:** PCTY was a high-growth SaaS darling that commanded a 50–60x PE at its 2021 peak ($314). The company grew recurring revenue 20%+ annually for years, powered by new client wins and cross-selling modules (talent management, benefits, time tracking, expense management). The stock has been in steady decline as growth has decelerated: recurring revenue growth went from 20%+ (FY2023) to 14% (FY2025) to 10–11% (FY2026 guide). The market is repricing PCTY from a "high-growth compounder" multiple to a "mature SaaS" multiple. AI-driven product adoption (PCTY's AI assistant for HR workflows) is a potential reacceleration catalyst, but it hasn't shown up in the numbers yet. The company has excellent unit economics (68% gross margin, 34% EBITDA margin, 20%+ ROE) and a net cash balance sheet.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: AI-Driven Growth Reacceleration (10% probability)

**What happens:** PCTY's AI-powered HR assistant gains strong adoption, driving higher ARPU (average revenue per user) and net revenue retention. Cross-selling of new AI modules accelerates land-and-expand. Recurring revenue growth reaccelerates to 15%+ in FY2027. Client retention improves further (already 92%+). EBITDA margin expands to 36–38% as AI reduces support costs.

The market re-rates PCTY as a "rule of 50+" SaaS company (15% growth + 36% margin = 51%). Multiple expands back toward growth-SaaS territory.

**Why only 10%:** AI-driven reacceleration in HR software is speculative. Large language model-based HR assistants are being launched by every HCM competitor (ADP, Paychex, Workday, UKG). PCTY's AI products are incremental features, not transformative platform shifts. The core payroll business is inherently slow-growth (tied to employment growth + pricing). No evidence yet of AI impact in the numbers.

### Scenario 3: Growth Continues Decelerating + Multiple Compression (35% probability)

**What happens:** Recurring revenue growth decelerates from 11% to 8–9% in FY2027. New client wins slow as the mid-market HCM space becomes saturated and competition intensifies (ADP Moving upmarket, UKG/Workday moving downmarket). Net revenue retention dips below 110%. Annual contract values shrink as clients negotiate harder in a soft labor market.

EBITDA margin holds at 33–34% (no degradation), but the growth deceleration is what matters. The market re-rates PCTY to ADP/Paychex-like multiples: 20–22x PE, 15–17x EV/EBITDA. Analysts cut price targets citing "mature profile."

**Why 35% (tied for most likely):** This is the trend in the data. Growth has decelerated every year: 25% → 20% → 14% → 11%. The law of large numbers is working against PCTY — at $1.6B recurring revenue, each percentage point of growth requires $16M in incremental ARR. The mid-market HCM TAM is finite. Competition is intensifying from both above (Workday) and below (Gusto, Rippling).

### Scenario 4: Macro Weakness + Client Churn Spike (20% probability)

**What happens:** A U.S. recession leads to SMB/mid-market layoffs. PCTY's revenue is partially tied to employee headcount (per-employee-per-month pricing). Client companies shrink, reducing PEPM revenue. Some clients downgrade or churn as they cut HR software spending.

Recurring revenue growth drops to 3–5%. EBITDA margin compresses to 28–30% as PCTY invests more in retention and sales to offset weakness. FCF declines 20%+. The market re-rates PCTY as a cyclical SaaS company exposed to employment, not a secular grower.

**Why 20%:** PCTY's business model has mild cyclical exposure — payroll revenue is tied to employment levels, and SMB/mid-market companies cut headcount faster than large enterprises in downturns. The 2022–2023 period showed PCTY was somewhat resilient, but that was a "jobs recovery" environment. A genuine employment recession hasn't tested PCTY since its scale-up phase.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2027E Revenue Growth x EV/Revenue Multiple (using revenue multiple due to SaaS nature)

**Assumptions:** FY2026 revenue ~$1.74B, net cash ~$400M, ~55M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = ((Revenue x (1 + growth) x Multiple) + Net Cash) / Shares

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PE 15x** | $68 | $75 | $83 | $90 |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price ($105) implies:** ~$5.00 EPS at 21x PE, or ~$1.91B FY2027 revenue at 2.8x EV/Revenue. The market is pricing in modest growth at a modest premium.
- **Floor ($68):** Recession EPS ($4.50) at 15x PE. Requires employment downturn + margin compression.
- **Upside ($180):** Strong EPS ($6.00) at 30x PE. Requires growth reacceleration to 15%+.
- **Most sensitive variable:** Revenue growth rate. The difference between 5% growth and 13% growth at 3.5x EV/Revenue is only $10/share. But the difference between a 2.5x multiple (mature payroll) and a 5.5x multiple (growth SaaS) at the same revenue is $100/share. **The multiple is what matters, and the multiple is driven by the growth narrative.**

### Framework 3 Takeaway

PCTY's valuation is driven almost entirely by the growth narrative, not the absolute level of earnings. At $105, the stock is priced for "boring 10% grower" status. If growth reaccelerates to 15%+, the multiple expands and the stock doubles. If growth decelerates to 5–7%, the multiple compresses to ADP-like levels and the stock drops 25–30%. The underlying business is excellent (69% gross margin, strong FCF, net cash) — this is a "what multiple does it deserve?" stock, not a "will the business survive?" stock.

4. **Net cash balance sheet** — no financial risk, pure business execution story.
