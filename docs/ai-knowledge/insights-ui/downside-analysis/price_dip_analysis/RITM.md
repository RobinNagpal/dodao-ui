## Rithm Capital (RITM) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$9.48 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $12.74 / $18.68 (as NRZ, June 2018)
**52-week low:** $8.43
**Already down:** ~49% from ATH, ~26% from 52-week high

RITM is a global asset manager and financial services conglomerate with $100B+ investable assets. Key platforms: Newrez (top-5 mortgage servicer, $878B+ UPB, $63.3B origination volume), Sculptor Capital ($38B AUM), Crestline ($18B AUM), Paramount Group (Class A office NYC/SF), and a massive MSR portfolio. FY2025 EAD (Earnings Available for Distribution) of $2.35/share (record, +12% YoY). GAAP EPS $1.04 (depressed by $421.8M Q4 MSR fair value mark).

**Business context:** RITM is transitioning from a mortgage REIT to a diversified asset manager. Book value $12.66/share vs. $9.48 stock price = 25% discount to book. 10.7% dividend yield on $1.00/year, with 43% payout ratio on EAD ($2.35). The Newrez spinoff was paused in favor of deeper integration. Paramount Group acquisition (Dec 2025) added Class A office real estate — the market views this negatively. MSR fair value swings of $200–500M per quarter create massive GAAP volatility, confusing investors and depressing the multiple.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Re-Rating to Book Value — Sum-of-Parts Recognized (15% probability)

**What happens:** RITM executes on the asset management buildout. Sculptor + Crestline generate fee-based earnings that are valued at asset manager multiples (1.5–2x book). Newrez continues delivering $1B+ pre-tax operating income. Paramount Group sells 1301 Sixth Avenue at the targeted $1.4B valuation, demonstrating value.

The market begins valuing RITM on a sum-of-parts basis rather than applying a blanket mortgage REIT discount. P/B rises from 0.75x to 1.0–1.2x. Dividend yield normalizes from 10.7% to 7–8% (via price appreciation, not dividend cut).

**Why only 15%:** The complexity discount is deep and persistent. Investors prefer simple stories, and RITM is simultaneously a servicer, originator, alt asset manager, and office REIT owner. The halted Newrez spinoff removed the clearest re-rating catalyst. And MSR fair value swings will continue to make GAAP earnings volatile regardless of underlying performance.

### Scenario 3: Rate Spike + MSR Volatility — Dividend Concern (30% probability)

**What happens:** Interest rates rise unexpectedly (10-year to 5.5%+). MSR portfolio gains fair value ($300–500M mark-up), but origination volumes collapse. Newrez's origination income drops 30–40%. Housing transactions decline to 4M annual pace (near GFC lows).

Alternatively, rates drop sharply — MSR portfolio takes a $500M+ fair value loss (as prepayments accelerate), but origination volumes surge. Either scenario creates massive GAAP volatility.

In both cases, EAD compresses to $1.80–2.00/share as one leg of the business offsets the other imperfectly. The $1.00 dividend is still covered (56% payout on $1.80 EAD) but the market questions sustainability because GAAP earnings swing wildly.

Paramount Group faces rising cap rates and weakening NYC/SF office demand. A potential writedown surfaces. Institutional investors with mortgage REIT allocation limits are forced to sell as the stock approaches compliance thresholds.

**Why 30%:** Interest rate volatility is the structural risk for RITM. The MSR/origination offset is imperfect — rates don't move linearly, and there's always a lag. The Paramount office portfolio adds a new risk that didn't exist before Dec 2025. And RITM's stock has proven it can trade at deep discounts for extended periods — the market doesn't give it credit for the hedge.

### Scenario 4: Credit Crisis + Forced Selling — Dividend Cut (20% probability)

**What happens:** A mortgage market stress event occurs. MSR portfolio values collapse (prepayment speeds spike on a rapid rate drop scenario). Simultaneously, DQ rates on the servicing portfolio spike (recession-driven defaults). Newrez faces servicing advance obligations that strain liquidity.

Sculptor/Crestline face redemptions as institutional investors flee alt assets. Paramount Group's office portfolio is marked down 20–30%. Total book value drops from $12.66 to $9–10/share.

RITM is forced to cut the dividend from $1.00 to $0.60–0.70 to preserve capital. The income investor base — which is the primary buyer at current prices — exits en masse. The stock enters a reflexive cycle similar to the 2020 COVID crash.

**Why 20%:** The 2020 COVID crash showed RITM (then NRZ) can fall 82% in a stress event ($18.68→$3.33). A full repeat is unlikely (the company is better diversified now), but a 50–60% decline from current levels ($9.48→$4–5) is plausible if a dividend cut materializes. The Paramount acquisition and the halted Newrez spinoff have made the company MORE complex, not less — complexity amplifies panic selling.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2026 EAD/Share × Price/EAD Multiple (for a financial, EAD replaces traditional earnings)

### Price Matrix (EAD × P/EAD)

| FY2026 EAD/Share → | $1.50 (crisis) | $1.90 (miss) | $2.30 (base) | $2.50 (beat) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P/EAD 2.5x** | $3.75 | $4.75 | $5.75 | $6.25 |
| **P/EAD 3.5x** | $5.25 | $6.65 | $8.05 | $8.75 |
| **P/EAD 4.5x** | $6.75 | $8.55 | **$10.35** | $11.25 |
| **P/EAD 6.0x** | $9.00 | $11.40 | $13.80 | $15.00 |

**Alternative view — P/BV basis (at $12.66 book, adjusting for BV changes):**

| Book Value/Share → | $9.50 (crisis) | $11.00 (decline) | $12.66 (stable) | $13.50 (grow) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P/B 0.40x** | $3.80 | $4.40 | $5.06 | $5.40 |
| **P/B 0.60x** | $5.70 | $6.60 | $7.60 | $8.10 |
| **P/B 0.75x** | $7.13 | $8.25 | **$9.50** ← current | $10.13 |
| **P/B 1.00x** | $9.50 | $11.00 | $12.66 | $13.50 |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price ($9.48) implies:** ~$2.30 EAD at 4.1x P/EAD, or book value at 0.75x. Both are reasonable for the base case.
- **Floor ($3.75):** Crisis EAD ($1.50) at 2.5x P/EAD. This requires a 2020-style mortgage market stress event. The P/BV floor at 0.40x on $9.50 BV = $3.80, matching closely.
- **Most sensitive variable:** P/EAD multiple (sentiment/re-rating). Moving from 4.5x to 2.5x at constant EAD ($2.30) drops price from $10.35 to $5.75 (−44%). Moving EAD from $2.50 to $1.50 at constant multiple (4.5x) drops price from $11.25 to $6.75 (−40%). **Both matter roughly equally — this is a stock driven by both earnings quality AND the market's willingness to assign a reasonable multiple.**

### Framework 3 Takeaway

RITM's stress test confirms it's a **complexity-and-volatility-driven discount stock.** The fundamentals (EAD, book value, origination growth) are strong, but the GAAP volatility and business complexity prevent the market from assigning a fair multiple. The downside floor (~$3.75–$5.00) requires a 2020-level stress event, which is low probability but not zero. The upside ($13–15) requires nothing more than the market assigning a reasonable 1x P/BV — achievable but requiring a simplification catalyst.
