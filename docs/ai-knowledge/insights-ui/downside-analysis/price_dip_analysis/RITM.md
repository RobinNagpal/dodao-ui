# Rithm Capital (RITM) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Rithm Capital is a global asset manager and financial services conglomerate with $100B+ investable assets, transitioning from a mortgage REIT to a diversified asset manager. Mortgage servicing rights account for roughly 40% of earnings (Newrez platform, $878B+ UPB, #4 US mortgage servicer), origination roughly 25% ($63.3B volume), and asset management/investment roughly 35% (Sculptor Capital $38B AUM, Crestline $18B AUM, Paramount Group Class A office in NYC/SF). The MSR portfolio provides counter-cyclical exposure — rising rates increase MSR value while compressing origination, and vice versa.

RITM posted FY2025 record EAD (Earnings Available for Distribution) of $2.35/share (+12% YoY) and GAAP EPS of $1.04 (depressed by a $421.8M Q4 MSR fair value mark-down). Book value is $12.66/share, growing +4.2% YoY. The stock trades at a 25% discount to book.

The stock has fallen approximately 26% from its 52-week high of $12.74 to around $9.48 (and approximately 49% from the all-time high of $18.68 as NRZ in June 2018). The decline was driven by MSR fair value swings of $200-500M per quarter creating massive GAAP volatility, the Paramount Group acquisition (December 2025) adding office REIT exposure the market views negatively, the halted Newrez spinoff removing the clearest value-unlock catalyst, and a persistent complexity discount — the market cannot decide if this is a mortgage REIT, asset manager, or conglomerate. The stock hit a 52-week low a month after reporting record EAD.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $8.50, implying roughly 10% further downside from $9.48. RITM is a complexity-and-volatility-driven discount stock — the fundamentals (EAD, book value, origination growth) are strong, but GAAP volatility and business complexity prevent the market from assigning a fair multiple. The stock trades at 4x distributable earnings and 75% of book value while generating a 19% EAD ROE.

The bull case requires the market to recognize sum-of-parts value: Sculptor + Crestline at asset manager multiples, Newrez delivering $1B+ pre-tax, and Paramount validating through asset sales — producing a re-rating to book value ($12.30-13.70). The severe bear case envisions a mortgage market stress event with MSR portfolio collapse, servicer advance obligations straining liquidity, a dividend cut from $1.00 to $0.60-0.70, and the income investor base exiting — producing a price of $4.25-5.70. The 2020 COVID crash saw RITM (then NRZ) fall 82% from $18.68 to $3.33, though the company is better diversified now. The 13.8% dividend yield (at $9.48 price on $1.00/year dividend) provides meaningful support.

## Competitive Position & Moat

RITM holds the #4 position in US mortgage servicing with $845B+ UPB and vertical integration across origination, servicing, and asset management. The Rocket/Mr. Cooper merger creates a $1.2T competitor, but RITM's Blue Owl-style platform enables $1B+ unitranches through Sculptor and Crestline that pure servicers cannot offer. The MSR/origination natural hedge (rates up helps servicing, rates down helps origination) is a structural advantage, and the 43% payout ratio on record EAD provides dividend safety.

However, the complexity discount is deep and persistent. RITM is simultaneously a servicer, originator, alternative asset manager, and office REIT owner. MSR fair value swings of $200-500M per quarter make GAAP earnings unintelligible to most investors. The halted Newrez spinoff removed the clearest value-unlock catalyst. The Paramount Group acquisition added a "problem sector" (office) at precisely the wrong time. The MSR/origination offset is imperfect — rates do not move linearly, and there is always a lag.

RITM's current -34% from 52-week highs matches a normal mREIT correction of -30 to -40%. The 2020 COVID crash (-82%) is the extreme template requiring a genuine liquidity crisis. Two Harbors (MSR-heavy) fell -70% in COVID. Precedent-implied further downside is -10 to -30%, with the dividend yield providing a floor as yield-seekers step in at higher yields.

## What to Watch

The single most powerful re-rating catalyst is Newrez spinoff revival — if reconsidered, the stock moves immediately. Absent that, monitor EAD trajectory and dividend sustainability: the 10.7% yield is the primary buyer magnet, and any cut would be devastating. Paramount Group asset sales at targeted valuations would validate the acquisition; failures increase the discount. At 0.75x P/BV, buybacks are massively accretive — watch for management signaling increased repurchase activity.
