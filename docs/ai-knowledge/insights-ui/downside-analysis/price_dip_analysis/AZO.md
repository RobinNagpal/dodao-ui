## AutoZone (AZO) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$3,316 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $4,388.11 / $4,388.11
**52-week low:** $3,210.72
**Already down:** ~24% from ATH

AZO is the largest US auto parts retailer with 7,000+ stores. TTM revenue $19.6B. Q2 FY2026 gross margin fell 137 bps (138 bps LIFO charge). Domestic same-store sales +3.4% (decelerating from +4.8% in Q1). EPS $27.63 (beat consensus but down 2.3% YoY). Commercial (DIFM) growing +9.8% but also decelerating. Expects ~$60M LIFO charges each of Q3 and Q4.

**Business context:** AZO is a defensive compounder (aging vehicle fleet = structural tailwind) facing temporary margin pressure from LIFO accounting charges driven by tariff-induced cost inflation on imported auto parts. The company has negative shareholder equity (−$2.9B) from $55B+ in cumulative buybacks — extremely capital-efficient but with no equity buffer. ROI is 42.5%. Tariff pass-through to consumers is the key question.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Tariffs Absorbed + LIFO Reverses (15% probability)
**What happens:** Tariff costs successfully passed through. LIFO charges normalize in FY2027. Commercial segment sustains double-digit growth. Same-store sales reaccelerate to 4–5%. EPS resumes growth.
**Price target: ~$4,000–4,300 (+20 to 30%)**

### Scenario 2: Gradual Recovery — Guidance Met (35% probability)
**What happens:** LIFO charges continue at ~$60M/quarter through FY2026 as guided. Gross margin stays compressed 100–150 bps. Same-store sales grow 3–4%. EPS is flat to slightly down YoY. The market waits for LIFO normalization.
**Price target: ~$3,200–3,500 (-3 to +6%)**

### Scenario 3: Tariff Escalation + Consumer Weakness (30% probability)
**What happens:** Tariffs on Chinese auto parts escalate to 40%+. LIFO charges worsen beyond $60M/quarter. Consumers resist price pass-through, compressing margins further. Same-store sales decelerate to 1–2%. EPS declines 10–15%.
**Price target: ~$2,600–2,900 (-13 to -22%)**

### Scenario 4: Recession + Consumer Trade-Down (20% probability)
**What happens:** Full recession hits. Consumer spending on vehicle maintenance drops (deferred repairs). Same-store sales go negative. Gross margin compresses 300+ bps cumulatively. EPS drops 20–25%. The negative equity becomes a talking point.
**Price target: ~$2,200–2,500 (-25 to -34%)**

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$3,050** (approximately 8% further downside).

## Framework 4: Precedent Drawdown + Catalyst Mapping

AZO has historically been one of the most defensive retailers. The current −24% drawdown matches the 2022 correction (~−20%). In the 2008–2009 recession, AZO actually outperformed (consumers repair rather than replace). The LIFO-driven margin issue is temporary and will reverse when inflation abates. **Precedent-implied further downside: −5 to −15%** — limited because the franchise is exceptional and the headwind is accounting-driven.
