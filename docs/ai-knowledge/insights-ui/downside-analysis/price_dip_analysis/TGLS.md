# Tecnoglass Inc. (TGLS) — Price Dip Analysis

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Tecnoglass is a Colombian-based vertically integrated manufacturer of architectural glass and windows, the #2 US glass fabricator. Architectural glass/windows for US construction accounts for roughly 70% of revenue, the Colombian domestic market roughly 20%, and other Latin American markets roughly 10%. The company's Colombian manufacturing base in Barranquilla provides a 30-40% cost advantage over US competitors. A new $400M US vinyl window facility is under construction to diversify away from Colombia-only production.

TGLS posted FY2025 revenue of $983.6M (+10.5% YoY), adjusted EBITDA of $291M (~29.6% margin), and a record backlog of $1.3B (+16% YoY) with book-to-bill above 1.1x for 20 consecutive quarters. However, Q4 2025 gross margin fell to 40.0% (from 42.8% full year) with net income dropping to $26.1M from $47.0M. FY2026 EBITDA guidance of $265-305M implied margin compression to 25-27%.

The stock has fallen approximately 56% from its ATH/52-week high of $90.34 to around $40. The collapse was triggered by a "perfect storm" of margin compression: Colombian peso appreciation of approximately 9.5% YoY (every 5% COP move equals 110bps margin hit), US aluminum prices near all-time highs (Midwest premiums doubled), new tariffs on aluminum imports, and the 2026 EBITDA guidance implying significant margin compression. The stock rose 800% from its 2020 COVID low to $90 ATH; much of that move was powered by margin expansion to 33% that may have been the anomaly, not the norm. Significant insider buying ($13.1M by Holding Energy in March 2026) provides a contrarian signal.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $35, implying roughly 12% further downside from $40. TGLS is trading at a trough multiple (7x EV/EBITDA) on mid-guidance EBITDA, which means either the stock is cheap and headwinds are transient, or the market correctly anticipates further margin deterioration below guidance.

The bull case requires COP depreciation back toward 4,500 COP/USD, aluminum prices softening 10-15%, and the US vinyl facility beginning to contribute — producing margin recovery to 30-32% and a stock price of $58-66. The severe bear case envisions a US multifamily construction downturn with permits dropping 20%+, COP appreciating to 3,600 COP/USD, and EBITDA collapsing to $200-220M — producing a price of $20-26. The balance sheet is pristine (0.16x D/E), so the risk is to equity value from margin compression, not to solvency.

## Competitive Position & Moat

TGLS holds the #2 position in US glass fabrication with a unique Colombian cost advantage of 30-40% versus domestic competitors. The record $1.3B backlog with 20 consecutive quarters of book-to-bill above 1.1x demonstrates sustained demand. The vertical integration (glass manufacturing, window fabrication, aluminum extrusion) creates efficiency advantages. The $400M US vinyl window facility, once operational, will provide tariff-immune and peso-immune revenue, structurally diversifying the business.

However, a single US tariff on Colombian goods could erase the entire moat overnight — the 30-40% cost advantage is denominated in the COP/USD exchange rate and trade policy, both of which are outside management's control. The Colombian peso has been structurally strengthening on nearshoring capital flows — the same trend that benefits TGLS's order book also hurts its cost structure, creating a paradox. The margin band is unusually wide (18% to 33% over the last 3 years) because of the COP/USD mismatch, making traditional valuation challenging. Customer concentration is meaningful, and the US construction cycle is late-stage with high rates suppressing multifamily starts.

TGLS's -56% drawdown is in line with building-products sector corrections (-45 to -60%). Historically TGLS has recovered strongly: +800% from 2020 low, +350% from 2022 low. But those recoveries were powered by margin expansion to 33%; if margins permanently reset to 25-27%, the recovery ceiling is lower. Insider buying of $13.1M at current levels mirrors patterns before previous recoveries. Precedent-implied further downside: -10 to -30%.

## What to Watch

The most important near-term signal is Q1 2026 gross margin trajectory: is Q4's 40.0% the trough or still declining? COP/USD rate is the continuous margin driver — below 4,000 means more pressure, above 4,200 means relief. US multifamily building permits are the leading indicator for orders beyond the $1.3B backlog. The $13.1M insider buying is the strongest insider signal of any stock analyzed; continued buying would reinforce conviction at these levels.
