## Tecnoglass Inc. (TGLS) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$40 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** ~$90.34 (Jun 2025) / $90.34
**52-week low:** ~$39.97 (March 2026)
**Already down:** ~56% from ATH

Tecnoglass is a Colombian-based vertically integrated manufacturer of architectural glass and windows, primarily serving the U.S. multifamily/commercial construction market. FY2025 revenue $983.6M (+10.5% YoY), adjusted EBITDA $291M (~29.6% margin), record backlog of $1.3B (+16% YoY) with book-to-bill above 1.1x for 20 consecutive quarters. Operates manufacturing in Barranquilla, Colombia with a new $400M U.S. vinyl window facility under construction.

**Business context:** TGLS rose ~800% from its 2020 COVID low (~$5) to $90 ATH in mid-2025, driven by strong U.S. construction demand, pricing power, and high EBITDA margins (peaked at 33%). The collapse from $90 to $40 was triggered by a "perfect storm" of margin compression: (1) Colombian peso appreciation of ~9.5% YoY (5% COP move = 110bps margin hit), (2) U.S. aluminum prices near all-time highs (Midwest premiums doubled), (3) new tariffs on aluminum imports adding SG&A cost, and (4) Q4 2025 gross margin fell to 40.0% (from 42.8% full year) with net income dropping to $26.1M from $47.0M. The 2026 EBITDA guidance of $265–305M implied margin compression to ~25–27%, spooking investors. Significant insider buying ($13.1M by Holding Energy in March 2026) provides a contrarian signal.

**Key dynamic:** TGLS is optically cheap on PE and EV/EBITDA, but those ratios are based on 2025 earnings that included 33% peak margins. The 2026 guidance ($265–305M EBITDA on $1.06–1.13B revenue) implies 25–27% EBITDA margins — a massive compression from the 33% peak just quarters ago. Insider buying of $13.1M at current levels is a meaningful bullish signal.

### Scenario 1: Headwinds Reverse + U.S. Facility Ramp (15% probability)

Aluminum prices soften 10–15% from peaks as tariff exemptions or alternative sourcing take effect. The $1.3B backlog converts at improving margins through 2026–2027. The U.S. vinyl facility begins contributing revenue in late 2026, eventually adding $200–300M in U.S.-manufactured revenue that is tariff-immune and peso-immune.

EBITDA margin recovers to 30–32%. Revenue reaches $1.15B+. The stock re-rates as the "peak earnings concern" narrative fades.

**Why only 15%:** COP has been strengthening on structural flows (remittances, oil, nearshoring). Aluminum tariffs are a political football with no clear resolution timeline. The U.S. facility is still 12–18 months from meaningful contribution. All three headwinds need to reverse simultaneously for this scenario.

**What happens:** COP continues appreciating toward 3,800 COP/USD (central bank keeps rates high to fight inflation). Aluminum costs stay elevated or rise further. New tariff rounds hit glass/window imports from Colombia specifically. The U.S. construction cycle weakens as high mortgage rates suppress multifamily starts.

**Why 35% (most likely):** The macro headwinds (COP strength, aluminum tariffs, construction cycle) are structural, not transient. Colombian peso appreciation is driven by nearshoring capital flows into Colombia — the same trend that benefits TGLS's order book also hurts its cost structure. High U.S. interest rates are already slowing multifamily construction starts. This is the "good company in a bad environment" scenario.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2026 EBITDA x EV/EBITDA Multiple

**Assumptions:** Net debt ~$150M, ~47M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = ((EBITDA x Multiple) - Net Debt) / Shares

### Price Matrix

| FY2026 EBITDA → | $220M (deep miss) | $265M (guide low) | $285M (guide mid) | $305M (guide high) | $340M (recovery) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EV/EBITDA 5x** | $20 | $25 | $27 | $29 | $33 |
| **EV/EBITDA 7x** | $30 | $36 | **$40** ← current | $42 | $47 |
| **EV/EBITDA 9x** | $39 | $47 | $51 | $55 | $62 |
| **EV/EBITDA 11x** | $49 | $59 | $64 | $68 | $77 |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price ($40) implies:** ~$285M EBITDA at 7x — the market is pricing in guidance midpoint at a trough multiple. This is either "cheap because it should be" or "the market is being too pessimistic."
- **Floor ($20):** Deep margin compression ($220M EBITDA) at recession multiples (5x). Requires construction downturn + COP squeeze simultaneously.
- **Upside ($77):** Margin recovery ($340M EBITDA) at a premium building-products multiple (11x). Requires headwinds reversal + U.S. facility ramp.
- **Most sensitive variable:** EBITDA margin. Every 100bps of margin on ~$1.1B revenue = ~$11M EBITDA = ~$1.60–2.30/share depending on multiple. The COP/aluminum double headwind creates unusually wide margin uncertainty.

### Framework 3 Takeaway

TGLS is trading at a trough multiple on mid-guidance EBITDA, which means either (a) the stock is cheap and the margin headwinds are transient, or (b) the market correctly anticipates further margin deterioration below guidance. The key insight is that TGLS's margin band is unusually wide (18% to 33% over the last 3 years) because of the COP/USD mismatch — this makes traditional valuation challenging. The balance sheet is pristine, so the risk is to equity value (margin compression) not to solvency.
