### Competitive Profiles ŌĆö Strongest Moats (5/5 and 4/5)

**AGM (5/5)** ŌĆö Congressionally chartered monopoly. The only national secondary market for US farm mortgages. GSE status provides 50-100 bps funding cost advantage. Competitors cannot replicate without an act of Congress.

**CRH (5/5)** ŌĆö #1 US aggregates and asphalt producer. Quarries are irreplaceable local monopolies: heavy materials economically viable within ~50 miles, permitting takes 7-10+ years with NIMBY opposition. Vertically integrated (quarry ŌåÆ asphalt ŌåÆ paving).

**AZO (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 US auto parts retail (6,720 stores, $19.6B revenue, 25% market share). Hub-and-spoke distribution gets parts to stores in hours. 12.6-year average US car age is a structural tailwind. EV threat is 15-20+ years from materiality.

**PGR (4/5)** ŌĆö #1-2 US auto insurer (18.1% share). Best-in-class telematics (Snapshot) enables granular risk pricing that takes years to replicate. Combined ratio ~87% vs. industry 99%+.

**ZTS (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 global animal health (25-31% companion animal share). $600M/yr R&D drives first-in-class innovations. FDA animal drug approval takes 5-7 years per species. Threat: Merck Numelvi challenges the ~$2B derm franchise.

**ZBRA (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 enterprise AIDC (40-50% share in mobile computers/barcode scanners). Proprietary software ecosystem, 10K+ channel partners, 5-7 year device lifecycles create deep lock-in.

**VITL (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 US pasture-raised eggs. Nearly 600 family farm partnerships built over 15+ years. Third-party certified supply chain is capital and time-intensive to replicate. Only ~2% household penetration.

**ZG (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 US online real estate traffic (43% share, 250M+ monthly users). Network effects: more consumers ŌåÆ more agent advertisers ŌåÆ more data/features. CoStar/Homes.com has been pulling back spend.

**LOAR (4/5)** ŌĆö 89% proprietary aerospace parts with sole-source positions on 20-30 year aircraft platforms. FAA/EASA certification creates multi-year barriers per part number.

**LAD (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 US auto retailer ($37.6B revenue). State franchise laws legally protect dealers from OEM-direct sales. Market massively fragmented (top 10 <10% combined share).

**ESAB (4/5)** ŌĆö #3 global welding. Consumables razor/blade model (60% of revenue is recurring filler metals/gas). Welding procedure re-certification creates switching costs.

**KTB (4/5)** ŌĆö #2 US jeans (Wrangler $1.91B +6%, Lee $750M -5%). Wrangler is #1 jean at Walmart with deeply embedded shelf space. Cultural moat in Western/rural America.

**ACM (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 ENR design firm globally. On shortlists for virtually every major US infrastructure program (IIJA, CHIPS). Security clearances and institutional knowledge create switching costs.

**FSV (4/5)** ŌĆö #1 NA residential property management. Manages 9,000+ communities. Multi-year HOA contracts with high board switching inertia.

## How Competition Changes the Downside Assessment

### Stocks Where Competition INCREASES Downside vs. Financial Analysis Alone
- **DUOL/UPWK/FRSH:** AI disruption is structural, not cyclical. Financial models that assume "growth decelerates" underestimate the risk of "product becomes obsolete."
- **SE:** TikTok Shop is a new competitive model (social commerce) with no historical analogue in SEA e-commerce.
- **BROS:** 7 Brew's explosive growth proves the drive-thru format is replicable, undermining the "unique culture" bull case.

### Stocks Where Competition DECREASES Downside vs. Financial Analysis Alone
- **AGM/CRH:** Monopoly/near-monopoly positions mean competitive risk is zero. All downside comes from macro/credit, not competition.
- **LOAR/ZBRA:** Sole-source/high-switching-cost businesses have downside driven by valuation/cycle, not competitive displacement.
- **AZO:** In a recession, auto parts retail actually benefits (consumers repair instead of replace).

### Stocks Where Competition IS THE Entire Bear Case
- **INVZ:** Hesai (profitable, 20x larger) + camera-only autonomy = existential competitive risk.
- **SLNO:** ACADIA's carbetocin + GLP-1 off-label could break the monopoly before exclusivity expires.
- **PRKS:** Epic Universe is a direct competitive event ŌĆö no ratio model captures "Universal opened a mega-park next door."

## Key Patterns

### Pattern 1 (Strongest Signal): Irreplaceable Position + Temporary Headwind

AZO, AGM, CRH, PGR all have moat scores of 4-5/5 ŌĆö competition literally cannot take their customers. Their declines (ŌłÆ23 to ŌłÆ31%) are driven by temporary factors (LIFO charges, ag credit cycle, valuation reset, earnings normalization) that reverse with time. These stocks have the least further downside because the franchise is permanent and the headwind is not.

### Pattern 2: Bad News Is Known and Priced

VITL, ARE, ELF, PCTY have depressed multiples reflecting public information. This pattern is weaker when the moat is also weak ŌĆö DUOL and FRSH appeared to fit but were downgraded because their moats (1/5) mean the "bad news" may be structural, not just a temporary overreaction.

### Pattern 3 (Bottom of Ranking): Unresolved or Structural Risk

ZBRA, UPWK, EQPT share unresolved negative catalysts. ZBRA faces unknown tariff policy + margin trajectory. UPWK faces unresolved AI displacement. EQPT faces leverage that amplifies any unknown. When risk is uncertain, the market may not have fully priced it.
