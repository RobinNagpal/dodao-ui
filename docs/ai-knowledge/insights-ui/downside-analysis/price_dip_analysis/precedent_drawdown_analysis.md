### Catalyst Mapping Assessment

The current episode most closely resembles the **2021–23 drawdown** but is **less severe on the revenue side** (growth vs. decline) and **more severe on the leverage side** (3.0x vs. 2.5x). The 2018–19 trade war is also relevant since the current tariff risk is similar in nature.

**Key precedent insight:** In the 2021–23 episode, ZBRA bottomed at roughly $200 after a −68% drawdown, coinciding with the revenue trough (Q3 2023: −31% YoY). The stock didn't bottom on the first miss — it took 3–4 consecutive quarters of misses before sellers were exhausted. **The current drawdown has only had one quarter of elevated concern (Q4 2025 report). If the pattern repeats, the bottom may not come until Q2–Q3 2026 earnings reveal whether margin headwinds are transitory or structural.**

**Precedent-implied expected further downside: −20 to −44%**, with the most likely outcome around **−20% ($160)** if revenue holds up, or **−44% ($113)** if a 2023-style revenue miss materializes.

**DUOL most resembles Netflix 2022 (−76%) or Meta 2022 (−77%), NOT Peloton.** The key reasons:

**The Peloton scenario (−98%, never recovers) requires the product to be commoditized.** For DUOL, this means general-purpose AI (ChatGPT, Gemini) must fully replace structured language learning. This is possible but not certain — DUOL's gamification and habit mechanics have no direct AI equivalent.

**Precedent-implied expected further downside: Limited (0–15%).** DUOL has already overshot the Netflix and Meta analogues. The stock is trading below where most comparable growth-stock crashes bottomed on a percentage basis. Now the precedent analysis confirms that DUOL has **already overshot comparable precedents**, suggesting the scenario modeling may be *overstating* further downside. The true expected downside may be closer to **−5 to −10%** with high upside optionality if the Netflix/Meta recovery pattern plays out.
