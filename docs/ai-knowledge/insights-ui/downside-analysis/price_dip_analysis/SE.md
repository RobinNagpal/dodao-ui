# Sea Limited (SE) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Sea Limited is a Singapore-based technology conglomerate operating three core businesses across Southeast Asia and Latin America. Shopee e-commerce accounts for roughly 60% of revenue ($127B GMV in FY2025, +27% YoY), SeaMoney fintech roughly 21% ($9.2B loan book outstanding, +80% YoY), and Garena gaming roughly 10% (Free Fire). The company processed $18.2B in total revenue in FY2025 (+29% YoY) and generated $4.5B in free cash flow.

SE posted FY2025 revenue of approximately $18.2B (+29% YoY), Q4 revenue of $6.9B (+38.4% YoY), and Q4 adjusted EPS of $0.80. Shopee GMV reached $127B (+27%), Garena generated $1.9B revenue (down from $4.3B pandemic peak but bookings rebounded +37% on Free Fire resurgence), and SeaMoney revenue hit $3.8B (+60% YoY) with loans outstanding at $9.2B (+80% YoY).

The stock has fallen approximately 60% from its 52-week high of $199.30 to around $79 (and 78% from the ATH of $366.99 in October 2021). The crash was driven by Q4 2025 EPS missing estimates despite the revenue beat, costs growing faster than revenue (COGS +40.4% vs revenue +38.4%), SeaMoney credit loss provisions spiking +67% in Q4, cautious 2026 guidance (Shopee GMV +25%, EBITDA "no lower than 2025"), and the structural threat of TikTok Shop taking market share across Southeast Asia.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $62, implying roughly 22% further downside from $79. SE's risk is concentrated in the lending business — if NPLs spike, EBITDA can drop dramatically because provisions flow straight through the P&L. The $9.2B loan book growing at 80% is the single variable that can take SE from "fairly valued" to "cheap" or "expensive" depending on credit quality outcomes.

**Weighted expected price calculation:**
- Bull midpoint $122.50 x 10% = $12.25
- Base midpoint $85.00 x 25% = $21.25
- Bear midpoint $51.00 x 40% = $20.40
- Severe midpoint $34.00 x 25% = $8.50
- **Weighted Expected Price = $62.40**

The bull case requires Shopee GMV growing 30%+ (beating guidance), TikTok Shop stalling on regulatory issues, and SeaMoney NPLs staying at 1.1% — producing a re-rating to $115-130. The severe bear case envisions a Southeast Asian economic slowdown where NPL ratio spikes to 4-5%, Shopee GMV growth decelerates to 10-15%, EBITDA drops 30-40%, and write-offs of $500M-1B are possible — producing a price of $28-40, near the 2022 trough. The 2021-2022 drawdown bottomed at $40 from $367; if this cycle rhymes, $40-55 is the likely floor.

The stress test matrix shows that at 16x EV/EBITDA (SE's 3-year median is ~17x, Southeast Asian internet peers trade at 12-18x), current price of $79 implies ~$3.0B EBITDA at 15x or ~$2.5B at 18x. The floor ($28) occurs at a credit cycle blowup with $2.0B EBITDA at 8x. The most sensitive variable is lending provisions — a 100bp NPL increase on a $12B+ loan book (2026 estimate) could wipe $500M-1B off EBITDA.

## Competitive Position & Moat

SE holds the #1 position in Southeast Asian e-commerce with 48-52% market share through Shopee, an integrated ecosystem spanning e-commerce, digital payments, and gaming that no competitor replicates. The $127B GMV, 700M addressable population in SE Asia, and rising internet penetration provide a structural secular growth tailwind. The $4.5B FCF and $1B buyback program demonstrate cash generation at scale. Garena's Free Fire, despite declining from pandemic peaks, remains a globally significant mobile game with rebounding engagement.

However, TikTok Shop is a genuine structural threat — not speculation, but visible in merchant data across every SE Asian market. Shopee's Vietnam market share has declined from 68% to 56% as TikTok Shop gained. Revenue at risk from TikTok Shop is estimated at $1.1-1.4B (6-8% of total revenue), the largest dollar amount across our coverage universe. Shopee's thin margins (operating margin 8.7%) leave little room for competitive response without returning to the subsidize-growth cycle. SeaMoney's $9.2B loan book growing at 80% YoY is the most dangerous asset: credit cycles in emerging markets always lag loan growth by 12-24 months, and the +67% Q4 provision spike may be the early signal. The Q4 earnings pattern — revenue +38% but COGS +40% — shows negative operating leverage.

SE's moat (3/5 — network effects + ecosystem integration) is moderate: #1 market share is real, but TikTok Shop demonstrates it can be eroded. Precedent-implied further downside: -15 to -45%. MercadoLibre recovered 3x from its 2022 trough; Grab never recovered from its 2022 crash. The difference is credit quality plus competitive moat strength — SE sits between these outcomes.

## What to Watch

The single most important metric is SeaMoney NPL ratio — any move above 1.5% signals the credit cycle is turning. TikTok Shop GMV market share trajectory in Indonesia (SE's largest market) determines whether Shopee can maintain pricing power or must return to subsidies. OpEx versus revenue growth rate is the operating leverage signal: if costs continue growing faster than revenue (as in Q4), the profitable growth thesis fails. Shopee take rate trends reveal whether Shopee is monetizing its dominant position (rising) or facing competitive pressure forcing subsidies (falling).

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 16x EV/EBITDA. SE's 3-year median EV/EBITDA is approximately 17x. Southeast Asian internet peers (Grab 12-15x, GoTo 10-14x, MercadoLibre 20-25x) bracket this range. Current price of $79 implies ~$3.0B EBITDA at 15x or ~$2.5B EBITDA at 18x.

**Valuation regime check:** SE's EV/EBITDA averaged ~25-30x in 2020-2022 (pandemic premium) and ~15-18x in 2023-2025 (normalization). The compression from pandemic-era multiples reflects the loss of the "growth at any cost" premium and is largely justified by the shift to profitability over growth. The pre-pandemic-era multiple is not relevant because SE's business was fundamentally different (pre-profit, Garena-dependent). The 15-18x range is the appropriate regime. For severe case, 8-10x reflects emerging market distress multiples (Grab 2022 trough, GoTo IPO-to-trough).

**Assumptions:** Net cash/debt approximately neutral, ~570M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = (EBITDA x Multiple) / Shares
