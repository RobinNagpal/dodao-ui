## Sea Limited (SE) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$79 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $199.30 / $366.99 (Oct 2021)
**52-week low:** $77.05
**Already down:** ~78% from ATH, ~60% from 52-week high

Sea Limited is a Singapore-based technology conglomerate operating three core businesses: Shopee (Southeast Asia & LatAm e-commerce, ~60% of revenue), Garena (digital entertainment / Free Fire gaming), and SeaMoney/Monee (digital financial services including lending, payments, and insurance). FY2025 revenue ~$18.2B (+29% YoY), Q4 revenue $6.9B (+38.4% YoY), adjusted EPS $0.80 (Q4), full year adjusted EBITDA significantly improved.

**Business context:** Sea dominates Southeast Asian e-commerce through Shopee, which processed $127B in GMV in FY2025 (+27% YoY). Garena generated $1.9B in revenue (down sharply from $4.3B pandemic peak but bookings rebounded +37% in 2025 driven by Free Fire resurgence). SeaMoney/Monee is the fastest-growing segment: revenue $3.8B (+60% YoY) with loans outstanding reaching $9.2B (+80% YoY). The stock crashed from $367 ATH in 2021 to the low-$40s in 2022 during the tech de-rating, rallied to $199 in mid-2025, then gave back most gains on Q4 2025 earnings miss (EPS missed estimates despite revenue beat) and concerns about OpEx growth outpacing revenue.

**Key dynamic:** SE is a high-growth platform with three distinct business lines at very different maturity stages. Shopee is a proven e-commerce leader but margins are thin and competitive intensity (TikTok Shop) is rising. Garena is a declining cash cow (gaming peaked in COVID). SeaMoney is the wild card — explosive loan book growth (+80% YoY to $9.2B) but credit loss provisions are spiking (+67% in Q4). The stock plunged after Q4 2025 earnings despite 38% revenue growth because costs grew faster (COGS +40.4%), EPS missed, and guidance was perceived as cautious (Shopee GMV +25% for 2026, EBITDA "no lower than 2025"). The market is questioning whether Sea can deliver profitable growth or is trapped in a subsidize-growth cycle.

### Scenario 3: Competitive Erosion + Lending Deterioration (40% probability)

**What happens:** TikTok Shop gains 5–8pp of GMV market share in Indonesia and Vietnam. Shopee responds by increasing S&M spend and subsidies, pushing EBITDA margins back toward zero. Revenue grows 15–20% but EBITDA stagnates or declines.

SeaMoney's $9.2B loan book starts showing cracks: NPL ratio rises from 1.1% to 2.0–2.5% as Southeast Asian consumer credit weakens (inflation, rate tightening). Provisions spike another 40–50%. Garena bookings decline QoQ as Free Fire engagement fades post-content burst.

Management cuts medium-term profitability targets. Analysts downgrade.

**Why 40% (most likely):** This is the highest probability scenario because: (1) TikTok Shop is genuinely taking share — this isn't speculation, it's visible in merchant data; (2) fast-growing lending books in emerging markets always deteriorate eventually — 80% loan growth is a red flag not a green flag; (3) the Q4 earnings already showed the pattern: revenue +38% but COGS +40%, EPS missed. Operating leverage is negative.

### Scenario 4: Regional Crisis + Credit Cycle Blow-Up (25% probability)

**What happens:** A Southeast Asian economic slowdown (triggered by China slowdown spillover, capital flight, or commodity price collapse) hits all three segments simultaneously. Shopee GMV growth decelerates to 10–15%. Consumer spending contracts, hitting both e-commerce volumes and ability to repay SeaMoney loans.

SeaMoney NPL ratio spikes to 4–5% on the $9.2B book. Provisions double. Management is forced to slow lending, which kills the growth narrative for the segment. Write-offs of $500M–1B are plausible. Garena bookings decline as discretionary gaming spend contracts.

Total EBITDA drops 30–40%. The stock reprices to 2022-era trough multiples.

**Why 25%:** Southeast Asian credit cycles are volatile. Indonesia, Vietnam, and Thailand have all experienced rapid consumer credit expansion followed by sharp corrections in the past decade. SeaMoney's 80% loan growth into a tightening environment mirrors the pattern that preceded previous EM fintech blowups (Ant Financial in China, Nubank in Brazil pre-2022). The $9.2B book is large enough to be systemically relevant to SE's P&L.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2026 Adjusted EBITDA x EV/EBITDA Multiple

**Assumptions:** Net cash/debt ~neutral, ~570M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = (EBITDA x Multiple) / Shares

### Price Matrix

| FY2026 EBITDA → | $2.0B (miss) | $2.5B (flat) | $3.0B (guide) | $3.5B (beat) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EV/EBITDA 8x** | $28 | $35 | $42 | $49 |
| **EV/EBITDA 12x** | $42 | $53 | $63 | $74 |
| **EV/EBITDA 16x** | $56 | $70 | **$84** ← near current | $98 |
| **EV/EBITDA 20x** | $70 | $88 | $105 | $123 |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price (~$79) implies:** ~$3.0B EBITDA at 15x, or ~$2.5B at 18x. The market is pricing in moderate growth at a moderate premium — not aggressive.
- **Floor ($28):** Credit cycle blowup with $2.0B EBITDA at 8x. Requires a regional economic crisis plus lending write-offs.
- **Upside ($123):** Beat scenario with $3.5B EBITDA at 20x. Requires Shopee dominance + lending quality + margin expansion. Unlikely near term.
- **Most sensitive variable:** Lending provisions. A 100bp NPL increase on a $12B+ loan book (2026 estimate) could wipe $500M–1B off EBITDA. This is the swing factor that makes SE harder to value than a pure e-commerce company.

### Framework 3 Takeaway

SE's valuation is not stretched at current levels — the stock is priced for guidance delivery at a fair multiple. The risk is concentrated in the lending business: if NPLs spike, EBITDA can drop dramatically because provisions flow straight through the P&L. The $9.2B loan book growing at 80% is the single variable that can take SE from "fairly valued" to "cheap" or "expensive" depending on credit quality outcomes.

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$60** (approximately 24% further downside from $79).

**Key catalysts and timing:**
- **Q1 2026 earnings (May/Jun):** OpEx vs revenue trajectory — if costs still growing faster, confirms the pattern.
- **SeaMoney NPL ratio (quarterly):** The single most important metric. Any move above 1.5% signals credit cycle turning.
- **TikTok Shop GMV data (monthly/quarterly):** Market share trajectory in Indonesia (SE's largest market).
- **Indonesia macro data (monthly):** Consumer confidence, inflation, IDR/USD — leading indicators for lending quality.
- **Garena bookings (quarterly):** If Free Fire re-engagement fades, the gaming cash cow disappears.
- **Shopee take rate (quarterly):** Rising take rate = Shopee monetizing its dominant position. Falling = competitive pressure forcing subsidies.

**Key risks:**
1. **Lending book ($9.2B, +80% YoY)** — fastest-growing segment is the most dangerous. Credit cycles always lag loan growth by 12–24 months.
2. **TikTok Shop competition** — genuinely disrupting Shopee's merchant and buyer base across SE Asia.
3. **OpEx discipline** — COGS grew faster than revenue in Q4 (40.4% vs 38.4%). Operating leverage is negative.
4. **Garena dependency fading** — gaming is now only 11% of revenue but was historically the cash engine funding Shopee losses.
5. **Geopolitical risk** — SE Asia exposure to China slowdown, US-China tensions affecting Tencent (SE's largest shareholder).

**Key catalysts (bullish):**
1. **SeaMoney expansion beyond credit** — digital banking, insurance, wealth management could diversify risk.
2. **Shopee logistics efficiency** — internal logistics network achieving scale could improve margins.
3. **$1B buyback program** — announced in Q3 2025, provides floor support.
4. **SE Asia secular growth** — 700M population, rising internet penetration, growing middle class.
