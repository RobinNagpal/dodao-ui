# AECOM Technology (ACM) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

AECOM is the world's #1 ranked engineering-design firm, providing advisory, planning, design, program management, and construction management services for infrastructure projects globally. TTM revenue is approximately $16.1B with record-high adjusted operating margin of 19.8% on net service revenue (NSR). Backlog stands at a record $26B (+9% YoY), with book-to-burn above 1.0x for 21 consecutive quarters. Top revenue products are: advisory/planning/design services for infrastructure (approximately 50% of NSR — transportation, water, environment, energy transition), program and construction management (approximately 30% of NSR — overseeing large public/private capital programs), and government/defense consulting (approximately 20% of NSR — federal advisory, environmental remediation, defense facilities).

The stock has fallen approximately 37% from its ATH of $135.52 to around $85. ACM crashed 21% in five days after its November 2025 investor day, where management simultaneously raised long-term targets (20%+ margin by FY2028, 15%+ EPS CAGR through FY2029) while guiding FY2026 free cash flow 42% lower to approximately $400M. The market read this as empire-building over shareholder returns. A 43-day federal government shutdown further delayed project awards. Broader concerns around DOGE-related spending cuts and infrastructure funding uncertainty have kept the stock depressed.

## Competitive Position & Moat

AECOM holds the #1 position globally in engineering-design revenue (ENR rankings). The moat rests on government security clearances and institutional knowledge that create high switching costs on multi-year federal programs, placement on shortlists for virtually every major US infrastructure program, and deep relationships across federal/state/local agencies built over decades. The backlog of $26B provides multi-year revenue visibility. Competitive displacement risk is minimal due to the security clearance and institutional knowledge moat (4/5).

The top 2 direct competitors are Jacobs Solutions (J, #2 in engineering/design) and WSP Global (WSP, large international infrastructure consultancy). Both are formidable but ACM's scale and government relationships provide structural advantages on large programs.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 20x FCF. Infrastructure engineering peers (Jacobs 22-25x, WSP 20-25x) bracket this range. ACM's current 20x on guided $400M FCF implies $85 — exactly the current price, meaning no margin of safety at current valuation.

**Valuation regime check:** ACM's FCF multiple averaged approximately 15-18x in 2018-2021 and expanded to approximately 20-25x in 2022-2025 (IIJA/IRA infrastructure premium). The expansion was partially justified by structural infrastructure spending tailwinds. For the severe case, 12-15x FCF (pre-IIJA multiple, reflecting uncertainty about government funding) is appropriate.

**Key variables:** FY2026 FCF x FCF Multiple. Assumes approximately 120M diluted shares.
