# Section 1: Analysis

### 1. What the Company Does and Why It Dropped

AECOM is the world's #1 ranked engineering-design firm, providing advisory, planning, design, program management, and construction management services for infrastructure projects globally. TTM revenue is ~$16.1B with record-high adjusted operating margin of 19.8% on net service revenue (NSR). Backlog stands at a record $26B (+9% YoY), with book-to-burn above 1.0x for 21 consecutive quarters.

**Top products / revenue lines:**
1. **Advisory, planning, and design services for infrastructure** (~50% of NSR) — transportation, water, environment, energy transition
2. **Program and construction management** (~30% of NSR) — overseeing large public and private capital programs
3. **Government and defense consulting** (~20% of NSR) — federal advisory, environmental remediation, defense facilities

**Why the stock dropped:** ACM crashed 21% in five days after its November 2025 investor day, where management simultaneously raised long-term targets (20%+ margin by FY2028, 15%+ EPS CAGR through FY2029) while guiding FY2026 free cash flow 42% lower to ~$400M. The market read this as empire-building over shareholder returns. A 43-day federal government shutdown further delayed project awards. Broader concerns around DOGE-related spending cuts and infrastructure funding uncertainty have kept the stock depressed. Q1 FY2026 beat on revenue and EPS with record backlog, but FCF skepticism remains the dominant overhang.

### 3. Competitive Position and Moat

AECOM holds the #1 position globally in engineering-design revenue (ENR rankings). Its moat rests on three pillars: (a) government security clearances and institutional knowledge that create high switching costs on multi-year federal programs, (b) placement on shortlists for virtually every major US infrastructure program due to track record and scale, and (c) deep relationships across federal, state, and local agencies built over decades. The backlog of $26B provides multi-year revenue visibility.

The investment thesis hinges on whether FY2026 truly is a one-time "investment year" or the start of a multi-year FCF drag. Investors should track quarterly FCF progression, federal budget/award activity, and margin trajectory on NSR.

## Scenario Details

### Scenario 1: Investment Year Pays Off — FCF Recovers FY2027 (15% probability)
**What happens:** AI and restructuring investments deliver margin expansion to 20%+ on NSR. FCF recovers to $600M+ in FY2027. Infrastructure tailwinds (IIJA/IRA) drive backlog conversion. The stock re-rates from "busted compounder" to "margin expansion story."
**Price target: ~$115–130 (+35 to 53%)**

### Scenario 2: Muddle Through — Guidance Met (30% probability)
**What happens:** FY2026 FCF hits ~$400M. EPS comes in at $5.85–6.05 (guided range). Backlog grows but the market stays skeptical until FCF recovers. Stock trades sideways.
**Price target: ~$80–95 (-6 to +12%)**

### Scenario 3: FCF Disappoints Further + Government Spending Cuts (35% probability)
**What happens:** Investment spending runs over budget. Federal spending uncertainty (DOGE-related cuts) delays project awards. FCF comes in below $350M. Analysts cut estimates further. The "investment year" becomes "investment years."
**Price target: ~$60–72 (-15 to -29%)**

### Scenario 4: Infrastructure Spending Cuts + Recession (20% probability)
**What happens:** IIJA/IRA spending delayed or cut under new administration. Government shutdown repeats. Commercial construction weakens. Revenue declines. FCF drops to $250–300M. The stock reprices as a declining government contractor.
**Price target: ~$45–55 (-35 to -47%)**

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$76** (approximately 11% further downside).
