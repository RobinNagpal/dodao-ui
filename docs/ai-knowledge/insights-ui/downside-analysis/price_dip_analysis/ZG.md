# Zillow Group (ZG) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Zillow is the dominant US real estate marketplace with 235M average monthly unique users, 9.6B annual website visits, and roughly 2x the traffic of #2 Realtor.com. Revenue comes from three products: Residential/Premier Agent (agent advertising and lead referrals, approximately $1.7B or 66% of revenue, +7% YoY), Rentals (landlord and property manager listing visibility, approximately $630M or 24% of revenue, +39% YoY), and Mortgages/Zillow Home Loans (direct mortgage originations, approximately $199M or 8% of revenue, +37% YoY). The company exited iBuying in 2021 after a $528M loss. FY2025 revenue was $2.58B (+16% YoY), GAAP net income $23M (first profitable year), adjusted EBITDA $230M (~8.9% margin), and FCF $420M.

The stock has fallen approximately 55% from its 52-week high of $90.22 to around $40.63. Three forces converged: the Rocket-Redfin-Compass alliance (Rocket acquired Redfin for $1.75B and partnered with Compass to push 500K+ listings onto Redfin) created the first vertically integrated search-to-agent-to-mortgage competitor that directly threatens Zillow's agent advertising model. Mortgage rates stuck at approximately 6.3% have suppressed housing transaction volume, limiting the core Residential segment to only 7% growth. Despite $420M in FCF, GAAP net income is only $23M and stock-based compensation dilutes shareholders by -8.56% annually.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $31, implying roughly 24% further downside from $40.63. The bear scenario carries the highest probability (35%) because the Rocket-Redfin-Compass alliance is not theoretical — Rocket spent $1.75B on Redfin and immediately locked in Compass's 500K listings, creating a closed loop that puts Zillow outside the transaction.

**Weighted expected price calculation:**
- Bull midpoint $56.00 x 15% = $8.40
- Base midpoint $34.50 x 30% = $10.35
- Bear midpoint $28.00 x 35% = $9.80
- Severe midpoint $20.00 x 20% = $4.00
- **Weighted Expected Price = $32.55**

Multiple compression is the primary risk. At $2.7B revenue and 10% EBITDA margin, the difference between 30x EV/EBITDA ($36) and 20x ($25) is a 31% swing. Revenue and margin combined only swing +/-$8 at a fixed 30x. ZG is a multiple bet, and the multiple depends on whether Zillow retains its moat against Rocket-Redfin.

The bull case requires mortgage rates to drift to 5.8-6.0% and Rocket-Redfin integration to hit friction, giving Zillow a 12-18 month window. The severe bear case envisions a US recession crushing housing (existing home sales dropping to 3.8M) with Rocket-Redfin gaining real traction, pushing total revenue down 3-5%.

## Competitive Position & Moat

Zillow's moat is real — no competitor matches its traffic volume or brand recognition for home search. The Zestimate has become a cultural reference point. But the moat is in the top of the funnel (search), not the transaction. Rocket-Redfin-Compass is building a moat around the full transaction, which may prove more defensible and monetizable. If agents get better conversion from Redfin/Compass leads (because a mortgage is already pre-approved via Rocket), they will shift ad spend away from Zillow regardless of traffic.

The top 2 direct competitors are Redfin/Rocket (now integrated, offering search-to-agent-to-mortgage in a closed loop) and Realtor.com (CoStar, #2 in traffic but significantly smaller). Revenue at risk from Rocket-Redfin is concentrated in the Residential/Premier Agent segment: if 20-30% of large agent teams shift ad budgets, that represents $340-510M in at-risk revenue (13-20% of total). SBC dilution of -8.56% annually further erodes shareholder value.

ZG's moat (3/5 — network effects + brand, but top-of-funnel only) is moderate: #1 traffic is real but the Rocket-Redfin alliance is the first credible end-to-end competitor in Zillow's history. The ROIC of -0.93% means the company is destroying economic value even as revenue grows.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 30x EV/EBITDA. ZG's 3-year median EV/EBITDA is approximately 30-40x (reflecting tech marketplace premium). Real estate marketplace peers (Redfin pre-acquisition was 15-20x, CoStar 25-35x) bracket this range. Current price of $40.63 implies approximately $2.7B revenue at 12% margin at 30x.

**Valuation regime check:** ZG's EV/EBITDA was negative/meaningless in 2020-2022 (pre-profit iBuying era) and ~30-50x in 2023-2025 (post-iBuying, transitioning to profitability). The current ~41x is within the post-pivot range. For the severe case, 20x EV/EBITDA (reflecting a competitive de-rating where the traffic moat is demonstrably weaker) is appropriate.

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue x EBITDA Margin. Assumes net cash $650M, ~245M diluted shares.

**Formula:** Price = ((Revenue x Margin x Multiple) + $0.65B) / 245M shares
