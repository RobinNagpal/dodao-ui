# Kontoor Brands (KTB) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Kontoor Brands owns Wrangler, Lee, and (since June 2025) Helly Hansen. Top revenue products are: Wrangler (approximately $1.75B revenue, the #1 jeans brand at Walmart with deep penetration in workwear, Western, and outdoor channels — the core cash engine), Lee (mainstream value denim at department stores, declining for years with no credible turnaround catalyst), and Helly Hansen (approximately $957M acquisition from Canadian Tire, premium outdoor/sailing brand with international exposure, growing but integration risk is real). Overall: approximately 65-70% wholesale, approximately 70% North America, approximately 85% manufactured in Asia. FY2025 revenue was $2.83B (including Helly Hansen), Q4 revenue $1.02B (+46% YoY), adjusted EPS $1.73 (Q4, beating estimates).

The stock has fallen approximately 23% from its ATH of $92.82 to around $71. Two converging fears drove the decline: tariff exposure (with 85% of production in Asia, India/Pakistan >60%, Kontoor faces a projected $135M tariff headwind in 2026, roughly 26% of EBITDA) and integration risk from the Helly Hansen acquisition layered onto an already-leveraged balance sheet (D/E 2.83x). The closure of Mexico manufacturing in September 2025 worsened tariff optics by reducing geographic diversification right as trade tensions escalated.

The key swing variable is EBITDA margin — tariff mitigation success or failure drives the outcome more than revenue trajectory.

## Competitive Position & Moat

Wrangler is the core moat — the #2 US jeans brand behind Levi's but #1 at Walmart, where shelf space relationships are deep and sticky. The brand has a cultural moat in Western/rural America that is difficult for competitors to replicate. The 65-70% wholesale distribution creates lock-in but also concentration risk. Helly Hansen adds premium diversification but is unproven within Kontoor's operating model. Lee is the weak link — losing share to private label and fast fashion with no credible turnaround catalyst.

The top 2 direct competitors are Levi Strauss (LEVI, #1 US jeans brand, stronger brand but different distribution) and private label / fast fashion (Walmart's own brands, Shein, etc., which threaten value denim pricing). Revenue at risk from private label is concentrated in Lee (already losing share) and potentially Wrangler at the margin — if tariff-driven price increases push Wrangler above the consumer's price tolerance, trade-down to Walmart private label could accelerate. Estimated revenue at risk: $100-200M from Lee continued decline + Wrangler trade-down (3-6% of total revenue).

KTB's moat (4/5 — brand/cultural moat in Western/rural America + Walmart shelf space lock-in) is strong for Wrangler specifically but diluted by Lee's chronic underperformance.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 10x EV/EBITDA. Mid-cycle branded apparel companies trade at 9-12x EV/EBITDA (Levi's 8-10x, PVH 8-10x, Hanesbrands 6-8x). KTB's Wrangler strength and Helly Hansen premium positioning justify the upper end. Current price of $71 implies approximately $3.4B revenue at 17% margin at 10x or $3.4B at 14% margin at 12x.

**Valuation regime check:** KTB's EV/EBITDA averaged ~8-9x in 2019-2021 (post-spinoff, pre-Helly Hansen) and expanded to ~10-12x in 2022-2025 (Wrangler momentum, quality execution). The expansion is partially justified by Wrangler's growth trajectory and strong FCF generation. For the severe case, 7-8x EV/EBITDA (VFC/HBI trough territory for levered apparel companies) is appropriate.

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue x EBITDA Margin. Assumes net debt $1.42B, ~55.6M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = ((Revenue x Margin x Multiple) - $1.42B) / 55.6M shares
