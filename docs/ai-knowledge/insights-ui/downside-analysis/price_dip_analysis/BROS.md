# Dutch Bros (BROS) — Price Dip Analysis

*Time horizon: next 12 months (through Q1 2027). All prices and ratios as of March 2026.*

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Dutch Bros is a fast-growing drive-thru coffee chain with 1,136 stores primarily in the western US, expanding nationwide toward a 4,000+ store target. Top revenue products are: drive-thru beverages in western US markets (approximately 85% of revenue, $2.1M average unit volume, the core franchise), new market expansion including the Clutch Coffee Bar acquisition in the Carolinas (approximately 10% of revenue), and mobile/loyalty app transactions (67% of total sales, driving frequency and data). FY2025 revenue was $1.64B (+28% YoY), net income approximately $96M (EPS $0.64), adjusted EBITDA approximately $277M (17% margin), SSS +5.6%.

The stock has fallen approximately 40% from its 52-week high of $77.88 to around $46.69. The decline was driven by valuation compression from extreme levels (79x PE, 34.5x EV/EBITDA), SSS guidance deceleration to +3-5% from +5.6%, massive -20.78% SBC dilution, and consumer spending fears. Market cap is approximately $8.2B and net debt approximately $820M.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $38, implying roughly 19% further downside from $47. BROS is fundamentally a multiple-driven stock — at 20x EV/EBITDA (still generous for a restaurant chain), even hitting guidance produces $36-40, below the current price.

## Competitive Position & Moat

Dutch Bros has built a distinctive drive-thru coffee culture with strong brand loyalty — 67% of transactions through the mobile app, and the "broista" service model creates emotional connection. The 1,136-store footprint versus 4,000+ target represents a long runway for unit growth.

The top 2 direct competitors are Starbucks (SBUX, dominant US coffee chain, turnaround under new CEO targeting drive-thru experience) and 7 Brew (rapidly expanding drive-thru-only coffee chain, 280+ stores, entering BROS's Arizona/Texas markets). Revenue at risk from 7 Brew overlap is modest near-term: 200-300 overlapping stores by 2028, 2-4% SSS drag in overlapping markets, approximately $35-70M per year (2-4% of revenue). The bigger risk is cannibalization from BROS's own store expansion density.

BROS's moat (3/5 — brand/culture + loyalty app) is moderate: the drive-thru coffee format is increasingly crowded, and -20.78% SBC dilution undermines shareholder value creation. ROIC of 5.7% is well below WACC.

## Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Base multiple justification:** 20x EV/EBITDA. Growth restaurant chains trade at 15-30x EV/EBITDA (Chipotle 25-30x, Wingstop 25-30x, mature restaurants 10-15x). BROS's 34.5x current multiple reflects "next Starbucks" premium. 20x represents a more normalized growth restaurant multiple. Current price implies approximately $2.0B at 18% margin at 25x.

**Valuation regime check:** BROS has been public since late 2021. EV/EBITDA ranged from ~25-40x (post-IPO growth premium). Pre-IPO, comparable drive-thru coffee chains traded at 12-18x. For the severe case, 12x EV/EBITDA (mature restaurant chain level) is appropriate.

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue x EBITDA Margin. Assumes 20x EV/EBITDA, net debt $820M, ~175M shares diluted.
