# Freshworks (FRSH) — Price Dip Analysis

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Freshworks is a SaaS company providing customer experience and employee experience software, positioned as the "affordable ServiceNow/Salesforce" targeting SMB and mid-market. Freshdesk (CX/helpdesk) accounts for roughly 45% of revenue, Freshservice (ITSM) roughly 35% and is the more defensible growth engine ($510M ARR growing 26%), and Freshsales CRM plus other products roughly 20%. Mid-market and enterprise clients now represent more than 60% of total ARR. The AI product "Freddy AI" is at $25M ARR nearly doubling YoY.

FRSH posted FY2025 revenue of $838.8M (+16% YoY), achieved first-year GAAP operating profitability ($13.2M), non-GAAP operating margin of 21.2%, and free cash flow of $223.1M (26.6% margin). The company holds $843.7M cash with zero debt and announced a $400M buyback program.

The stock has fallen approximately 50% from its 52-week high of $16.14 to around $8.03 (and 85% from ATH of $53.36 in November 2021). The decline was driven by growth deceleration (21% FY2023 to 16% FY2025 to 13.5-14.5% guided FY2026), a 17% drop post-Q4 earnings on soft 2026 profit outlook, AI disruption fears (AI-native startups could make traditional helpdesk ticketing obsolete), net dollar retention declining from 108% to 104%, and the Artisan Small Cap Fund specifically exiting over AI disruption concerns. The $3.6B accumulated deficit from years of SBC dilution weighs on GAAP optics.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $7.90, roughly flat with approximately 2% further downside from $8.03. At 1.7x forward EV/Revenue and 7x EV/FCF, FRSH is deep value for the SaaS sector. The discount exists because AI disruption risk is real — if AI-native customer support tools make traditional helpdesk ticketing obsolete, Freshdesk's entire value proposition erodes.

The bull case requires Freshservice to continue dominating at 25%+ growth, winning ServiceNow deals at 30% lower cost, while Freddy AI scales from $25M to $80M+ ARR — producing a re-rating from 1.7x to 4-5x EV/Revenue and a price of $14.50-18.50. The severe bear case envisions AI agents fundamentally disrupting the helpdesk model, enterprises building their own AI support systems, SMBs switching to $50/month AI-native tools versus Freshdesk at $300/month, and FRSH becoming "legacy SaaS" at 0.8-1x EV/Revenue — producing a price of $3.20-4.40. The $844M cash ($2.81/share) means FRSH cannot go to zero — even in the worst case, you are buying a $1B revenue SaaS company with $844M cash.

## Competitive Position & Moat

FRSH holds approximately 3% of the CX market with a genuine total-cost-of-ownership advantage versus ServiceNow and Salesforce, particularly in the mid-market. Freshservice (ITSM) is the standout asset — growing 26%, winning enterprise deals, and more defensible than Freshdesk because ITSM has deeper workflow complexity that is harder for AI to fully automate. The 85% gross margin, $223M FCF, and $844M cash with zero debt provide exceptional financial resilience. At $8, the $400M buyback can retire approximately 17% of shares outstanding.

However, the moat is weak across the broader business. Low switching costs mean customers can migrate to competitors or AI-native alternatives with relatively little friction. ServiceNow is moving downmarket with cheaper tiers targeting FRSH's mid-market sweet spot. AI commoditization is the structural threat: Intercom's Fin, Zendesk's AI, and numerous startups demonstrate that AI can handle over 50% of support tickets today. If that reaches 80%+, the "system of record for tickets" becomes less valuable. The net dollar retention declining from 108% to 104% signals the beginning of expansion slowdown.

FRSH's -85% from ATH exceeds the typical SaaS crash of -60 to -70%, matching only Twilio (-82%) among major SaaS companies. Zendesk, FRSH's closest competitor, was taken private at a similar point in its decline — suggesting PE sees value in depressed SaaS assets. FRSH at 1.7x EV/Revenue is cheaper than where Zendesk was acquired. Precedent-implied further downside: -5 to -25%.

## What to Watch

The key variable is whether AI helps or hurts Freshworks. Freddy AI ARR trajectory ($25M and doubling) signals whether FRSH can monetize AI rather than be disrupted by it. Net dollar retention is the canary — below 104% confirms expansion is stalling; below 100% signals contraction. Freshservice growth rate (currently 26%) is the bull case engine; deceleration below 20% removes the best narrative. Buyback execution pace signals management's view of intrinsic value. At 1.7x EV/Revenue, FRSH is a plausible PE target (Zendesk precedent).
