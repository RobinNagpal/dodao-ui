# Section 1: Analysis

## What the Company Does & Why It Dropped

Zoetis is the world's largest animal health company, spun off from Pfizer in 2013. It develops and sells medicines, vaccines, and diagnostics for companion animals (~65% of revenue) and livestock (~35%). FY2025 revenue was $9.5B (+6% organic), adjusted EPS $6.41, with 2026 guidance of $9.83-10.03B revenue and $7.00-7.10 adjusted EPS.

**Top revenue products:**
1. **Apoquel/Cytopoint** dermatology franchise (~$2B) — now facing Merck Numelvi competition (approved Feb 2026), the first real challenger since Apoquel launched in 2013
2. **Simparica Trio** parasiticide — growing steadily, less at risk from current headwinds
3. **Librela** (OA pain mAb for dogs/cats, $500M+) — declining 16% in the US on neurological safety concerns, FDA label updates, and "Dear Veterinarian" warnings
4. **Livestock vaccines/medicines** (~35% of total revenue) — steady but low growth

**Why the stock dropped 54%:** Four reinforcing headwinds hit simultaneously. Librela, the flagship growth driver, saw its U.S. sales decline 16% after FDA-documented neurological adverse events led to label changes and veterinarian warnings. Broader companion animal market weakness emerged as vet visits declined, particularly among Gen Z/Millennial pet owners under inflationary pressure. Merck's Numelvi gained FDA approval in February 2026, creating real competition for the $2B dermatology franchise for the first time. Management cut Q3 2025 revenue guidance, confirming the slowdown. The result was massive multiple compression from ~35x forward PE to ~19x — the cheapest ZTS has traded since its 2013 IPO.

## Competitive Position & Moat

ZTS holds the #1 global position in companion animal health with 25-31% market share, operating in a duopoly with Elanco. The moat rests on $600M/year R&D spend, FDA approval timelines of 5-7 years per species (creating enormous barriers to entry), and relationships with 100K+ veterinarians. The company generates 72% gross margins, $2.3B FCF, 45% ROE, and 25.5% ROIC — franchise-quality economics.

**The key threat:** Merck's Numelvi is the first real challenger to the dermatology franchise since 2013. Revenue at risk: Numelvi could take 10-15% of Apoquel/Cytopoint share ($200-300M). Librela's safety issues compound this by removing the growth driver that was supposed to offset competitive pressure.

## What to Watch

The next 6-12 months will determine whether ZTS is temporarily impaired or structurally de-rated. Librela sales trajectory is the single most important variable — stabilization would signal the safety overhang is manageable, while further decline would confirm franchise damage. Numelvi prescription share data will reveal how fast Merck is taking dermatology market share. Vet visit trends from AVMA/IDEXX quarterly reports are the leading indicator for the entire companion animal segment.
