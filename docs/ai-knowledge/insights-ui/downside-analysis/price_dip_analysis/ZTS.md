## Zoetis Inc. (ZTS) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$115 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $172.23 / ~$249 (Nov 2021)
**52-week low:** $113.30
**Already down:** ~54% from ATH, ~33% from 52-week high

Zoetis is the world's largest animal health company, spun off from Pfizer in 2013. The company develops and commercializes medicines, vaccines, and diagnostics for companion animals (~65% of revenue) and livestock (~35%). FY2025 revenue $9.5B (+2% reported, +6% organic operational), net income $2.7B ($6.02/share), adjusted EPS $6.41. 2026 guidance: revenue $9.83–10.03B (+3–5% organic), adjusted EPS $7.00–7.10.

**Business context:** ZTS was a premium compounder for years — steady 7–10% revenue growth, 70%+ gross margins, 35%+ operating margins, strong pricing power in a duopoly with Elanco. The stock peaked at ~$249 in late 2021 and has been declining ever since. The primary catalysts for the decline: (1) Librela (OA pain mAb for dogs/cats) — the company's flagship growth driver — saw U.S. sales decline 16% as reports of neurological side effects led to FDA label updates and "Dear Veterinarian" warnings; (2) broader companion animal market weakness with declining vet visits, particularly among Gen Z/Millennial pet owners under inflationary pressure; (3) Merck's FDA approval of Numelvi (Feb 2026) creating real competition in the dermatology franchise (Apoquel/Cytopoint); (4) Q3 2025 revenue guidance was cut, signaling management acknowledged the slowdown.

**Key dynamic:** ZTS is a blue-chip compounder that the market has de-rated from ~35x forward PE to ~19x forward PE — a massive multiple compression for a business with 72% gross margins and $2.3B FCF. At 19x forward PE, ZTS is cheaper than it has been at any point since its 2013 IPO except briefly during COVID. The question is whether this is a buying opportunity (high-quality franchise temporarily impaired) or a value trap (structurally lower growth + Librela permanently damaged).

U.S. Librela sales stabilize and begin growing again in H2 2026. Vet visits recover as inflation eases and pet owners resume routine care. Numelvi (Merck's dermatology competitor) has a slow launch, preserving Apoquel/Cytopoint franchise.

Revenue growth reaccelerates to 8–10% organic. Adjusted EPS reaches $7.50+. The market re-rates ZTS back toward historical multiples.

**Why only 10%:** Librela's safety issues are FDA-documented. Once a drug gets a "Dear Veterinarian" warning and label update for neurological adverse events, veterinarian confidence is slow to recover — this is the animal health equivalent of a black-box warning. Vet visit decline is driven by structural factors (inflation, Gen Z pet spending patterns) not just cyclical ones. Numelvi will take dermatology share.

U.S. Librela sales decline another 25–30% in 2026 to $250–300M globally. Veterinarian prescribing shifts to alternative OA treatments (NSAIDs, physical therapy). The franchise's projected $1B+ peak sales target is permanently lowered to $500–600M.

Simultaneously, Merck's Numelvi gains meaningful traction in dermatology, taking 10–15% share from Apoquel/Cytopoint in its first full year. Vet visits continue declining 2–3% annually. Revenue growth drops to 1–2% organic. Adjusted EPS comes in at $6.75–6.90 (below guide).

**Why 40% (most likely):** Drug safety concerns that have reached FDA labeling and "Dear Veterinarian" warnings rarely fully resolve. Veterinarians are risk-averse prescribers — once safety concerns are established, it takes years (not quarters) to rebuild confidence. The Numelvi launch is real competition that ZTS hasn't faced in dermatology since Apoquel launched in 2013. And declining vet visits is a secular headwind.

Companion animal revenue declines 3–5% (first decline in ZTS's history as a public company). Librela faces class-action litigation from pet owners whose animals experienced neurological adverse events — even if ZTS ultimately prevails, the legal overhang suppresses the multiple.

**Why 20%:** Consumer spending on pets is discretionary despite the "humanization of pets" thesis. During the 2008–2009 recession, even veterinary spend contracted. ZTS has never been tested through a real recession at this scale. Librela litigation is a real possibility — the FDA adverse event reports include deaths, and plaintiff attorneys are already circling.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2026 Adjusted EPS x PE Multiple

**Assumptions:** ~430M shares diluted.

### Price Matrix

| FY2026 Adj. EPS → | $6.00 (miss) | $6.50 (weak) | $7.05 (guide mid) | $7.50 (beat) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PE 12x** | $72 | $78 | $85 | $90 |
| **PE 15x** | $90 | $98 | $106 | $113 |
| **PE 19x** | $114 | $124 | **$134** | $143 |
| **PE 25x** | $150 | $163 | $176 | $188 |
| **PE 30x** | $180 | $195 | $212 | $225 |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price (~$115) implies:** ~$7.05 EPS at 16x, or ~$6.50 at 18x. The market is pricing in slightly below guidance at a below-historical multiple. There's modest skepticism baked in.
- **Floor ($72):** Recession EPS ($6.00) at 12x PE. This would require a genuine companion animal revenue decline plus Librela litigation. For context, Elanco trades at 13x today.
- **Upside ($225):** Beat EPS ($7.50) at 30x PE. This requires Librela recovery + vet visit rebound + re-rating to mid-cycle multiples. Extremely unlikely near-term.
- **Most sensitive variable:** PE multiple. ZTS has traded between 25–40x PE for most of its public history. The current 19x is already a deep de-rating. If the market decides 15x is fair (pharma with pipeline risk), the stock goes to $106 on guidance EPS. If historical multiples (25–30x) return, the stock goes to $175–210. **The fundamental question is: is ZTS still a premium compounder (25x+) or has it become a pharma company (15–18x)?**

### Framework 3 Takeaway

ZTS's stress test reveals that the stock's future depends almost entirely on whether the market assigns it a "compounder" multiple or a "pharma" multiple. The business itself generates $2.3B in FCF with 72% gross margins — this is a phenomenal franchise. But the Librela safety issues and vet visit decline have called the "steady compounder" thesis into question. At 19x forward PE, the stock is cheap if the growth story is temporarily impaired. It's fairly valued if 3–5% growth is the permanent new normal.

ZTS is closer to the IDEXX template (high-quality franchise in a cyclical downturn) than the Teva template (structural impairment), but Librela's safety issues add a Teva-like element that didn't exist for IDEXX.

If vet visits continue declining and Librela doesn't stabilize, a further de-rate to 15x ($106) is plausible. A floor of $81–92 seems reasonable unless litigation materializes, which could take the stock to $63–72.

**Key catalysts and timing:**
- **Q1 2026 earnings (May):** Librela sales trajectory — is the decline stabilizing or accelerating?
- **FDA adverse event data (ongoing):** Additional Librela safety reports could further damage prescriber confidence.
- **Numelvi launch metrics (quarterly):** Merck's dermatology competitor — watch prescription share data (Vetcove, veterinary data services).
- **Vet visit data (AVMA/IDEXX quarterly):** Leading indicator for companion animal spend. Any recovery = bullish.
- **Librela litigation (ongoing):** Watch for class-action filings from pet owner groups. Material litigation would add a 10–15% discount.
- **2027 pipeline updates:** New product launches (next-gen parasiticides, diagnostics) could diversify away from Librela dependency.

**Key risks:**
1. **Librela safety / reputation damage** — FDA-documented neurological adverse events. "Dear Veterinarian" warning already issued. Litigation risk.
2. **Declining vet visits** — structural shift in pet owner behavior, especially younger demographics under inflationary pressure.
3. **Numelvi competition** — first real challenger to Apoquel/Cytopoint dermatology franchise since 2013.
4. **Multiple compression risk** — if growth stays at 3–5%, the market may decide 15x PE is fair (vs. historical 30x+).
5. **Pet spending cyclicality** — "humanization of pets" thesis is untested in a genuine recession.

**Key catalysts (bullish):**
1. **Librela safety data stabilization** — if adverse events plateau, prescriber confidence can rebuild over 12–24 months.
2. **International Librela growth** — less safety concern friction in EU/LatAm markets.
3. **$2.3B FCF** — massive cash flow generation allows buybacks, dividends, and pipeline investment.
4. **72% gross margins** — among the highest in pharma. This is a franchise business with enormous pricing power.
5. **Duopoly position** — ZTS + Elanco control the majority of the animal health market. Barriers to entry are high.
6. **Valuation floor** — at 19x PE, ZTS is at its cheapest valuation since IPO. Much of the bad news is priced in.
