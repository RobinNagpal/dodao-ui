## Soleno Therapeutics (SLNO) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$30 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $90.32 / $90.32
**52-week low:** $29.43
**Already down:** ~67% from ATH

SLNO is a commercial-stage rare disease biotech with VYKAT XR (diazoxide choline), the first and only FDA-approved treatment for hyperphagia in Prader-Willi Syndrome (PWS). FDA approved March 2025, launched April 2025. FY2025 revenue $190.4M in just 9 months of sales. 98.6% gross margin. 859 active patients, 630 prescribers. Turned profitable in Q3 2025 — FY2025 net income $20.9M ($0.40/share). Q4 EPS $0.80 beat consensus by 17–31%.

**Business context:** SLNO is a single-product biotech with monopoly pricing on an orphan drug (7-year US exclusivity, 10-year EU pending). PWS affects ~20,000 patients in the US, ~400,000 worldwide. The bull case is a $1.5–2.8B peak revenue opportunity with 98.6% gross margins. The stock crashed 67% because of a Scorpion Capital short seller report (Aug 2025) alleging safety concerns (excess fluid retention, potential heart failure in children) and a subsequent securities class action lawsuit. Management acknowledged the short report caused "lower start forms and increased discontinuations." EMA decision on European approval expected mid-2026.

**Key dynamic:** SLNO is a unique case in this analysis — it's not a "beaten-down company with deteriorating fundamentals." The fundamentals are accelerating (revenue tripling quarter-over-quarter, first-year profitability, monopoly pricing). The 67% crash is driven by a single event: the Scorpion Capital short report + subsequent safety concerns + class action lawsuit. This makes SLNO a pure **safety-signal binary** — either the safety concerns are manageable (and the stock recovers dramatically), or they're real (and the drug faces label changes or worse). There is no "slow decline" scenario for a monopoly orphan drug.

The fluid retention signal proves manageable with labeling guidance (monitor hydration, adjust dosing in pediatric patients). The Scorpion Capital allegations are debunked through independent reviews.

Prescriber confidence returns. Start forms re-accelerate to 100+/month. Active patients grow from 859 to 2,000+ by end of 2026. Revenue hits $500–600M in FY2026 (full year + recovered growth). EPS grows to $3–4/share.

EMA approves VYKAT XR mid-2026, opening the European market (~9,500 patients in 5 major markets). EU launch in late 2026 adds another revenue stream.

The securities lawsuit settles for a modest amount ($10–20M) as the safety data proves the company's original disclosures were adequate.

**Why 25%:** The drug IS working for 859 patients, and the Q4 sequential acceleration ($92M vs. $66M in Q3) shows the safety scare hasn't killed demand — it slowed it temporarily. Orphan drugs with monopoly status and 98.6% gross margins have enormous pricing power. The forward PE of 8.5x implies the market already expects significant earnings growth — if the growth materializes, the stock catches up.

### Scenario 3: Safety Signal Confirmed — Label Changes + Prescriber Hesitation (30% probability)

**What happens:** FDA mandates a REMS (Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy) requiring cardiac echocardiograms before and during VYKAT XR treatment. This adds $2,000+ per patient in monitoring costs and creates prescriber reluctance ("I'll wait for more data before starting new patients").

Discontinuations increase from 10% to 20%. New start forms drop to 50/month (from 100+ pre-Scorpion). Active patients grow only modestly to 1,000–1,100. Revenue is $280–320M in FY2026 — still growing from the $190M base but far below the $500M+ trajectory needed to justify peak sales estimates.

EMA delays approval pending the FDA REMS outcome. The European market is pushed to 2027+. The securities lawsuit gains traction, with plaintiffs securing discovery documents that show the company knew about cardiac signals earlier than disclosed.

Competing treatments (Aardvark ARD-101 Phase 3 data, GLP-1 off-label use in PWS) begin to erode VYKAT's monopoly narrative.

**Why 30%:** Drug safety signals that disrupt launches are common in biotech. The Scorpion Capital report specifically cited hospitalizations for "potential heart failure" in children — if even a small fraction of these are confirmed, FDA will act. The REMS requirement is the likely middle ground between "no action" and "market withdrawal." This outcome is bad for the stock but not fatal for the drug.

### Scenario 4: Drug Withdrawal / Black Box Warning (15% probability)

**What happens:** Post-marketing surveillance reveals a pattern of serious cardiac adverse events in pediatric PWS patients. FDA issues a black box warning or — worst case — requests voluntary market withdrawal. This is the Vioxx/Belviq template: a drug with clear efficacy but unacceptable safety risk in a vulnerable population.

Revenue collapses as prescribers stop writing new prescriptions and patients discontinue. FY2026 revenue stalls at $150–200M, then declines. The company's entire value proposition was VYKAT XR — there is no backup product.

The securities lawsuit becomes a major liability ($200–500M settlement range). Cash from operations evaporates. The company either finds a new indication for DCCR or explores strategic alternatives.

**Why 15%:** Full drug withdrawal is rare but not unprecedented, especially when the safety concern involves children with a vulnerable genetic condition. The Scorpion report alleged "children hospitalized for potential heart failure" — if this pattern is confirmed by independent data, the FDA response will be swift and severe. PWS patients have underlying cardiac and metabolic vulnerabilities that make them a particularly high-risk population for fluid retention drugs.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue × Price/Sales Multiple

**For a commercial-stage orphan drug with 98.6% gross margins, Price/Sales is the most relevant metric (since margins are near-100%, P/S ≈ P/Gross Profit).**

**Assumptions:** ~52M shares diluted. Cash/debt roughly offset.

### Price Matrix (Revenue × P/S)

| FY2026 Revenue → | $200M (stall) | $320M (slow) | $400M (moderate) | $550M (accelerate) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P/S 3x** | $11.54 | $18.46 | $23.08 | $31.73 |
| **P/S 5x** | $19.23 | **$30.77** ← current | $38.46 | $52.88 |
| **P/S 8x** | $30.77 | $49.23 | $61.54 | $84.62 |
| **P/S 12x** | $46.15 | $73.85 | $92.31 | $126.92 |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price ($30) implies:** ~$320M revenue at 5x P/S, or ~$200M at 8x. The market is pricing in either a significant growth slowdown at a low multiple, or very modest revenue at a fair multiple.
- **Bull case ($85–127):** If safety concerns resolve, revenue accelerates to $550M, and the market assigns 8–12x P/S (standard for monopoly orphan drugs), the stock is a 3–4x. This matches analyst PTs of $105–114.
- **Floor ($11.54):** Revenue stalls at $200M with 3x P/S. This requires REMS + prescriber abandonment but NOT drug withdrawal.
- **True floor ($4.50):** Drug withdrawal scenario — only liquidation value of IP/cash remains.
- **Most sensitive variable:** Revenue trajectory (which is driven by safety signal outcomes). Moving from $550M to $200M at constant 5x P/S drops price from $53 to $19 (−64%). The safety binary determines which revenue path plays out.

### Framework 3 Takeaway

SLNO's stress test confirms this is a **pure binary/safety-signal stock.** The matrix shows an extraordinarily wide range ($11.54 to $126.92) because the single variable that matters — safety profile — determines whether this is a $550M revenue monopoly or a $200M cautionary tale. Traditional ratio analysis and multiple comparison are less useful here. Monitor FDA communications, REMS decisions, and post-marketing safety data above all else.
