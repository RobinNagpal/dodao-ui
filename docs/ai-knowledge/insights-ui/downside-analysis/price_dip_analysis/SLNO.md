# Soleno Therapeutics (SLNO) — Price Dip Analysis

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Soleno Therapeutics is a commercial-stage rare disease biotech with a single product: VYKAT XR (diazoxide choline), the first and only FDA-approved treatment for hyperphagia in Prader-Willi Syndrome (PWS). VYKAT XR accounts for 100% of revenue. PWS affects approximately 20,000 patients in the US and approximately 400,000 worldwide. The drug was FDA approved in March 2025 and launched in April 2025, achieving $190.4M in revenue in just 9 months of sales with 98.6% gross margin, 859 active patients, and 630 prescribers. The company turned profitable in Q3 2025, posting FY2025 net income of $20.9M ($0.40/share). Q4 EPS of $0.80 beat consensus by 17-31%.

The stock has fallen approximately 67% from its ATH/52-week high of $90.32 to around $30. The crash was driven entirely by a Scorpion Capital short seller report in August 2025 alleging safety concerns (excess fluid retention, potential heart failure in children) and a subsequent securities class action lawsuit. Management acknowledged the short report caused "lower start forms and increased discontinuations." The revenue trajectory was accelerating dramatically (Q2 $33M to Q3 $66M to Q4 $92M) and the forward PE of 8.5x implies the market expects significant earnings growth if the safety concerns prove manageable. Analyst consensus price target is $105-114 (73-79% above current), with 100% Strong Buy ratings.

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $30, roughly flat — the equally weighted bull and bear scenarios offset each other. This makes SLNO unique in the analysis: it is a pure binary/safety-signal stock with an extraordinarily wide outcome range.

The bull case requires post-marketing data to confirm safety is consistent with Phase 3 trials, prescriber confidence to return, active patients to grow to 2,000+ by end of 2026, and EMA approval mid-2026 opening the European market — producing a price of $54-75. The severe bear case envisions post-marketing surveillance revealing a pattern of serious cardiac adverse events in pediatric PWS patients, FDA issuing a black box warning or requesting voluntary market withdrawal (the Vioxx/Belviq template), and the securities lawsuit becoming a major liability ($200-500M) — producing a price of $4.50-9. There is no "slow decline" scenario for a monopoly orphan drug; the outcome is genuinely binary.

## Competitive Position & Moat

VYKAT XR has monopoly status as the only FDA-approved treatment for hyperphagia in PWS, with 7-year US orphan drug exclusivity and 10-year EU exclusivity pending. The 98.6% gross margin reflects genuine orphan drug pricing power. Revenue tripled quarter-over-quarter during the launch (Q2 $33M to Q3 $66M to Q4 $92M), demonstrating strong physician demand for a condition with no prior treatment. The forward PE of 8.5x on a drug with monopoly positioning and accelerating revenue is extraordinary if the safety profile holds.

However, the moat is entirely contingent on the drug's safety profile — a class action lawsuit plus safety signals equal existential risk for a single-product company. Orphan drug safety events historically result in 80-95% stock declines when they lead to label changes or withdrawal. Competing treatments are emerging: Aardvark ARD-101 has Phase 3 data expected in 2026, and off-label GLP-1 use in PWS could erode the monopoly narrative. The $70.1M cash and $49.9M long-term debt provide limited runway if the safety crisis deepens and revenue stalls. Scorpion Capital will likely publish additional allegations if the stock recovers, creating ongoing headline risk.

The Cassava Sciences (SAVA) precedent is most relevant — short seller report plus safety/data integrity allegations plus class action, resulting in -85% decline over 2 years. The critical difference: SLNO has a drug generating $190M+ revenue with 859 patients on treatment, while SAVA's drug was never approved. If the drug stays on market (even with a label update), the SAVA analogy breaks down and the stock recovers. If safety leads to withdrawal, the Belviq template (-95%) applies.

## What to Watch

FDA post-marketing safety review is the single most important catalyst — any FDA communication (safety alert, REMS requirement, label update) determines the trajectory. EMA decision (mid-2026) would validate the global safety profile if approved; delay or rejection confirms concerns. Quarterly prescription data (start forms, discontinuation rates, active patient count) reveal whether the safety scare is temporary or permanent. The securities class action has a May 5, 2026 lead plaintiff appointment deadline. Aardvark ARD-101 Phase 3 data in 2026 could end VYKAT's monopoly narrative.
