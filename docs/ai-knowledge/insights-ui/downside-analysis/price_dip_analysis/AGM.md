## Federal Agricultural Mortgage / Farmer Mac (AGM) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$158 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $210.78 / $208.25
**52-week low:** $146.69
**Already down:** ~24% from ATH

AGM is a government-sponsored enterprise (GSE) providing a secondary market for agricultural real estate mortgages, rural utility cooperative loans, and broadband infrastructure loans — essentially the "Fannie Mae of farming." FY2025 core EPS $16.66 (+7% YoY), record net effective spread of $383M, outstanding business volume $33.4B (+13% YoY). Dividend raised 7% to $6.00/year (3.45% yield). Strong capital: 13.3% Tier 1, 277 days liquidity.

**Business context:** The core franchise is performing excellently (record spread income, record business volumes, 13% growth). The stock crashed because credit quality is deteriorating: provision for losses spiked to $32.9M in FY2025, nonaccrual loans reached 1.42% of total (up 41% YoY), and the agricultural economy is struggling — only 52% of farm borrowers expected to be profitable in 2025, dropping below 50% in 2026 (lowest since 2020). Nearly 70% of ag lenders are "very worried" about grain profitability. This is a credit cycle bet: if ag credit holds, the stock is cheap at 10x PE. If it deteriorates further, provisions eat into earnings.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Ag Credit Stabilizes — Growth Continues (20% probability)
**What happens:** Farm profitability stabilizes. Provisions normalize to $15–20M. Nonaccruals peak and decline. Business volume continues toward $50B target. Core EPS grows to $18+.
**Price target: ~$195–220 (+23 to +39%)**

### Scenario 2: Muddle Through — Elevated but Manageable Credit (35% probability)
**What happens:** Provisions stay at $30–35M. Nonaccruals stabilize at 1.3–1.5%. Core EPS is flat at $16–17. Dividend maintained and growing. Stock trades at 10x earnings.
**Price target: ~$155–170 (-2 to +8%)**

### Scenario 3: Ag Credit Deterioration Accelerates (30% probability)
**What happens:** Farm profitability drops below 50%. Grain sector stress spreads to livestock/dairy. Provisions spike to $50–60M. Nonaccruals rise to 2.0–2.5%. Core EPS drops to $13–14. Market prices in dividend risk.
**Price target: ~$115–135 (-15 to -27%)**

### Scenario 4: Agricultural Crisis + Trade War (15% probability)
**What happens:** Tariff escalation collapses agricultural exports. Farm bankruptcies spike. Provisions exceed $80M. Nonaccruals reach 3%+. Core EPS drops to $8–10. The GSE's implicit government backing prevents bankruptcy but equity value compresses.
**Price target: ~$80–100 (-37 to -49%)**

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$145** (approximately 8% further downside).

## Framework 4: Precedent Drawdown + Catalyst Mapping

AGM fell 54% during COVID (2020) as agricultural markets panicked, then recovered within 12 months. The current −24% is mild by comparison but driven by a different catalyst (credit cycle vs. liquidity crisis). **Precedent-implied further downside: −5 to −25%** — depends entirely on whether the ag credit cycle is a mild slowdown or a structural crisis.
