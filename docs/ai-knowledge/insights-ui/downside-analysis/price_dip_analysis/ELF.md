## e.l.f. Beauty (ELF) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$59.35 (March 30, 2026)
**52-week high:** $150.99
**52-week low:** $49.40
**Already down:** ~61% from high

ELF posted FY2025 (ending March 2025) revenue of $1.3B (+28.3% YoY), net income of $112M, EPS of $1.92, and gross margin of ~71%. Guided FY2026 net sales growth of 22-23%, with second half growth of 31-33% and Adj EBITDA of $323-326M. Note: ELF has a fiscal year ending in March, so FY2026 = calendar year ending March 2027.

**Business context:** ELF is the #1 value cosmetics brand in the U.S., known for disrupting prestige beauty at drugstore prices. Products are primarily manufactured in China (~75% of sourcing). The company sells through mass retail channels (Target, Walmart, Amazon) and is accelerating international expansion. The stock crashed due to a confluence of factors: 55% China tariff rate hitting 75% of production ($50M annualized cost impact), gross margin compression of 165bps, a PEG of 2.89 that was too rich for tariff headwinds, and a broader consumer discretionary selloff. With ~58M shares, market cap is ~$3.4B and net debt is ~$400M.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Tariffs Stabilize + Market Share Gains Continue (20% probability)

**What happens:** Tariff rates hold at current 55% levels and do not escalate further. ELF successfully passes through price increases to consumers, and the price hikes stick because the brand's value proposition still undercuts prestige competitors by 50-70%. Revenue grows 22-23% as guided. Gross margin recovers toward 70%+ as supply chain adjustments and pricing actions take effect. International expansion (UK, Canada, Italy) contributes incremental growth. Adj EBITDA hits $323-326M guidance. Market rewards execution with EV/EBITDA of 18-20x.

**Why this is only 20%:** The market has already priced in significant tariff damage. For the stock to recover, ELF needs to demonstrate that price increases do not slow volume growth — and with 75% China sourcing, any tariff escalation instantly re-prices the stock downward. Consumer willingness to absorb higher prices on "value" cosmetics is unproven at this scale.

### Scenario 2: Tariffs Absorbed, Growth Slows Modestly (30% probability)

**What happens:** ELF absorbs most tariff costs through a combination of modest price increases (3-5%), supplier negotiations, and limited production shifts. Revenue grows 18-20% — still strong but below the 22-23% guidance. Gross margin stays compressed at 68-69% as tariff costs are only partially offset. Consumer demand holds but growth decelerates as price-sensitive shoppers notice small increases. EV/EBITDA compresses to 15-17x as the market adjusts growth expectations.

**Why this is the most likely scenario at 30%:** This represents the "muddle through" outcome. ELF has proven pricing power historically, but a 55% tariff on 75% of production is unprecedented. Partial absorption is the most realistic middle ground — some margin pain, some price passthrough, some growth deceleration. The stock stabilizes near current levels.

### Scenario 3: Tariff Escalation + Margin Squeeze (35% probability)

**What happens:** Tariffs increase further — either the base rate rises above 55% or additional tariffs are layered on Chinese cosmetics imports specifically. ELF's 75% China concentration becomes an acute liability. The company attempts price increases but consumers resist — at some point, a $14 lipstick loses its "value" positioning when it was $8 two years ago. Revenue grows only 12-15% as volume slows. Gross margin drops to ~65% as tariff costs overwhelm pricing actions. EBITDA margin compresses significantly. The market de-rates ELF to 12-14x EV/EBITDA, treating it more like a challenged consumer brand than a high-growth disruptor.

**Why this is the highest probability scenario at 35%:** The tariff trajectory is escalatory — 55% is already in place and political rhetoric suggests more is coming. ELF has the most concentrated China exposure of any major beauty company. Supply chain diversification takes 18-24 months minimum. The company is stuck with China production for at least the next 4-6 quarters regardless of policy changes.

### Scenario 4: Recession + Supply Chain Disruption (15% probability)

**What happens:** A U.S. recession hits consumer spending. Even "value" cosmetics face pressure as consumers cut discretionary spending entirely — trading down from ELF to no-name brands or simply buying less. China supply chain faces disruption beyond tariffs (geopolitical tensions, shipping delays, quality control issues from rushed production shifts). Revenue is flat to +5%. Gross margin drops below 65%. Debt/EBITDA spikes above 5x, raising covenant concerns. The market treats ELF as a distressed consumer brand at 10x EV/EBITDA.

**Why 15%:** A full recession combined with supply chain disruption is a tail risk but not implausible. ELF's leverage at 3.9x Debt/EBITDA leaves little buffer. The company has never operated through a recession at this scale — its 2017-2018 crash (-73%) happened during a strong economy, purely on growth concerns. A recession would be a new and more severe test.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue (tariff/demand driven) x EBITDA Margin (tariff cost/pricing driven)

**Assumptions:** 16x EV/EBITDA (beauty company mid-cycle), net debt $400M, 58M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = ((Revenue x Margin x 16) - $400M) / 58M shares

### Price Matrix at 16x EV/EBITDA

### Price Matrix at 20x EV/EBITDA (Bull Case)

| FY2026 Revenue → | $1.45B | $1.60B |
|---|---|---|
| **EBITDA Margin 17%** | **$78** | $91 |
| **EBITDA Margin 21%** | $102 | **$109** |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price ($59) implies:** ~$1.50B revenue at ~18% EBITDA margin at 16x — slightly below guidance with moderate margin compression.
- **Floor ($44):** Revenue of $1.45B at 17% margin at 12x. This requires tariff escalation + demand softening + multiple compression. Matches Scenario 3/4 overlap range.
- **Upside ($115):** Full guidance beat + 23% margin at 16x. Requires tariff stabilization + pricing power + execution.
- **Most sensitive variable:** EBITDA margin. At $1.55B revenue, moving margin from 17% to 23% at 16x swings price from $68 to $103 (+51%). Revenue sensitivity is smaller — at 19% margin, moving from $1.45B to $1.65B swings price from $72 to $89 (+24%). **Margin matters more than revenue for ELF, and margin is the variable most directly hit by tariffs.**

### Framework 3 Takeaway

The stress test confirms that ELF's downside is **tariff-margin driven**. The 75% China sourcing concentration means tariff changes flow almost directly to EBITDA margin. At 12x on worst-case fundamentals, the floor is ~$44 — roughly 26% below the current price. The current price of $59 embeds moderate pessimism (below-guidance revenue, compressed margins) but does not fully price in tariff escalation scenarios.

## Framework 4: Precedent Drawdown + Catalyst Mapping

| Precedent | Drawdown | Context | Recovery |
|-----------|----------|---------|----------|
| ELF 2017-2018 | -73% ($34→$9) | Growth stalled, market lost faith in value cosmetics model | 3 years to recover |
| Ulta Beauty (ULTA) 2019 | -50% | Growth concerns, Amazon competition fears | 12 months to recover |
| Estée Lauder (EL) 2022-2025 | -65% | China slowdown, travel retail collapse | Has not recovered |

**Key parallels:**
- ELF's current -61% decline matches the Estée Lauder precedent almost exactly. Both are beauty companies hit by China-related headwinds.
- The ELF 2017-2018 crash (-73%) shows the stock can fall further from here if the growth narrative breaks entirely. That crash happened during a strong economy — a recession would be worse.
- Ulta's -50% decline and 12-month recovery is the optimistic template, but Ulta had no China tariff exposure.
- **Precedent-implied further downside: -5 to -20%** from current levels, with the EL "no recovery" scenario as the risk case.

## Key Catalysts to Watch

- **Q1 FY2026 earnings (July/August 2026):** First read on tariff impact and pricing power. A gross margin miss below 68% validates Scenario 3.
- **U.S.-China tariff policy (ongoing):** Any tariff rate increase above 55% directly hits 75% of ELF's production. Watch for USTR actions on cosmetics specifically.
- **Supply chain diversification progress:** Management has discussed shifting production out of China. Timeline and cost of diversification are critical — 18-24 months minimum.
- **Consumer spending data (monthly):** Retail sales in beauty/cosmetics category. Trade-down from prestige to ELF would be bullish; trade-down from ELF to private label would be bearish.
- **Debt/EBITDA trajectory:** At 3.9x, ELF is already elevated. Crossing 4.5x could trigger rating agency scrutiny and covenant concerns.
- **International expansion metrics:** UK, Canada, and Italy growth rates. International is the margin of safety — less tariff-exposed, growing from a small base.
