## Hesai Group (HSAI) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$20.22 (March 25, 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $30.85 / $30.85
**All-time low:** $3.55 (Aug 2024)
**Already down:** ~34% from ATH

HSAI is the world's #1 automotive LiDAR company by revenue and shipments. FY2025 revenue RMB 3.03B ($433M), up 46% YoY. First full-year GAAP-profitable LiDAR company (net income $62.3M). Shipped 1.6M units in 2025 (tripled YoY). Dual-listed on NASDAQ + Hong Kong (raised $531M from HK listing in Sept 2025). Guided 2–3M unit shipments for 2026.

**Business context:** HSAI is the sole profitable pure-play LiDAR company globally. Primary competitor Luminar filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in Dec 2025. ~80%+ revenue from Chinese OEMs (BYD, Li Auto, Geely). Key products: AT128 (128-channel ADAS), FT120 (solid-state), ET25 (next-gen long-range). 90%+ automated manufacturing. Building Thailand factory (2026–2027) to bypass US tariffs. Pentagon "Chinese Military Companies" blacklist since Oct 2024 (lawsuit ongoing). US is <10% of revenue.

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: L3 Autonomy Accelerates — Guidance Beat (15% probability)

**What happens:** China and Europe both advance L3 regulatory frameworks in 2026, requiring multi-LiDAR configurations (2–3 units per vehicle) for highway autopilot. HSAI's top 2 OEM customers (who've committed 100% LiDAR adoption for all 2026 models) expand orders. Unit shipments hit 3M+ (high end of guide). The Thailand factory becomes operational ahead of schedule, unlocking non-Chinese OEM wins.

Meanwhile, the robotaxi market (Baidu Apollo, Pony.ai, Waymo) scales fleet deployments, pulling through high-ASP Pandar/QT series units. ASP erosion slows because multi-unit vehicle configurations offset per-unit price declines.

Revenue hits $700M+ (60%+ growth). Operating leverage kicks in — gross margin stays above 40% while opex as a % of revenue drops from ~36% to ~28%. GAAP EPS approaches $1.00 (vs. ~$0.46 in FY2025).

**Why only 15%:** L3 regulation is politically complex and has been delayed repeatedly. The stock dropped 14% in a single day on March 25 despite strong earnings — the market is pricing in geopolitical risk, not fundamentals. Pentagon blacklist + US-China tensions make institutional investors cautious regardless of fundamentals.

### Scenario 3: China EV Slowdown + ASP Pressure — Mild Miss (35% probability)

**What happens:** Chinese EV market growth decelerates to single digits as consumer spending weakens and government EV subsidies expire. HSAI's unit shipments hit 2M (low end of guide) but ASP declines faster than expected — 20–25% YoY as RoboSense aggressively undercuts pricing and OEMs demand volume discounts.

Revenue comes in at $500–540M (+15–25% growth, below the 35–40% implied by guidance). The market punishes the growth deceleration heavily because the 44x PE is predicated on sustained 40%+ growth.

Gross margin compresses to 36–38% as ASP erosion outpaces manufacturing cost reductions. Operating margin stays thin at 3–5%. The Pentagon blacklist overhang intensifies as a new US administration potentially adds restrictions on Chinese LiDAR in US autonomous vehicles.

HSAI announces more SBC to retain talent, diluting shareholders by 3–4% on top of existing 8–10% SBC burden. The stock re-rates from a "growth" premium to a "China tech" discount.

**Why 35% (most likely):** China EV growth is already showing signs of saturation in tier-1 cities. LiDAR ASP erosion is structural — the technology follows a consumer electronics cost curve. RoboSense is a credible competitor with similar technology. Geopolitical risk is the constant overhang that prevents multiple expansion regardless of fundamentals.

### Scenario 4: Geopolitical Escalation + LiDAR TAM Collapse (20% probability)

**What happens:** US-China tensions escalate significantly. The US bans Chinese-made LiDAR sensors in autonomous vehicles sold in Western markets (similar to the proposed ban on Chinese software in connected vehicles). The Pentagon blacklist is expanded, and major US index funds are pressured to divest from Chinese military-linked companies.

Simultaneously, Tesla's pure-vision FSD achieves L3 certification in China, proving that cameras alone can handle autonomous driving. Several Chinese OEMs (HSAI's core customers) begin offering "camera-only" ADAS options at lower price points. LiDAR becomes optional rather than standard equipment.

The Thailand factory (designed to circumvent tariffs) becomes less relevant because the ban targets Chinese-owned companies, not just Chinese-manufactured products. HSAI's addressable market effectively shrinks to China + non-Western markets.

Revenue stalls at $450–500M (+5–15%). The LiDAR TAM thesis (that every autonomous vehicle needs LiDAR) gets questioned. The stock re-rates from a growth company to a mature Chinese tech company at a deep discount.

Precedent: Luminar's Chapter 11 bankruptcy (Dec 2025) showed that LiDAR companies with less differentiation and no profitability go to zero. HSAI is profitable, but the question shifts from "will they be profitable?" to "how big is the TAM?"

**Why 20%:** A full Western ban on Chinese LiDAR is a real possibility (the connected vehicle software ban is the template). Tesla's vision-only approach has been steadily improving. If both happen — geopolitical exclusion + TAM shrinkage — the stock reprices not on earnings quality but on terminal value. The net cash of $5.87/share provides a hard floor — HSAI won't go bankrupt — but the equity value above net cash could compress dramatically.

## Framework 3: Stress Test / Sensitivity Matrix

**Key variables:** FY2026 Revenue (unit shipments × ASP) × Gross Margin (ASP erosion vs. manufacturing scale)

**Assumptions:** 6x EV/Revenue (high-growth LiDAR pure-play), net cash $917M, 136.4M shares diluted.

**Formula:** Price = ((Revenue × EV/Rev multiple) + Net Cash) / 136.4M shares

### Price Matrix at Various EV/Revenue Multiples

| FY2026 Revenue → | $450M (stall) | $540M (miss) | $620M (low guide) | $700M (beat) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EV/Rev 2.0x** | $13.30 | $14.60 | $15.80 | $16.95 |
| **EV/Rev 3.0x** | $16.60 | $18.60 | $20.40 | **$22.20** |
| **EV/Rev 4.3x** | $20.90 | $23.70 | $26.30 | $28.90 |
| **EV/Rev 6.0x** | $26.60 | $30.50 | $34.10 | **$37.70** |

**Alternative view — EV/EBITDA approach (assumes 10% EBITDA margin at scale):**

| FY2026 EBITDA → | $45M (stall) | $54M (miss) | $62M (guide) | $70M (beat) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EV/EBITDA 20x** | $13.30 | $14.60 | $15.80 | $16.95 |
| **EV/EBITDA 30x** | $16.50 | $18.60 | **$20.30** ← current | $22.20 |
| **EV/EBITDA 40x** | $19.80 | $22.60 | $24.80 | $27.40 |

**What the matrix reveals:**
- **Current price ($20.22) implies:** ~$540M revenue at 4.3x EV/Rev, or $62M EBITDA at 30x. Both are mid-guide scenarios with today's valuation multiples.
- **Floor ($13.30):** Revenue stalls at $450M + multiple compresses to 2x EV/Revenue. This requires geopolitical exclusion + TAM collapse. Net cash ($5.87/share) provides a sub-floor even below the matrix.
- **True floor (~$7–8):** If you strip out net cash and assign 1x EV/Revenue to a $450M stalling business, you get ~$7. This requires near-total loss of growth narrative.
- **Most sensitive variable:** EV/Revenue multiple. Moving from 4.3x to 2.0x at constant revenue ($620M) drops price from $26.30 to $15.80 (−40%). Moving revenue from $700M to $450M at constant multiple (4.3x) drops price from $28.90 to $20.90 (−28%). **Multiple matters more than revenue for HSAI — geopolitical risk is the valuation driver, not fundamentals.**

### Framework 3 Takeaway

HSAI's stress test reveals a **geopolitical/multiple-driven stock, not a fundamental one.** The business is performing well (46% growth, first-ever profitability, tripling shipments), but the stock price is controlled by what multiple the market assigns — and that multiple is driven by Pentagon blacklist risk, US-China tensions, and LiDAR TAM narrative. The net cash floor of $5.87/share (29% of current price) is the strongest downside protection of any stock in this analysis.
