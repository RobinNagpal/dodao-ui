## Progressive Corporation (PGR) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$199 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $289.88 / $289.88
**52-week low:** $197.92
**Already down:** ~31% from ATH

PGR is the third-largest US auto insurer. FY2025 combined ratio 87.4% (vs. 96% target — unsustainably good). NPW grew ~16% annually 2021–2025, now decelerating to +4–5%. EPS $19.29 (FY2025), consensus FY2026 ~$15.40 (−20% decline expected). ROE of 34–40%. Ultra-conservative balance sheet (0.2x D/E).

**Business context:** PGR's decline is earnings normalization, not business deterioration. The combined ratio of 87.4% was driven by lower accident frequency and favorable reserve development — both cyclical tailwinds that are mean-reverting. As the hard market cycle matures, competitors catch up on rate adequacy, reducing PGR's relative pricing advantage. A $950M Florida charge in Q3 2025 signaled that regulatory risk is real. The stock is transitioning from "earnings upgrade cycle" to "earnings stabilization at a high level."

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Hard Market Extends — CR Stays Sub-90% (15% probability)
**What happens:** Accident frequency stays low. Competitors don't catch up on pricing. PGR maintains 88–90% combined ratio. EPS stays at $17+. The decline was overdone.
**Price target: ~$250–275 (+26 to +38%)**

### Scenario 2: Orderly Normalization — CR to 92–94% (35% probability)
**What happens:** Combined ratio normalizes gradually over 2 years. EPS declines to $14–16 (FY2026) then stabilizes. PGR trades at 13–15x normalized earnings. Market share gains continue.
**Price target: ~$195–220 (-2 to +11%)**

### Scenario 3: Soft Market Acceleration — CR to 96%+ (30% probability)
**What happens:** Competitors aggressively cut prices. Claims inflation accelerates (repair costs, medical, litigation). Combined ratio returns to 96% target or slightly above. EPS drops to $10–12. The $950M Florida precedent repeats in other states.
**Price target: ~$140–165 (-17 to -30%)**

### Scenario 4: Catastrophe Year + Regulatory Squeeze (20% probability)
**What happens:** Major hurricane season + regulatory profit caps expanding beyond Florida. Combined ratio spikes to 100%+ for a year. EPS drops to $6–8. The stock reprices as a cyclical insurance company rather than a growth compounder.
**Price target: ~$100–125 (-37 to -50%)**

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$178** (approximately 11% further downside).

## Framework 4: Precedent Drawdown + Catalyst Mapping

PGR typically pulls back 20–35% when the insurance market shifts from hard to soft, then recovers as discipline wins out over 2–3 years. The current −31% is at the upper end of typical insurance cycle corrections. **Precedent-implied further downside: −5 to −20%.** PGR's best-in-class underwriting discipline (consistent sub-96% over decades) means it recovers faster than peers.
