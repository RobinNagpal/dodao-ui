# Blue Owl Capital Corporation (OBDC) — Price Dip Analysis

# Section 1: Analysis

## What This Company Does & Why the Stock Dropped

Blue Owl Capital Corporation is an externally managed BDC (Business Development Company) with an $18.6B portfolio across 232 companies, managed by Blue Owl Capital (OWL). Senior secured first lien loans account for roughly 80% of the portfolio, second lien/mezzanine roughly 15%, and equity co-investments roughly 5%. OBDC is the #2 BDC by assets ($17.2B), and the Blue Owl platform enables $1B+ unitranches that smaller BDCs cannot offer.

OBDC posted Q4 2025 adjusted NII of $0.36/share (below the $0.37 base dividend), NAV of $14.81/share (down from $15.26, -2.9% YoY). Non-accruals improved to 2.3%/1.1% (cost/fair value) from a 2.7%/1.3% peak. The supplemental dividend was eliminated. The stock trades at a 22-26% discount to NAV with a 13.5% dividend yield.

The stock has fallen approximately 27% from its 52-week high of $15.36 to around $11.25 (and 33% from ATH of $16.70). The decline was driven by NII compression from Fed rate cuts (portfolio yield down 100-150 bps), supplemental dividend elimination, adjusted NII undercovering the base dividend, NAV eroding for 4 consecutive quarters, and the OBDC II merger fiasco — announced at 0.80x NAV, terminated after 14 days following FT exposure and investor backlash, triggering 6 class-action lawsuits. The 0.75x P/NAV discount is the widest among major BDC peers (ARCC and MAIN trade near NAV). However, Moody's upgraded OBDC to Baa2, $1.4B in assets were sold at 99.8% of par (validating portfolio quality), and a $300M buyback is underway buying at a 22-26% discount (massively accretive).

## How Much Further Can It Fall?

The probability-weighted expected price is approximately $10.30, implying roughly 8% further downside from $11.25. The market is pricing in a 10-15% base dividend cut. The key question is binary: does the base dividend get cut? If it does, income investors exit and the stock drops to $9.50-10.20. If it does not and NII stabilizes, the discount narrows and the stock recovers toward $13-14.

The bull case requires the Fed to pause rate cuts, NII to stabilize at $0.36-0.38, non-accruals to peak and decline, and the OBDC II overhang to fade — producing a discount narrowing from 25% to 10-15% and a price of $13-14. The severe bear case envisions a private credit reckoning with software/SaaS portfolio defaults spiking, non-accruals to 4%+, NAV dropping to $12.50-13.50, and a 25-30% dividend cut — producing a price of $7.50-9.00. The 13.5% yield provides significant cushion as income investors step in at higher yields.

## Competitive Position & Moat

OBDC is the #2 BDC by assets with $17.2B, and the Blue Owl platform provides a structural advantage enabling $1B+ unitranches that smaller competitors cannot offer. The Moody's upgrade to Baa2 and the $1.4B in asset sales at 99.8% of par validate portfolio quality. The $300M buyback at 0.75x P/NAV is massively accretive to remaining shareholders.

However, spread compression threatens the entire private credit industry as every alternative asset manager scales into direct lending. The OBDC II merger fiasco damaged governance credibility and triggered 6 class-action lawsuits — the 0.75x P/NAV discount (widest among major peers) reflects this trust deficit. NII compression from rate cuts is structural for all floating-rate BDCs, not specific to OBDC. The external management structure creates inherent conflicts (fees flow to Blue Owl regardless of stock performance).

Non-accrual trends determine whether the portfolio quality thesis holds — improvement from the 2.7%/1.3% peak to 2.3%/1.1% is encouraging but needs to continue. The $300M buyback pace signals management's conviction about the discount.
