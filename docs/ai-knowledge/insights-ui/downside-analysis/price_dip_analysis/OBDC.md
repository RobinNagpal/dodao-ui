## Blue Owl Capital Corporation (OBDC) — Price Dip Analysis

**Current price:** ~$11.25 (March 2026)
**52-week high / ATH:** $15.36 / $16.70 (late 2024)
**52-week low:** $10.71
**Already down:** ~33% from ATH, ~27% from 52-week high

OBDC is an externally managed BDC with an $18.6B portfolio across 232 companies, managed by Blue Owl Capital (OWL). Q4 2025 adjusted NII $0.36/share (below $0.37 base dividend), NAV $14.81 (down from $15.26, −2.9% YoY). Non-accruals improved to 2.3%/1.1% (cost/FV) from 2.7%/1.3% peak. Supplemental dividend eliminated. 13.5% dividend yield. Trading at 22–26% discount to NAV.

**Business context:** OBDC faces a convergence of negatives: (1) NII compression from Fed rate cuts (portfolio yield down 100–150 bps), (2) supplemental dividend gone, (3) adjusted NII undercovering the base dividend, (4) NAV eroding 4 consecutive quarters, (5) the OBDC II merger fiasco — announced at 0.80x NAV, terminated after 14 days following FT exposure and investor backlash, triggering 6 class-action lawsuits. The market is pricing in a 10–15% base dividend cut. The 0.75x P/NAV discount is the widest among major BDC peers (ARCC/MAIN trade near NAV). However, Moody's upgraded OBDC to Baa2, $1.4B in assets sold at 99.8% of par (validating portfolio quality), and a $300M buyback is underway (buying at 22–26% discount = massively accretive).

## Framework 1: Scenario-Based Ratio Modeling

### Scenario 1: Credit Stabilizes + NII Bottoms (15% probability)
**What happens:** Fed pauses rate cuts. NII stabilizes at $0.36–0.38. Non-accruals peak and decline. NAV stabilizes. The OBDC II overhang fades. Buyback at 0.75x P/NAV is massively accretive. Discount narrows from 25% to 10–15%.
**Price target: ~$13.00–14.00 (+16 to +24%)**

### Scenario 2: Muddle Through — Dividend Maintained, Discount Persists (30% probability)
**What happens:** Adjusted NII is $0.35–0.37, roughly covering the dividend. NAV drifts lower to $14.50–14.70. Market remains skeptical after governance fiasco. Discount stays at 20–25%. Total return comes from 13.5% yield.
**Price target: ~$10.75–11.50 (-4 to +2%)**

### Scenario 3: Dividend Cut + Further NAV Erosion (35% probability)
**What happens:** NII compresses to $0.32–0.34 as rates fall further. Base dividend cut 10–15% (from $0.37 to $0.32–0.33). Non-accruals rise to 2.5–3.0% at cost. NAV drops to $14.00–14.40. Income investors exit on the cut. Discount widens to 30%.
**Price target: ~$9.50–10.20 (-9 to -16%)**

### Scenario 4: Credit Crisis + Software Defaults Spike (20% probability)
**What happens:** Private credit broadly faces a reckoning. Software/SaaS portfolio companies (heavy OBDC exposure) see wave of defaults. Non-accruals spike to 4%+. NAV drops to $12.50–13.50. Dividend cut 25–30%. The BDC model is questioned. Discount widens to 35–40%.
**Price target: ~$7.50–9.00 (-20 to -33%)**

**Probability-weighted expected price: ~$10.30** (approximately 8% further downside).
