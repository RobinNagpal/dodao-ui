## AGM-specific checklist

Use this as a practical monitoring checklist. These are **monitoring rules**, not company guidance.

**1) Provision for losses / total credit expense**
Why it applies: AGM is a lender-guarantor. When credit cost jumps, EPS and sentiment both get hit fast.
Safe: provision **below $8M** in a quarter and total credit expense **below 10% of revenue**.
Watch: **$8M-$10M** or **10%-12% of revenue**.
Red: **above $10M** or **above 12% of revenue**.
AGM signal: Q4 2025 was **red** at **$16.0M provision** and about **16.0% of revenue**.
Expected pricing impact: **High**. This is the type of signal that can cause a **double-digit one-day drop**, and AGM already showed that on Feb. 20.

**2) Nonaccrual loans**
Why it applies: for AGM, this is one of the clearest signs that credit problems are moving from “watch” to “real.”
Safe: **below 1.0% of total loans**.
Watch: **1.0%-1.3%**.
Red: **above 1.3%** or rising **15%+ YoY**.
AGM signal: year-end 2025 was about **1.42%** of total loans, and nonaccrual loans were up about **41% YoY**.
Expected pricing impact: **High** if it rises alongside provisions; **medium** if it rises alone.

**3) Substandard assets**
Why it applies: this is the best “before the charge-off” warning signal for AGM. Management itself says sustained increases here likely mean higher future provisions.
Safe: Agricultural Finance **below 3.3%**; Infrastructure Finance **below 1.0%**.
Watch: Ag Finance **3.3%-4.0%**; Infrastructure **1.0%-1.5%**.
Red: above those levels for **2 quarters**.
AGM signal: Ag Finance was **3.52%** and Infrastructure Finance was **0.96%** at year-end 2025.
Expected pricing impact: **Medium**, but it becomes **high** if provisions start following it.

**4) 90-day delinquencies**
Why it applies: this is the simplest headline credit metric to track each quarter.
Safe: **below 0.40%** of outstanding volume.
Watch: **0.40%-0.50%**.
Red: **above 0.50%**.
AGM signal: the Q3 2025 presentation showed **0.57%**, and year-end 2025 was **0.40%**.
Expected pricing impact: **Medium**. By itself this usually does not crash the stock, but it tells you risk is building.

**5) Capital + liquidity**
Why it applies: AGM depends on confidence in a strong funding and capital position.
Safe: Tier 1 capital **above 13%** and liquidity **well above 150 days**.
Watch: Tier 1 **12%-13%** or liquidity **120-150 days**.
Red: Tier 1 **below 12%** or liquidity drifting near the **90-day** regulatory floor.
AGM signal: this was **not** the problem; Tier 1 was **13.3%** and liquidity was **277 days**.
Expected pricing impact: **Very high** if it turns weak, but right now this is actually the part of the thesis that says the business is still sound.
