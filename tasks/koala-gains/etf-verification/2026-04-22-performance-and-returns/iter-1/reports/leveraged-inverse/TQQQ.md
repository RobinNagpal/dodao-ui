# ETF Report — NASDAQ/TQQQ

- **Group:** Leveraged & Inverse Trading (`leveraged-inverse`)
- **Category:** Trading--Leveraged Equity

## Performance & Returns

### Summary

The performance profile of this 3x leveraged ETF is Mixed, reflecting strong absolute gains during secular bull markets offset by recent momentum breakdowns. The fund boasts a 1-year return of `114.53%` and a 3-year return of `237.11%`, demonstrating its upside capture power. However, a 6-month reversal of `-16.12%` highlights the reality of compounding decay when trend strength falters. Ultimately, this is a tactical trading tool that successfully executes its daily mandate but carries extreme path-dependent risk.

### Overall analysis

Recent returns reveal a broad-based pullback that is weighing heavily on short-term performance. The ETF has posted a 1-month return of `-11.96%` and sits in negative territory for the year with a YTD return of `-16.84%`. During this same window, the benchmark NASDAQ 100 Index managed a positive `3.84%` gain. This divergence underscores how quickly the fund's 3x daily leverage amplifies downside volatility during corrective periods.

Looking at the longer-term record, the effects of continuous bull market compounding become visible. The fund has generated a 10-year CAGR of `36.02%`, substantially outpacing standard equity benchmarks. However, the 5-year CAGR sits lower at `12.52%`, narrowly edging out the index's 5-year annualized gain of `11.73%`. This compression over the medium term illustrates how periodic bear markets disproportionately erase multi-year gains due to volatility drag.

The technical and momentum position is entrenched in a downtrend. At a stock price of `$43.95`, the fund trades below its 50-day moving average by `-9.75%`. Momentum signals confirm this weakness, with the daily RSI sitting at `46.06`. Additionally, the fund is trading `-27.87%` below its all-time high set in late 2025, indicating that the prevailing trend is currently tilted toward the downside.

The core strength of this ETF is its capacity to turbocharge returns during sustained rallies, as evidenced by its 2023 calendar-year surge of `198.26%`. The primary risk is its inherent structural decay; an example occurred in 2022, when the fund suffered a `-79.08%` drop. Supported by a highly elevated beta of `3.53`, the fund performs as designed. Overall, this ETF's performance profile looks mixed because its strong long-term absolute growth is highly dependent on timing and offset by severe drawdowns during trend reversals.

_While the fund deviates from a 3x multiple of its benchmark over annual periods, this drift is the mathematical reality of its daily reset structure rather than a tracking failure._

As a leveraged ETF, the fund is designed to track 3x the daily return of the NASDAQ 100 Index, making long-term benchmark tracking intentionally unaligned. In 2024, the index returned `24.09%`, while this ETF delivered `58.23%`. Similarly, the fund recorded a `34.37%` gain for the full year of 2025. Because daily rebalancing unlinks long-term performance from a static 3x multiple of the underlying index, the annual divergence is structural and expected. The fund successfully replicates its target on a daily basis.
