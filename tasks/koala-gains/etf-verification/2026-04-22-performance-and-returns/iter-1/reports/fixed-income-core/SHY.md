# ETF Report — NASDAQ/SHY

- **Group:** Fixed Income — Core & Government (`fixed-income-core`)
- **Category:** Short Government

## Performance & Returns

### Summary

The performance profile for the iShares 1-3 Year Treasury Bond ETF is Mixed in absolute return terms but highly effective relative to its defensive mandate. While long-term compounding is inherently low, the fund tracks its benchmark with excellent precision, trailing the index by just `0.05` percentage points over the past three years. Although it currently lags the US Fund Short Government category median over the trailing 1-year period, its primary function as a low-volatility income generator remains completely intact. Overall, for retail investors, the ETF successfully delivers a stable, short-duration Treasury exposure with a reliable current yield.

### Overall analysis

The recent performance snapshot of this short-duration Treasury ETF highlights steady, incremental gains characteristic of its low-risk mandate. Over the trailing 1-month and year-to-date periods, the fund posted NAV returns of `0.52%` and `0.48%`, respectively. However, looking at the 1-year mark, the fund's `3.43%` NAV return trails both its stated ICE BofA US Treasury Bond (1-3 Y) index's `3.74%` gain and the broader US Fund Short Government category average of `4.26%`. This recent underperformance relative to peers suggests that competing funds within the category may be taking slightly more credit or duration risk to boost short-term yield. Meanwhile, the latest price action shows a mild pullback, with the 1-month price change sitting at `-0.99%`, indicating that recent total returns are being buoyed almost entirely by income distributions rather than capital appreciation.

Over longer horizons, the performance picture aligns perfectly with a passive, risk-free bond mandate. The fund's 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year CAGRs sit at `3.90%`, `1.71%`, and `1.65%`, respectively. While these figures are modest compared to equities or high-yield credit, they accurately reflect the structurally low interest rate environment that dominated much of the past decade. Crucially, the fund replicates its index exceptionally well over time; its 10-year annualized NAV return of `1.67%` is just slightly behind the index's `1.72%`. Its percentile ranking among peers has remained highly stable over extended windows, settling into the `45th` percentile out of 62 funds over 5 years and the `52nd` percentile out of 55 funds over 10 years. Because the Short Government category contains active managers attempting to optimize the yield curve, a low-cost passive tracker is expected to land squarely near the median, making this an entirely successful long-term track record.

From a technical and momentum standpoint, the ETF is currently trading in neutral-to-bearish territory, with a share price of `82.3`. This places it slightly below both its 50-day moving average of `82.743` and its 200-day moving average of `82.785`. The daily RSI of `42.382` indicates a mild downward drift but lacks deep oversold conviction. Furthermore, the fund is resting just `0.11%` above its 52-week low of `82.21`, while remaining `-1.08%` below its 52-week high of `83.2`. It is important to emphasize that for short-duration Treasury ETFs, traditional technical indicators like moving averages and RSI are largely noise. Price movements in this asset class are dictated almost entirely by macroeconomic factors—specifically Federal Reserve interest rate policy and inflation expectations—rather than underlying equity-like momentum or chart patterns.

The core strengths of this ETF lie in its defensive posturing and reliable tracking precision. It demonstrated exceptional resilience by gaining `4.15%` during the volatile 2023 calendar year, and currently offers a solid defensive income floor with a `3.70%` SEC yield. On the downside, its trailing 1-year category ranking has slipped to the `79th` percentile (out of 78 funds), and its long-term capital growth is fundamentally restricted by the mechanics of the 1-3 year Treasury segment. Overall, this ETF's performance profile looks mixed in absolute terms because of its low structural ceiling, but it remains a highly effective portfolio tool that reliably delivers stable, low-volatility income without any structural surprises.

### Factor results

#### `long_term_cagr` — Pass

_The fund’s long-term compounding reflects the historically low-yield environment for short-term Treasuries, rather than any structural fund weakness._

The ETF's 10-year CAGR sits at `1.65%`, with a 15-year CAGR of `1.35%` and a 20-year CAGR of `2.00%`. For a short-duration government bond fund, long-term capital appreciation is entirely dependent on prevailing interest rates rather than corporate earnings growth. These modest annualized returns are exactly consistent with the asset class's historical yields over the zero-interest-rate periods that dominated the 2010s. Because the ETF faithfully tracks its risk-free mandate, this low absolute compounding is the expected outcome of its strategy rather than a performance failure.

#### `benchmark_tracking` — Pass

_The fund tracks its stated ICE BofA US Treasury Bond (1-3 Y) index with excellent precision across nearly all measured periods._

As a passive index tracker, the fund's primary objective is to replicate its benchmark before fees. Over the past 3 years, the fund posted an annualized NAV return of `3.98%` against the index's `4.03%`, representing a minimal tracking difference of just `0.05` percentage points. Similarly, over 10 years, its `1.67%` annualized return narrowly trails the index's `1.72%`. Since this tracking error remains well within the strict `0.5` percentage point tolerance expected for core fixed-income funds, the ETF efficiently fulfills its replication mandate with no problematic drag.

#### `category_peer_standing` — Pass

_The fund maintains a solid, middle-of-the-pack standing among its Short Government category peers over extended periods._

The ETF's percentile ranks show it hovering near the median over longer horizons, placing in the `45th` percentile out of 62 funds over 5 years and the `52nd` percentile out of 55 funds over 10 years. In the short term, its standing has weakened, dropping to the `79th` percentile out of 78 funds over the trailing 1-year period. However, in a category that includes active managers who can tactically adjust duration to chase yield, achieving median long-term performance is the standard and intended outcome for a strictly passive 1-3 year Treasury tracker.

#### `income_vs_price_return` — Pass

_Nearly all of the fund's total return is generated through steady income distributions, successfully overriding long-term price decay._

Over the past 10 years, the ETF's price has declined by `-3.03%`, yet the fund has delivered a positive 10-year total return of `17.82%`. This confirms that dividend distributions—not capital appreciation—are the sole engine of its performance. The fund boasts 25 years of continuous dividend payments, currently offering an SEC yield of `3.70%` and a TTM yield of `3.75%`. For a short-term Treasury ETF, this pattern of flat-to-negative price movement offset by monthly income distributions is exactly how the product is engineered to function.

#### `rate_environment_resilience` — Pass

_The fund’s short duration protects it from severe drawdowns during rate-hiking cycles, consistently outperforming broader bond benchmarks in high-stress years._

Short-term Treasury funds are designed to strictly limit interest-rate sensitivity. During the aggressive rate-hiking environment of 2022, the ETF posted a NAV loss of `-3.90%`. Crucially, this drawdown was notably less severe than the US Fund Short Government category average loss of `-5.15%` and the index drop of `-5.42%`. Conversely, in falling-rate environments like 2019 and 2020, it successfully generated positive returns of `3.42%` and `3.01%`, respectively. This proves the ETF effectively mitigates duration risk when rates spike while still capturing moderate upside when rates fall.
