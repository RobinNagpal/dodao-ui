# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/MUB

- **Group:** Municipal Bonds (`muni`)
- **Category:** Muni National Interm

## Performance & Returns

### Summary

The performance profile is **Strong** in terms of fulfilling its passive mandate, delivering a `7.32%` 1-year NAV return that slightly edged out its benchmark. Absolute long-term growth is inherently modest in this asset class, but the fund tracks the ICE AMT-Free US National Municipal index tightly, consistently staying within `0.40` percentage points of the benchmark across all trailing periods. Overall, MUB is a reliable, tax-exempt income tool that executes exactly what it is designed to do, though retail investors should expect stability and distribution payouts rather than capital appreciation.

### Overall analysis

Over the most recent periods, the ETF shows steady, positive performance. It posted a `1.19%` 1-month NAV return and a `1.65%` 6-month price gain, culminating in a year-to-date NAV increase of `1.05%`. Looking over the past year, the fund tracked its Muni National Interm category closely and slightly outperformed the ICE AMT-Free US National Municipal index, generating a return that beat the `7.11%` benchmark figure. The latest upside reflects broad bond market stabilization rather than unique fund momentum.

Zooming out, the absolute numbers reflect the lower-yielding environments of the past decade. The fund achieved trailing NAV returns of `3.16%` over 3 years, `0.90%` over 5 years, and `2.03%` over 10 years. Because the ETF operates as a passive index tracker, it predictably trails the `0.99%` 5-year return of the category average, which includes active managers who can take on additional credit risk. Still, its 10-year percentile rank sits at `48` out of `176` funds, demonstrating that it successfully holds the median line net of fees compared to its peers. Its tracking gap against the benchmark's `2.17%` 10-year return is negligible.

In terms of market positioning, the current share price of `$106.43` sits wedged between its 50-day moving average of `107.45` and its 200-day moving average of `106.30`, indicating a neutral trend. The daily RSI reads `44.65`, confirming momentum is neither overbought nor oversold. Shares trade `-9.92%` below their all-time high and just `-2.36%` off the 52-week peak. However, technical signals and momentum metrics carry minimal predictive weight in the municipal bond asset class, where price action is dictated almost entirely by macroeconomic interest rate shifts.

The main strength of this ETF is its tax-advantaged income efficiency. It sports an SEC yield of `3.39%`, which roughly equals a tax-equivalent yield of `4.98%` for an investor in the `32%` federal tax bracket. A secondary strength is its precise indexing, minimizing tracking error drag. The primary risk is structural duration sensitivity; when rates spiked, the fund suffered a `-7.35%` price drop in 2022. Overall, this ETF's performance profile looks strong because it executes its passive municipal tracking mandate highly efficiently, even if absolute total returns are heavily constrained by bond market mechanics.

### Factor results

#### `long_term_cagr` — Pass

_The fund delivers very modest long-term compounding, which is entirely typical for high-quality municipal bond portfolios._

The ETF has generated a 5-year CAGR of `0.91%` and a 10-year CAGR of `1.99%`. While a 15-year CAGR of `3.07%` looks slightly better, these low absolute growth rates reflect the yield-focused nature of the asset class rather than any fund-specific failure. Since the ETF passively tracks its benchmark within `0.35` percentage points across these longer horizons, it is functioning properly. Capital appreciation is not the goal here, and the underlying compounding engine relies heavily on steady tax-free distributions.

#### `returns_consistency` — Pass

_The fund's year-to-year returns are generally stable, though punctuated by occasional rate-driven drawdowns._

Annual returns usually hover in the low-to-mid single digits, such as `5.86%` in 2023, `1.11%` in 2024, and `3.69%` in 2025. The major exception was a stark `-7.50%` NAV drop in 2022 during the global rate-hiking cycle. The fund's percentile ranking in its category has fluctuated, moving from the `31st` percentile in 2020 down to the `81st` in 2024 before settling at `52nd` year-to-date. This middle-of-the-pack consistency is exactly what investors should expect from a passive index tracker operating alongside active peers.

#### `category_peer_standing` — Pass

_MUB consistently lands near the median of its category, a successful outcome for a passive municipal tracker._

Over the 5-year trailing period, the fund ranks `56th` among `233` investments in its peer group. While it occasionally dips into the bottom quartile (ranking in the `79th` percentile over a 3-year window), passive bond ETFs routinely trail the median active manager before taxes due to structural constraints. Because the fund consistently matches its stated index net of its low `0.05%` expense ratio, its positioning indicates efficient index tracking rather than managerial underperformance.

#### `rate_environment_resilience` — Pass

_The fund behaves exactly as an intermediate-duration bond portfolio should, losing ground when rates spike and recovering when they fall._

In a falling-rate environment like 2019, MUB posted a robust `7.28%` NAV return. Conversely, aggressive rate hikes naturally pressure its holdings. Crucially, its drawdown in 2022 was an asset-class event rather than a fund-specific vulnerability; the category average dropped by `-8.23%` that same year, meaning MUB actually proved slightly more resilient than its typical active peer. Its duration profile balances decent yield capture with moderate interest rate risk.

#### `income_vs_price_return` — Pass

_Almost all of the fund's total return is driven by its tax-exempt distributions rather than price appreciation._

Over the past 10 years, the ETF's share price has declined by `-4.73%`, yet the fund still achieved a positive total return because of its steady payouts. MUB features a TTM yield of `3.19%`, translating to a tax-equivalent yield of approximately `4.69%` assuming a `32%` federal tax rate. The fund has paid dividends for `20` consecutive years, effectively offsetting gradual NAV erosion and fulfilling its primary mandate of delivering reliable income.
