# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/HYG

- **Group:** Fixed Income — Credit & Income (`fixed-income-credit`)
- **Category:** High Yield Bond

## Performance & Returns

### Summary

HYG presents a mixed but mandate-aligned performance profile for retail investors seeking broad high-yield credit exposure. The fund delivers a robust SEC yield of `6.59%` and a solid trailing 1-year NAV return of `10.01%`, slightly outpacing the category median. However, due to its passive structure in an active-heavy category, it persistently trails its underlying index by roughly `0.40` to `0.90` percentage points across longer periods. Overall, it serves as a functional, yield-generating tracking tool despite standard structural lags.

### Overall analysis

Looking at recent performance, HYG has participated fully in the latest credit rally. The ETF posted a trailing 1-year NAV return of `10.01%`, edging past the US Fund High Yield Bond category median of `9.89%` but trailing its benchmark, the iBoxx USD Liquid High Yield Index, which returned `10.39%`. Short-term returns show continued stability with a 3-month NAV gain of `0.90%` and a YTD return of `1.43%`. This indicates the recent upward momentum is broad-based across the credit spectrum rather than isolated noise.

Over longer horizons, the tracking drag inherent in passive high-yield investing becomes more apparent. HYG posted an annualized 5-year NAV return of `3.92%` and a 10-year return of `5.19%`. Across these same periods, the index returned `4.47%` and `6.12%`, respectively. This performance gap of `0.55` to `0.93` percentage points is expected given the transaction costs associated with less liquid corporate credit. Compared to its peers, the fund ranks in the 61st percentile out of 523 funds over 5 years, and 60th out of 423 funds over 10 years. Because the category is heavily populated by active managers, landing near the median is a standard outcome for a passive index tracker.

Technically, the fund is hovering in neutral territory. The current share price of `79.63` sits just beneath its 50-day moving average of `80.22` and its 200-day moving average of `80.51`. The daily RSI registers at `49.03`, indicating an evenly balanced market neither overbought nor oversold. It trades `2.13%` below its 52-week high and `6.06%` above its 52-week low. As is typical for fixed-income ETFs, these moving average and momentum signals carry less weight, as returns are structurally driven by yield rather than price momentum.

Overall, this ETF's performance profile looks mixed because it successfully delivers standard asset-class income, but structurally lags its benchmark. Its primary strength is reliable income generation, highlighted by a `6.59%` SEC yield and a `10.01%` 1-year total return. Its main weakness is the long-term tracking difference, underperforming the index by `0.93` percentage points annualized over 10 years, alongside standard credit-market rate sensitivity that led to an `11.37%` NAV drop in 2022.

### Factor results

#### `benchmark_comparison` — Fail

_While the fund performs in line with peers, it persistently trails its underlying index across multiple timeframes, creating a notable performance drag._

The ETF consistently falls short of its stated benchmark, the iBoxx USD Liquid High Yield Index, across virtually all measured horizons. Over a 10-year span, the ETF returned `5.16%`, which is noticeably below the benchmark index's `6.10%` return, representing a nearly `1.0` percentage point annual gap. Over 5 years, it trails the benchmark `3.88%` to `4.43%`. Although its absolute returns are still respectable and it matches the category average, the persistent underperformance against the benchmark index highlights significant structural drag, largely stemming from fees and the inherent trading costs associated with corporate bonds.

#### `category_peer_standing` — Pass

_The ETF consistently lands near the middle of its heavily active peer group, a standard outcome for a passive instrument._

In the US Fund High Yield Bond category, HYG ranks in the 49th percentile among 594 funds over 1 year, the 38th percentile out of 564 funds over 3 years, and the 60th percentile out of 423 funds over 10 years. Because the vast majority of these peers are active managers who can selectively avoid defaulting issuers, a passive index tracker is expected to land near the middle of the pack. Trailing the category median slightly over 10 years (`5.19%` vs `5.35%`) is not a failure of the fund, but rather a feature of its passive design.

#### `drawdown_and_recovery` — Pass

_The fund's massive distance from its all-time high reflects the structural nature of bond ETFs, while its recent price action remains tight._

HYG is currently down `-25.26%` from its all-time high set back in 2007. For a high-yield bond fund, this price erosion is entirely normal, as a massive portion of the fund's total return is paid out as cash distributions rather than retained in the share price. Looking at more recent and relevant data, the fund's 52-week range of `$75.08` to `$81.36` shows that recent downside volatility has been quite mild. The absolute historical drawdown is a mathematical reality of bond ETFs, not a true failure of capital preservation.

#### `income_vs_price_return` — Pass

_Distributions drive virtually all of the fund's total return, offsetting structural price erosion over time._

Over the past 10 years, HYG delivered a total price return of `66.84%`, even though the share price itself declined by `1.60%`. Similarly, over the past 5 years, the price dropped `8.64%` while the total return reached `19.96%`. This dynamic is exactly how high-yield bond funds operate: the `6.59%` SEC yield compensates investors for the natural price decay caused by credit defaults and interest rate changes. The fund passes this factor by effectively translating underlying corporate bond coupons into consistent investor returns.

#### `long_term_cagr` — Pass

_Long-term compounding aligns with the high-yield market's expected returns, though it trails the raw index._

HYG produced a 5-year CAGR of `3.71%`, a 10-year CAGR of `5.25%`, and a 15-year CAGR of `4.74%`. As a passive tracker of the iBoxx USD Liquid High Yield Index, the fund structurally lags the benchmark's 10-year annualized return of `6.12%`. This `0.93` percentage point shortfall reflects the fund's expense ratio and the unavoidable transaction friction of trading high-yield corporate bonds. Since this tracking difference remains within the 1.0 percentage point tolerance for high-yield passive mandates, the fund delivers on its core objective.

#### `price_trend_momentum` — Fail

_The fund is currently exhibiting sluggish to negative momentum, trading just below its most important longer-term moving averages._

The ETF currently trades at `$79.63`, which is only marginally above its short-term 20-day moving average of `$79.42`. More concerning for momentum investors is that the price is pinned beneath all of its key longer-term trendlines, including the 50-day moving average (`$80.22`), the 150-day moving average (`$80.57`), and the crucial 200-day moving average (`$80.51`). The daily relative strength index (RSI) sits at a perfectly neutral `49.03`, confirming a complete lack of strong directional conviction. The inability to reclaim these key longer-term moving averages shows lackluster current momentum and a failure to establish a defined uptrend.

#### `rate_environment_resilience` — Pass

_HYG absorbs rate volatility in line with its benchmark and broader category._

During the rapid rate-hiking cycle of 2022, HYG suffered an `11.37%` NAV loss. This drawdown was a macroeconomic event rather than a fund-specific failure, closely tracking the `11.09%` loss of its underlying index and the `10.09%` decline in the category median. In more favorable rate environments, such as 2019, the fund captured upside efficiently with a `14.23%` gain. Credit spreads naturally provide some cushion against pure interest rate risk, keeping its volatility aligned with its mandate.

#### `returns_consistency` — Pass

_The fund exhibits normal credit-market cyclicality, reliably capturing both the upside and downside of broad market swings._

HYG posted positive NAV returns in 7 of the past 8 full calendar years, including a `12.41%` gain in 2023 and an `8.56%` return in 2025. It experienced one major disruption in 2022, declining `11.37%`. Its relative consistency is demonstrated by its steady percentile ranks, which fluctuate within a reasonable band from the 36th percentile in 2025 to the 68th percentile in 2020. The fund recovers adequately from drawdown years, performing precisely as the high-yield asset class dictates.

#### `short_term_returns` — Pass

_Short-term momentum has flattened out recently, with the fund treading water over the last few months despite boasting a strong 1-year total return._

Over the past 1-year period, the ETF posted a robust return of `9.88%`, indicating a strong recovery and favorable credit conditions over the broader trailing period. However, looking closer at the most recent months, its momentum has significantly slowed down. The fund sits at just `0.15%` year-to-date, and is slightly negative over both the past 3 months (`-0.16%`) and 1 month (`-0.14%`). While the broader 1-year picture remains undeniably strong, the current short-term stall shows that the fund is simply chopping sideways right now, digesting previous gains.
