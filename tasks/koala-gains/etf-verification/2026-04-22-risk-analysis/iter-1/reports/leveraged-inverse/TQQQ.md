# ETF Report — NASDAQ/TQQQ

- **Group:** Leveraged & Inverse Trading (`leveraged-inverse`)
- **Category:** Trading--Leveraged Equity

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

The risk profile for TQQQ is Mixed, serving purely as a tactical trading instrument rather than a long-term investment. The fund executes its 3x daily leverage mandate, reflected in a `3.53` beta and a 5-year upside capture of `303`. However, the mathematical realities of daily resets expose long-term holders to severe volatility drag, evidenced by a `389` downside capture and a `-79.03%` maximum drawdown. Overall, this ETF delivers on its promised extreme short-term exposure but carries structural decay risks for buy-and-hold investors.

### Overall analysis

TQQQ exhibits high volatility by design, maintaining a multi-year beta of `3.53` against the broad market. This amplified volatility aligns closely with the fund's mandate to deliver three times the daily returns of the Nasdaq-100. Because leverage is reset daily, the realized beta slightly exceeds a perfect multiplier over longer horizons due to compounding effects. While the fund registers a trailing Sharpe ratio of `0.84` and a Sortino ratio of `1.38`, these multi-year risk-adjusted metrics are fundamentally flawed lenses for a daily-reset instrument. A leveraged fund's primary risk metric is its intentional, amplified standard deviation, making it suitable only for short-horizon directional trades rather than buy-and-hold allocations.

The structural dangers of holding a leveraged ETF through a bear market are clearly illustrated by the fund's historical drawdown profile. During the 2022 rate shock and tech sell-off, TQQQ suffered a maximum drawdown of `-79.03%` between January and December, while its underlying index fell `-24.88%`. In a more recent 3-year window, it experienced a `-33.31%` drop compared to the benchmark's `-8.82%`. These drops show how rapid market corrections can severely impact long-term capital when leverage is applied. Despite an absolute portfolio risk score of `246` (Extreme), its risk versus its specific Trading--Leveraged Equity category is rated `Low`. This peer-relative comparison indicates that its deep price swings are entirely standard for this highly specialized, high-beta peer group.

As a leveraged-inverse category fund, the core risk driver is daily-reset tracking fidelity and path-dependency decay. Over a 5-year period, the ETF achieved an upside capture ratio of `303` relative to the index's `99`, demonstrating functional tracking of its target multiplier in sustained bull runs. However, the downside capture over the same window widened to `389`. This asymmetry represents the mathematical reality of volatility drag. In choppy or downward trending periods, daily compounding works aggressively against the investor, amplifying losses well beyond the stated daily leverage multiple. Therefore, holding this fund over multi-month or multi-year windows guarantees exposure to structural decay.

The fund's primary strength is its precise mandate execution, achieving a 10-year upside capture of `321` to capture extended bull cycles. Additionally, its robust trading volume of over `58` million shares ensures the high liquidity necessary for short-term entry and exit without significant bid-ask slippage. The clear red flags are its structural path-dependency decay, shown by a 10-year downside capture of `363`, and the prolonged peak-to-trough drops spanning `12` months in 2022 that can rapidly erode capital. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks mixed because it is a highly effective tactical tool for day traders but structurally misaligned as a buy-and-hold asset.
