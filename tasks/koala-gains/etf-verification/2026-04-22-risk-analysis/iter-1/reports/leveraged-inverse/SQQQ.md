## Risk Analysis

### Summary

The risk profile is Mixed, reflecting flawless short-term execution coupled with inevitable long-term capital decay. It operates with a 5-year beta of `-3.42` against the broad market and boasts extreme liquidity with over `32,316,010` shares traded daily. However, buy-and-hold investors face mathematical certainty of wealth destruction, highlighted by a 10-year worst drawdown of `-99.96%`. Overall, it is a highly effective tactical hedging tool that becomes entirely unsuitable if held beyond a very brief window.

### Overall analysis

This ETF delivers amplified volatility by design, operating with a 1-year beta of `-3.77` to provide triple-inverse equity exposure. Multi-year risk-adjusted returns are severely negative, with a trailing Sharpe ratio of `-0.909` and a Sortino ratio of `-1.118`. However, applying traditional buy-and-hold risk metrics to a daily-reset derivative product is fundamentally flawed; these inverse ratios reflect the structural reality of shorting a long-term bull market rather than poor fund management. The volatility perfectly fits its stated mandate as a day-trading and tactical hedging instrument.

Because the fund resets its leverage target daily, compounding drag makes total capital loss over multi-year windows inevitable. This is evidenced by a 5-year maximum drawdown of `-95.62%`, which lasted an extended `56 Months` from its local peak. Yet, when evaluated against peers in the Trading--Inverse Equity space, Morningstar assigns it a relative risk rating of Low. This indicates that while the absolute downside is severe, it behaves exactly in line with competing ultra-short products navigating the exact same structural headwinds.

For a leveraged-inverse fund, the dominant risk driver is daily tracking fidelity and path-dependency decay, measured heavily by capture ratios. Over a 3-year window, the fund registered an upside capture of `-313` and a downside capture of `-241`. This asymmetry reveals the heavy cost of volatility drag in choppy markets: it captures slightly more than triple the underlying index's gains on the way down, but struggles to maintain exact proportions during sustained upward rallies due to constant daily rebalancing.

The fund's primary strength is its structural reliability, achieving its intended inverse tracking without triggering unexpected operational failures. However, the ultimate red flag is path-dependency decay, illustrated by a total `-100.00%` collapse from its split-adjusted all-time high and a high absolute risk score of `307`. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks mixed because it offers highly efficient execution for immediate tactical downside protection, but carries absolute mathematical certainty of portfolio destruction if held as a long-term investment.
