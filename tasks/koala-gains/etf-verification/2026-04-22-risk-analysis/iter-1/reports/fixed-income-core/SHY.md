# ETF Report — NASDAQ/SHY

- **Group:** Fixed Income — Core & Government (`fixed-income-core`)
- **Category:** Short Government

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

SHY presents a strong, highly defensive risk profile perfectly suited for conservative portfolios seeking capital preservation. As a short-term government bond fund, it exhibits minimal volatility, maintaining a 5-year standard deviation of 2.04% which sits comfortably below the category average of 2.49%. Its worst 10-year drawdown was just -5.35% during the 2022 rate shock, outperforming its peer average and shielding investors from severe fixed-income losses. Overall, the fund successfully delivers on its low-volatility mandate, taking below-average risk to generate average category returns.

### Overall analysis

SHY is a short-duration government bond fund, meaning its volatility is structurally minimal by design. Its 5-year standard deviation is an exceptionally low 2.04%, operating safely below the Short Government category average of 2.49%. Because it is insulated from broad equity market swings, its equity beta (0.05) is near zero, aligning perfectly with its defensive mandate. While its multi-year Sharpe ratios are negative—such as its -0.98 over a 5-year window—this is a common characteristic of short-duration bonds during periods when cash rates outpaced short bond yields. The primary objective here is downside mitigation rather than maximizing risk-adjusted excess returns.

The true test for fixed-income funds is their drawdown magnitude during periods of rising interest rates. During the unprecedented 2022 rate shock, SHY experienced a maximum 10-year drawdown of just -5.35%, charting a peak in February 2021 to a valley in October 2022. This performance was highly resilient compared to the category's -7.00% drawdown and the benchmark index's -7.63% decline. Across 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year observation windows, Morningstar consistently rates its risk as "Below Average" while its category-relative return is ranked as "Average," confirming strong peer-relative downside protection.

For short government funds, duration (interest-rate risk) is the primary driver of volatility. Placed in the Morningstar "High / Limited" style box, SHY holds high-quality government debt with limited maturity. This short-duration profile successfully shielded the fund when long-term interest rates spiked; while long-duration treasury funds suffered losses exceeding -25% in 2022, SHY strictly capped its downside. This confirms the fund is functioning exactly as intended for a limited-duration mandate, avoiding the severe rate-shock damage seen in aggregate or long-term bond funds. Short-term technical indicators reflect this extreme stability, with RSI hovering near 42.3 amid a virtually flat long-term trajectory.

SHY's core strengths include its extraordinarily tight 52-week trading range ($82.21 to $83.20) and a shallow maximum multi-year drawdown (-5.35%) that notably outperformed category peers. A minor weakness is its lagging 5-year Sharpe ratio (-0.98 vs the category's -0.83), reflecting a very slight drag in relative excess returns. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because it cleanly delivers the low-volatility, capital-preserving characteristics required of a short-term treasury fund, having successfully dodged the most destructive phases of recent interest rate cycles.

### Factor results

#### `overall_volatility` — Pass

_SHY exhibits exceptionally low volatility, successfully fulfilling its conservative mandate as a short-term government bond fund._

As a short-duration Treasury fund, equity beta is not a meaningful risk lens; its current beta of 0.05 simply reflects a total decorrelation from the stock market. Instead, standard deviation accurately reflects its price stability. The fund's 5-year standard deviation is 2.04%, which is 0.45 percentage points lower than the category average of 2.49%. Price movements are exceedingly narrow, evidenced by an Average True Range (ATR) of just 0.13 and a 52-week range spanning less than one dollar ($82.21 to $83.20). This extremely calm price action is precisely what investors require from a cash-alternative or capital-preservation vehicle.

#### `risk_adjusted_return` — Pass

_While SHY's multi-year Sharpe ratios are negative, this is a structural feature of short-term bonds during recent rate cycles rather than a fund failure._

Over the 5-year and 10-year periods, SHY posted Sharpe ratios of -0.98 and -0.46, respectively. These figures are marginally weaker than the category averages of -0.83 and -0.37. Applying the narrow 0.5 percentage point threshold for fixed-income funds, SHY's risk-adjusted return sits In Line with the category. Negative Sharpe ratios in the short-duration space over the last decade were primarily driven by cash rates temporarily outpacing low short-term bond yields, forcing the excess return numerator below zero. Because the fund matches the risk/return mechanics of its category and serves as a volatility dampener rather than a return engine, it executes its mandate properly.

#### `worst_drawdown` — Pass

_SHY experienced a maximum multi-year drawdown of just -5.35% during the 2022 rate shock, comfortably outperforming its category peers._

The fund's deepest multi-year drop was a remarkably shallow -5.35%, stretching from a peak in August 2021 to a valley in October 2022 during an aggressive Federal Reserve rate-hiking cycle. This result is highly favorable when compared to the Short Government category's average drawdown of -6.86% and the benchmark index's -7.54%. By losing 1.51 percentage points less than its peers, the fund demonstrated excellent capital preservation during fixed income's worst modern stress test. The ETF's all-time drop from its peak is logged at just -5.12%, reinforcing its structural safety during broader market panics.

#### `risk_vs_peers` — Pass

_SHY consistently takes less risk than its category peers while delivering average relative returns._

Across the 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year evaluation windows, Morningstar rates SHY's risk versus the category as "Below Avg." while consistently rating its returns as "Average." This represents an optimal tradeoff for a conservative fixed-income allocation. Its 10-year standard deviation sits at 1.63%, safely below the category's 2.01%. Because the fund operates with meaningfully less risk than its peers but does not suffer a corresponding penalty in relative return tiering, it is managing risk efficiently and shielding investors from unnecessary downside variance.

#### `interest_rate_sensitivity` — Pass

_Limited duration completely protected the fund from the severe capital destruction that hit longer-term bond funds in 2022._

Classified within the "High / Limited" fixed-income style box, SHY is explicitly designed to minimize interest-rate risk. This structural design proved exceptionally effective during the 2022 rate shock. While long-duration government funds lost roughly -25% to -31% as the 10-year Treasury yield spiked, SHY's maximum 5-year drawdown was contained at a mere -5.35%. This performance was better than the category average loss of -6.86%. By limiting maturity strictly to the 1-3 year band, the fund neutralizes severe duration risk, operating exactly as promised for yield-seeking but rate-wary retail investors.
