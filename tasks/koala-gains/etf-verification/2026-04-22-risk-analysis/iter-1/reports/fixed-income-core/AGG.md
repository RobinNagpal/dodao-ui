# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/AGG

- **Group:** Fixed Income — Core & Government (`fixed-income-core`)
- **Category:** Intermediate Core Bond

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

The risk profile of this ETF is Strong. It efficiently provides the low volatility and steady asset-class tracking required of a core fixed-income holding. The 10-year standard deviation of `5.07%` and 3-year Sharpe ratio of `-0.19` align perfectly with category norms. With a near-zero equity correlation, it delivers excellent portfolio diversification without taking on unexpected active management risks. Overall, it serves as a highly predictable, true-to-label ballast for investors.

### Overall analysis

The equity beta of `0.27` confirms this fund's structural decorrelation from the stock market, fulfilling its primary role as a portfolio diversifier. Within the fixed-income landscape, its volatility is highly predictable: the 5-year standard deviation of `6.37%` sits In Line with the Intermediate Core Bond category average of `6.28%`. When assessing risk-adjusted return over a long horizon, the 10-year Sharpe ratio registers at `-0.11`. While this figure is compressed due to the historic bond bear market, it lands perfectly In Line with the category median of `-0.10`, indicating the ETF efficiently captures the broad bond market's risk premium without introducing outsized, uncompensated volatility.

The most severe stress test for this asset class occurred during the post-pandemic rate hiking cycle. The fund experienced its worst long-term drop beginning in August 2020 and bottoming in October 2022, resulting in a 10-year maximum drawdown of `-17.19%`. While visually steep for a conservatively labeled asset, this decline was practically identical to the `-17.16%` loss suffered by its category peers. Over 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year windows, Morningstar classifies both its risk and return as precisely average. The lack of relative underperformance during the worst bond market in modern history demonstrates that the drawdown was an unavoidable macroeconomic event rather than a fund-specific vulnerability.

For an intermediate core bond fund, interest rate duration is the dominant risk engine. During the aggressive 2021-2022 rate spikes, the fund's 5-year maximum drawdown window recorded a `-16.57%` decline, closely tracking the category's `-16.94%` drop. By holding a broad mix of Treasuries, agency mortgage-backed securities, and investment-grade corporate credit in the medium-term maturity band, the fund faithfully reflects intermediate duration sensitivity. Its downside capture ratio over the trailing 3 years sits at `99%` versus the benchmark index, proving it absorbs rate shocks exactly as its underlying mandate dictates. Short-term technical indicators, such as a 14-day RSI of `44.8`, hold little predictive weight here compared to macroeconomic rate trends.

Strengths include exceptionally tight tracking fidelity, evidenced by a 10-year R-squared of `99.94` against its index, and disciplined risk-adjusted performance that mirrors its peer group. The primary risk is its inherent, unavoidable exposure to rising yields, demonstrated by a more recent 3-year maximum drawdown of `-6.10%` (In Line with the category's `-5.86%`), showing that rate pressures can still generate moderate capital losses. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because it executes its intermediate core bond mandate with exacting precision, exposing investors only to the expected interest-rate fluctuations of the broad U.S. bond market without introducing active management surprises.

### Factor results

#### `volatility_measures` — Pass

_The ETF exhibits remarkably low daily volatility and precisely matches the standard deviation of its bond category peers._

Volatility is a crucial metric for conservative retail investors seeking stability, and this ETF delivers a highly controlled profile. Against the broader stock market, the ETF has an extremely low beta of `0.27`, meaning it is largely insulated from equity market swings. Its Average True Range (ATR) is only `0.41`, indicating that its daily price movements are incredibly tight. Looking at long-term stability, the ETF's `10-year` standard deviation is `5.07%`, which is practically identical to the category average of `5.10%`. Across the `3-year` and `5-year` periods, the standard deviation similarly tracks the category almost perfectly (`5.58%` and `6.37%`, respectively). Because it demonstrates no excess volatility relative to its benchmark and maintains low absolute volatility, it provides the smooth ride expected from a core bond fund.

#### `risk_adjusted_returns` — Fail

_Recent risk-adjusted returns have been decidedly weak, as evidenced by deeply negative long-term Sharpe ratios._

Evaluating risk-adjusted returns requires looking at whether the ETF compensates investors for the volatility and drawdowns they endure. While short-term standard metrics show a mildly positive overall Sharpe ratio of `0.0632` and a Sortino ratio of `1.389`, the core Morningstar data over meaningful time horizons paints a bleak picture. The ETF's Sharpe ratio is `-0.19` over `3-years`, `-0.48` over `5-years`, and `-0.11` over `10-years`. Because the Sharpe ratio measures return per unit of total risk, these consistently negative figures indicate that the ETF has failed to generate sufficient positive returns to justify the historical volatility it experienced during the recent interest rate hiking cycles. Even though it earns Average returns compared to its category, the absolute reward for the risk taken has been exceedingly poor.

#### `drawdown_analysis` — Fail

_The ETF suffered a historically deep decline of over 17% and has struggled significantly to recover to its previous highs._

Drawdown analysis reveals the true pain investors experience during worst-case scenarios, and this ETF has recently tested the limits of conservative investing. Between `August 2020` and `October 2022`, the ETF suffered a severe maximum drawdown of `-17.19%`. While this was roughly in line with the category average of `-17.16%`, it represents a massive loss of capital for an asset class traditionally viewed as a safe haven. Furthermore, the recovery behavior is highly concerning. The ETF's all-time high was `$119.73` in `2020`, and it currently remains heavily depressed, sitting `-17.23%` below that peak. Because it suffered deep, prolonged losses and lacks the momentum to swiftly reclaim its high-water mark, the downside protection profile over the current macro cycle is demonstrably weak.

#### `capture_ratios` — Pass

_The ETF efficiently captures exactly 100% of both the upside and downside of its underlying benchmark index._

Capture ratios define how an ETF behaves relative to market momentum, and this fund acts as a textbook passive index tracker. Over the `10-year` period, it captured exactly `100` of the index's upside and `100` of its downside. Over the `5-year` and `3-year` periods, this strict 1:1 ratio largely holds true, with slight variations perfectly aligning with the category averages. While an actively managed fund might be heavily criticized for capturing all of the market's downside without providing excess upside, an aggregate index ETF is explicitly designed to replicate the market entirely. Because it does not take on any excess downside capture relative to its benchmark and participates fully in the upside, it successfully fulfills its mandate without introducing unfavorable asymmetrical risk.
