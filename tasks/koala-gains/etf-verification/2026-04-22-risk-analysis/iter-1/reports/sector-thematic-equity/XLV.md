# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/XLV

- **Group:** Sector & Thematic Equity (`sector-thematic-equity`)
- **Category:** Health

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

The risk profile of the State Street Health Care Select Sector SPDR ETF (XLV) is Strong. The fund boasts a 3-year beta of `0.71` and an overarching downside capture ratio of `88`, indicating a much calmer ride than the broader equity market with a highly favorable downside profile. It reliably limits damage, evidenced by a consistent `Below Avg.` Morningstar risk rating compared to its sector peers across all timeframes. Overall, XLV fully delivers on the defensive promise of the healthcare space by successfully offering below-average volatility without sacrificing long-term market returns.

### Overall analysis

The ETF consistently provides a lower-volatility ride than both the broad market and its peers, a dynamic that aligns perfectly with the defensive reputation of the US Health category. Looking at multi-year periods, the fund operates with a 5-year beta of `0.64` and a trailing 3-year standard deviation of `14.10%`. When compared to the category average standard deviation of `17.99%`, it becomes clear that this index dampens the turbulence often found in actively managed or sub-sector healthcare strategies. Because the mandate is broad-based defensive sector exposure, this below-market volatility is precisely what retail investors should expect. Furthermore, the risk-adjusted return profile is solid and steadily rewarded over time. Over the 10-year window, its Sharpe ratio of `0.56` exceeds the category median of `0.44`. The shorter 5-year Sharpe ratio of `0.25` also outpaces the peer group's severely compressed `0.04`. This divergence in Sharpe ratios proves that long-term investors are being fairly compensated for the underlying price swings, as the passive market-cap weighting captures healthcare premiums without taking on the uncompensated hazards of stock picking.

When measuring peak-to-trough losses, this vehicle showcases strong downside resilience against market shocks and sector-specific headwinds. During the most recent recorded stress window—stretching from September 2024 to July 2025—the portfolio's maximum drawdown halted at `-15.65%`. While a double-digit decline is always a noticeable event for a retail portfolio, the comparative gap is what truly matters in risk assessment. This drop is substantially milder than the `-29.28%` maximum plunge suffered by competing healthcare strategies over the trailing decade. Investors holding this passive vehicle experienced nearly half the destruction of capital compared to those in more aggressive sector funds. Additionally, Morningstar flags the fund's overall risk as below-average across all measured windows, while simultaneously categorizing its returns as `Average` or `Above Avg.`. This consistent disconnect between low risk taken and high relative reward earned is the hallmark of an effective thematic holding. It confirms that the fund efficiently preserves capital during stress events, making the inevitable recovery phase much easier to hold through.

As a sector-specific equity fund, internal concentration is a built-in feature rather than a flaw, but the portfolio manages these allocations within highly reasonable bounds. The top 10 holdings account for roughly `59%` of total assets, sitting at the higher end of typical industry ranges but successfully avoiding extreme consolidation. Its largest single position, Eli Lilly, makes up approximately `14%` of the allocation. While a double-digit weighting implies that this single pharmaceutical giant meaningfully dictates the daily price path of the ETF, it remains safely beneath the critical threshold that often plagues top-heavy technology or energy funds. Most importantly, this structural top-heaviness has not generated painful sector-peer drawdowns. The underlying mega-cap bias actually provides a defensive moat, as cash-rich pharmaceutical and managed-care companies effectively buffer the portfolio against the severe volatility routinely seen in smaller biotechnology or medical-device competitors. For an investor seeking targeted thematic equity, the concentration is adequately compensated by the stability of the underlying businesses.

The fund's primary strengths revolve entirely around its strong capital protection and consistent risk-adjusted efficiency compared to its peer group. Its 5-year downside capture ratio of `81` is notably superior to the category norm of `106`, confirming it reliably sidesteps the worst of broad market drops and acts as a functional portfolio anchor. Furthermore, its ability to generate an alpha of `1.30` over a ten-year horizon against its baseline index reinforces the efficiency of its specific risk posture. The main vulnerability lies in its inherent sensitivity to concentrated pharmaceutical trial results and shifting federal regulatory environments, given the heavy weighting assigned to just a few top industry players. A significant pipeline failure for one of its top three holdings would exert immediate drag on the fund's net asset value. However, the overall execution remains highly disciplined and historically resilient. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because it successfully limits severe drawdowns and peer-relative turbulence while maintaining highly consistent risk-adjusted returns over full market cycles.
