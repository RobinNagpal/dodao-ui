# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/XLK

- **Group:** Sector & Thematic Equity (`sector-thematic-equity`)
- **Category:** Technology

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

XLK's risk profile is Strong, combining exceptional peer-relative capital protection with highly efficient risk-adjusted returns. The ETF managed to keep its 10-year Sharpe ratio at `0.97`—easily clearing the `0.74` category average—while consistently taking less overall risk than competing technology funds. However, investors must accept severe top-heavy positioning, with the top 10 holdings accounting for `61.22%` of the entire portfolio. Overall, this ETF delivers a highly rewarding, capital-efficient ride for those willing to stomach extreme single-name concentration.

### Overall analysis

XLK demonstrates a highly efficient approach to sector-level volatility. Over a five-year window, the fund registered a standard deviation of `20.89%`, which notably undercuts the `23.32%` volatility experienced by the broader technology category. It translates these price swings into robust absolute and relative gains, evidenced by a 5-year Sharpe ratio of `0.64` that more than doubles the `0.28` category median. Furthermore, a 3-year Sortino ratio of `1.72` confirms that the fund's excess movement has been overwhelmingly biased toward the upside rather than the downside.

When sector stress inevitably hits, the ETF has historically protected capital far better than its direct peers. During the brutal 2022 rate shock, the fund experienced a maximum drawdown of `-31.23%` from January to September of that year. While painful in absolute terms, this 9-month slide was significantly shallower than the catastrophic `-40.97%` drop suffered by the broader category. Its structural discipline is further highlighted by a 5-year downside capture ratio of `108`, which proved vastly superior to the punishing `130` category average, proving that the index construction effectively limits downside leakage during market panic.

The primary trade-off for this historical outperformance is a severe lack of within-theme diversification. As a market-cap-weighted product, the ETF is heavily skewed toward a tiny cluster of mega-cap giants rather than the broader tech ecosystem. As of April 2026, NVIDIA alone commands `15.45%` of the portfolio, closely trailed by an Apple weight of `12.62%` and Microsoft at `9.77%`. With nearly `38%` of total assets locked into just three companies, the fund's future trajectory is immensely path-dependent on these specific business models rather than general technology sector trends.

The fund’s core strengths lie in its peer-beating defensive posture and its ability to participate aggressively in rallies, shown by a 5-year upside capture of `127` (outpacing the `112` category mark). The most glaring red flag remains its single-name concentration, which strips away the diversification benefit typically expected from an ETF wrapper. Short-term activity also points to rising sensitivity, with the 1-year beta ticking up to `1.41`. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because it effectively dampens category-level drawdowns while delivering structurally superior risk-adjusted returns.

### Factor results

#### `overall_volatility` — Pass

_The fund exhibits amplified price swings that align perfectly with its aggressive technology mandate, while remaining less volatile than its direct peers._

Technology funds are inherently volatile, and XLK's 5-year beta of `1.22` confirms it moves with more velocity than the broader equity market. However, the true test is how it compares to funds executing the exact same mandate. The ETF's 5-year standard deviation of `20.89%` is actually lower than the `23.32%` average seen across its category, meaning it provides a comparatively smoother ride. Because the volatility profile perfectly fits the stated tech-sector focus without exceeding peer boundaries, it easily clears the mandate-alignment test.

#### `risk_adjusted_return` — Pass

_The ETF generates exceptional risk-adjusted performance, consistently doubling or tripling the category median across multi-year windows._

Sharpe ratios are the ultimate indicator of whether an investor is being adequately paid for taking on excess sector risk. XLK excels here, posting a 5-year Sharpe ratio of `0.64` against a mere `0.28` for the category. Its longer-term 10-year Sharpe sits at a robust `0.97`. A strong 3-year Sortino ratio of `1.72` reinforces that the fund achieves these results by capturing upside volatility rather than subjecting investors to wild downside swings. The fund's risk compensation is elite for its asset class.

#### `worst_drawdown` — Pass

_The fund proved its resilience during the 2022 tech wreck by avoiding the catastrophic losses that decimated its peer group._

Drawdown severity must be judged against the asset class environment. During the 2022 rate shock, XLK fell `-31.23%` before hitting its valley in September. While a 30% loss is a severe absolute decline, the broader technology category plummeted by an abysmal `-40.97%` during the exact same period. By avoiding almost 10 percentage points of downside capture compared to similar funds, XLK proved that its specific portfolio construction serves as a relatively sturdy anchor during severe sector-wide corrections.

#### `risk_vs_peers` — Pass

_The ETF achieves an incredibly rare feat by taking below-average risk while delivering above-average category returns._

Morningstar grades XLK's risk level as `Below Avg.` over both the 3-year and 10-year periods when measured against competing tech funds. Crucially, the fund does not sacrifice performance to achieve this safety, as its 5-year and 10-year returns are ranked `Above Avg.` against the same peer group. Taking less relative risk to achieve higher relative reward is the textbook definition of a structurally sound portfolio. It easily passes this test by demonstrating disciplined, peer-beating risk management.

#### `concentration_risk` — Fail

_The fund is exceedingly top-heavy, with extreme single-stock exposure that violates conservative diversification thresholds._

While XLK's concentration has historically driven its outperformance, it poses a massive structural risk. As of April 2026, the portfolio's top 10 holdings command over `61%` of total assets. More concerningly, single-name concentration exceeds the typical `15%` threshold for hidden stock-specific risk, with NVIDIA alone holding a `15.45%` weight [1.1]. Because the fund's fortunes are overwhelmingly tethered to just a few mega-cap names rather than the broader technology theme, it fails the threshold for safe within-theme diversification.
