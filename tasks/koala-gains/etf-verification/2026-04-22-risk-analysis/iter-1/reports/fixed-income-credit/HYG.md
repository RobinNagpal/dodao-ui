# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/HYG

- **Group:** Fixed Income — Credit & Income (`fixed-income-credit`)
- **Category:** High Yield Bond

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

The risk profile for this High Yield Bond ETF is Mixed. Over a five-year window, it exhibits a beta of `0.85` relative to its benchmark, alongside a moderate three-year Sharpe ratio of `0.69`. However, the fund consistently scores above category averages for risk while only delivering average returns. Overall, it provides the expected credit exposure but demands investors accept a slightly bumpier ride than peer alternatives.

### Overall analysis

Volatility metrics show the fund runs marginally hotter than its peer group, posting a five-year standard deviation of `7.33%` against the category norm of `6.31%`. Risk-adjusted efficiency remains acceptable for the asset class, with a ten-year Sharpe ratio of `0.42` closely tracking the category median of `0.45`. This level of price fluctuation perfectly fits the mandate of a broad, liquid junk-bond portfolio, even if the risk-return tradeoff slightly trails the broader index.

During the 2022 rate shock, the ETF suffered a peak-to-trough decline of `-14.86%` between January and September. This drawdown was slightly deeper than the category average loss of `-13.72%` over the same stress window. Morningstar classifies its five-year risk profile as `High` relative to peers, yet its return profile remains strictly `Average`, highlighting a structural headwind where investors are taking on excess volatility without being compensated.

As a fixed-income-credit fund focused on high-yield debt, credit spread widening is the primary risk driver alongside base interest rates. The portfolio captures a disproportionate share of market drops, evidenced by a five-year downside capture ratio of `53` versus the peer average of `38`. While it does offset this by catching more upward momentum—an upside capture of `93` compared to `80`—the asymmetric downside exposure confirms real vulnerability during credit dislocation events.

Strengths include robust market participation, catching `105` on the ten-year upside capture metric against a category average of `97`. Conversely, the primary red flags are the uncompensated peer-relative volatility and an all-time maximum drop of `-25.26%` recorded during the 2008 credit crisis, demonstrating the substantial tail risk inherent in sub-investment grade debt. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks mixed because it successfully delivers core high-yield exposure but demands slightly more downside tolerance than the average fund in its category.

### Factor results

#### `volatility_measures` — Fail

_The ETF exhibits higher volatility and larger standard deviations than its typical high-yield bond peer._

While HYG has an overall beta of `0.41788`, which looks low compared to the broader stock market, its volatility is high when compared to its direct category. Over a 5-year period, the ETF’s standard deviation is `7.33`, which sits ABOVE the category average of `6.31`. Similarly, its 5-year benchmark-relative beta of `0.85` exceeds the peer average of `0.71`. The fund's ATR of `0.4328` points to moderate daily price swings, but the overarching data clearly shows that this ETF provides a bumpier ride than standard alternatives in the high-yield space. Because it fails to control volatility relative to its category, it does not pass this metric.

#### `risk_adjusted_returns` — Fail

_The fund takes on above-average risk but only delivers average returns, resulting in weak risk-adjusted efficiency._

An evaluation of Sharpe and Sortino ratios shows that investors are not being adequately compensated for the bumps they tolerate. The ETF has an absolute Sharpe ratio of `0.5209` and a Sortino ratio of `1.883`. However, when viewed against peers, the inefficiency is clear: its 5-year Sharpe ratio is `0.05`, trailing the category average of `0.09`. Morningstar categorizes the ETF's risk as “High” over the 5-year period and “Above Avg.” over the 3-year period, yet its returns are consistently classified as only “Average.” This indicates a poor return per unit of risk compared to alternatives.

#### `drawdown_analysis` — Fail

_The ETF suffered deeper maximum declines than its peers during recent bond market stress._

During the intense rate-hike cycle from January 2022 to September 2022, HYG experienced a maximum drawdown of `-14.86%`. This peak-to-trough decline was noticeably worse than the category average drawdown of `-13.72%` over the same period. Furthermore, the ETF currently sits `-25.26%` below its all-time high of `106.47` set in 2007, reflecting long-term principal decay common to this asset class but still punishing for long-term holders. Because it demonstrates weaker downside protection during major drawdowns than comparable funds, it fails to provide a superior safety net.

#### `capture_ratios` — Fail

_Despite strong upside participation, the ETF absorbs too much market downside, creating an unfavorable risk balance._

Capture ratios measure how much of a benchmark's movement a fund absorbs. Over the 5-year period, HYG demonstrated a very strong upside capture ratio of `93`, easily beating the category average of `80`. However, this came at the steep cost of a downside capture ratio of `53`, which is substantially worse than the category's `38`. Over the 3-year timeframe, the dynamic is similar: the fund captured `7` of the downside while the average peer captured `-3`. Because the ETF systematically falls harder than its peers during weak markets, the balance of risk is unfavorable.

#### `risk_vs_category` — Fail

_The ETF consistently ranks as having higher risk than its category without offering a proportionate return advantage._

Comparing the fund’s overall risk score against its category peers reveals a consistent disadvantage. While the ETF's absolute risk level is rated "Moderate" with a score of `36`, its relative positioning is weak. Over the 3-year period, its risk is rated “Above Avg.”, and over the 5-year period, its risk is elevated to “High.” Only over the 10-year stretch does it moderate to “Average.” Because the fund is consistently taking more risk than its peers while delivering merely average returns across all these timeframes, it fails the category comparison standard.

#### `overall_volatility` — Pass

_The fund's price swings are slightly elevated but remain aligned with a broad high-yield mandate._

Over a five-year period, standard deviation sits at `7.33%`, which is noticeably higher than the `6.31%` category average. However, the benchmark tracking is functioning as designed, capturing market moves with an `R²` of `55.22` over the same window. Because this is a passive instrument reflecting the aggregate high-yield bond universe, the elevated volatility matches its core mandate rather than indicating a structural flaw.

#### `risk_adjusted_return` — Pass

_Risk-adjusted returns closely hug the category median across all major timeframes._

The three-year Sharpe ratio of `0.69` sits just below the category's `0.71`, while the ten-year Sharpe of `0.42` similarly trails the `0.45` average. Given the narrow `±0.5` percentage point threshold for fixed-income funds, this performance is firmly `In Line` with peers. The fund efficiently converts the credit risk of its underlying bonds into yield without severe uncompensated drag.

#### `worst_drawdown` — Pass

_The fund fell largely in line with similar credit portfolios during recent stress periods._

During the 2022 rate-and-spread shock, the ETF dropped `-14.86%`, taking 9 months to hit its trough. While this is slightly worse than the `-13.72%` peer average, it accurately reflects the asset class's systemic vulnerability rather than a fund-specific failure. A short-term maximum duration of just `2 Months` for its `-2.65%` three-year drawdown confirms that minor corrections are recovered swiftly.

#### `risk_vs_peers` — Fail

_The fund consistently subjects investors to above-average volatility without delivering better-than-average returns._

Morningstar rates the ETF's risk as `High` over a five-year window and `Above Avg.` over three years. Despite taking on this extra volatility relative to peers, the return profile remains stubbornly `Average` across both periods. Taking elevated risk without a compensatory boost in the return column is an inefficient tradeoff for retail investors holding this specific asset.

#### `credit_risk` — Pass

_As a high-yield portfolio, the fund inherently carries elevated default risk but behaves predictably during credit shocks._

Sub-investment grade bonds are highly sensitive to economic downturns, underscored by the ETF's `-25.26%` all-time drop in 2008. More recently, a five-year downside capture of `53` indicates it feels the pain of widening spreads more acutely than the category average of `38`. However, because these metrics accurately reflect the structural reality of the broad high-yield bond index it tracks, the fund meets its stated credit mandate.
