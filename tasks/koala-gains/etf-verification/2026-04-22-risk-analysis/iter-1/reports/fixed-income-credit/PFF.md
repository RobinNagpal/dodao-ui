# ETF Report — NASDAQ/PFF

- **Group:** Fixed Income — Credit & Income (`fixed-income-credit`)
- **Category:** Preferred Stock

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

The risk profile for PFF is Weak compared to its preferred stock peers. While its low beta to the broad equity market offers some diversification, the fund consistently exhibits higher volatility and deeper drawdowns than its category median. Across multiple timeframes, it delivers below-average returns while absorbing average to above-average risk. Ultimately, investors are taking on a bumpier ride without the risk-adjusted compensation to justify it.

### Overall analysis

Looking at standard volatility metrics, PFF's 5-year beta of `0.53` highlights its intended structural detachment from broad equities, functioning normally for a preferred stock allocation. However, when judged against its direct peers, the fund runs noticeably hot. Its 5-year standard deviation sits at `10.97`, materially exceeding the category median of `9.50`. This elevated volatility severely dilutes its risk-adjusted performance; the fund's 3-year Sharpe ratio is an anemic `0.09`, falling far short of the `0.56` category norm.

Drawdown and recovery behavior further expose the fund's vulnerabilities during structural stress. During the 2022 rate shock, PFF suffered a peak-to-valley drop of `-19.36%` between January and October. While preferreds generally suffered in this environment, PFF fell deeper than the category average's `-16.41%` decline. Furthermore, its 5-year downside capture ratio of `94` is substantially worse than the category's `66`, indicating the fund absorbs nearly all the market's negative momentum while peers successfully shield capital.

As a preferred stock fund, PFF sits at the intersection of interest-rate duration and subordinated credit risk. Its deeper losses in 2022 underscore a heightened sensitivity to combined rate-and-spread shocks compared to active or defensively positioned peers. Its 3-year upside capture ratio of `82` against a category median of `88` shows that when credit markets recover, the fund struggles to pace the group, resulting in a structural drag over full credit cycles.

Strengths include its broad equity decorrelation, anchored by a `0.25` 1-year beta, which provides ballast against pure stock market selloffs. However, the red flags are significant: a 10-year Morningstar risk rating of "Above Avg." paired with a "Below Avg." return profile, and a 10-year Sharpe ratio of `0.13` that lags the category's `0.24`. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks weak because it systematically subjects investors to heavier drawdowns and higher standard deviations than category peers without delivering the requisite upside compensation.

### Factor results

#### `overall_volatility` — Fail

_PFF experiences notably higher price swings than its preferred stock peers across multiple timeframes._

While PFF's beta indicates it moves less than the broad equity market, evaluating it against its bond-like preferred peers reveals elevated volatility. Over a 3-year period, the fund's standard deviation of `8.25` is significantly higher than the category median of `6.60`. This trend persists over the 5-year window, where PFF sits at `10.97` versus the category's `9.50`. Because the fund's structural volatility is materially higher than what is normal for its specific mandate, it fails this metric.

#### `risk_adjusted_return` — Fail

_The fund consistently fails to adequately compensate investors for the volatility it assumes._

PFF's ability to translate risk into return is poor relative to similar funds. Over the trailing 3-year window, the ETF generated a Sharpe ratio of `0.09`, radically trailing the category median of `0.56`. Over 5 years, its Sharpe ratio is negative at `-0.17`, again worse than the `-0.09` category mark. Investors are taking on more risk but yielding disproportionately lower excess returns.

#### `worst_drawdown` — Fail

_The fund suffered deeper peak-to-trough losses than its peers during recent rate shocks._

In the 2022 macroeconomic tightening cycle, PFF recorded a maximum drawdown of `-19.36%`. Applying the strict bounds for fixed-income and preferred mandates, this loss is notably worse than the category median drawdown of `-16.41%` over the same period. Since the ETF fell harder than its direct peers during a major stress test, it fails the comparative downside standard.

#### `risk_vs_peers` — Fail

_PFF carries average to above-average peer risk but routinely delivers below-average returns._

A core test for retail suitability is whether an ETF's risk position is rewarded. PFF's Morningstar profile shows a "Below Avg." return classification across 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year windows. Concurrently, its risk rating ranks as "Average" for 3 and 5 years, and "Above Avg." over 10 years. Taking on equal or greater peer risk without capturing a commensurate return premium is a structural failure.

#### `credit_risk` — Fail

_The fund's structural sensitivity to credit and rate shocks resulted in worse-than-category losses._

Preferred stocks carry inherent subordination risk, acting like bonds in rate hikes and equities in credit crises. PFF's `-19.36%` drawdown during the 2022 rate-and-spread shock highlights a vulnerability that exceeded the category average. Additionally, a 3-year downside capture ratio of `44` versus the category's `14` demonstrates that its underlying holdings bleed more capital during market stress than more actively risk-managed peers.
