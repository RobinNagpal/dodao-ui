# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/JEPI

- **Group:** Alternative Strategies (`alt-strategies`)
- **Category:** Derivative Income

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

JEPI's risk profile is Strong for its specific mandate. The fund successfully dampens broad market volatility with a 5-year beta of `0.59` and effectively limits losses, boasting a 5-year worst drawdown of `-13.12%` that easily beat its index. By delivering an asymmetric capture profile of `64%` upside against just `60%` downside, the ETF proves its covered-call strategy works exactly as intended. Overall, this ETF offers a well-protected, smoother ride for income-focused retail investors.

### Overall analysis

JEPI exhibits significantly lower volatility than the broader equity market, reflected in its 5-year beta of `0.59` and standard deviation of `10.13%`. This fits its Derivative Income mandate perfectly, as the strategy trades away extreme upside for yield and dampened variance. Its 5-year Sharpe ratio of `0.50` outperforms the category average of `0.40`, indicating that investors are being compensated reasonably well for the specific risks taken in this options-based framework compared to similar alternative funds.

During the 2022 rate shock, JEPI proved its structural resilience. Its maximum 5-year drawdown was contained to `-13.12%` (peaking in January 2022 and bottoming in September 2022), which was materially better than the category's `-16.72%` drop and roughly half of the broad index's `-24.88%` decline. Furthermore, Morningstar rates its multi-year risk as "Below Avg." while generating "Average" returns compared to peers, highlighting efficient loss mitigation without entirely sacrificing performance.

For a covered-call and derivative income strategy, asymmetric capture is the ultimate test. Over the past 5 years, JEPI has delivered a `64` upside capture and a `60` downside capture. While the fund's upside participation slightly trails the category average of `67`, it compensates with superior downside protection, catching less of the market's drops versus the category's `70`. In the 3-year window, downside capture remains at `60`, reinforcing that the options overlay is actively binding and providing the intended defensive properties against broad equity corrections.

Strengths include a highly protective maximum drawdown and a favorable Sortino ratio of `0.93`, showing controlled downside volatility. A potential risk is the constrained 3-year upside capture of `56`, meaning investors will heavily lag during sustained equity bull runs. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because it precisely executes its covered-call mandate, suppressing volatility and cushioning drawdowns while generating an above-average risk-adjusted return against its category peers.
