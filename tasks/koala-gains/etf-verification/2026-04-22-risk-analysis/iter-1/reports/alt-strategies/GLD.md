# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/GLD

- **Group:** Alternative Strategies (`alt-strategies`)
- **Category:** Commodities Focused

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

GLD's risk profile is Strong. The ETF acts as a clear portfolio diversifier, marked by a near-zero 5-year beta of `0.20` and highly asymmetric downside capture ratios, including a 5-year downside capture of `-34`. Its 3-year Sharpe ratio of `1.46` notably exceeds the Commodities Focused category average of `0.58`, showing superior risk-adjusted returns. The worst 10-year drawdown of `-17.34%` is materially milder than the broad index's `-30.34%` drop. Overall, this ETF delivers on its mandate by offering a low-correlation, high-protection profile that effectively cushions against equity market volatility.

### Overall analysis

GLD's volatility profile perfectly fits its safe-haven mandate, characterized by near-zero market correlation. The ETF's beta sits at a muted `0.08` over 1 year and `0.20` over 5 years, confirming that its daily and monthly price swings are decoupled from broad equity markets. Standard deviation is also well-controlled; over a 3-year window, the fund's `16.86` standard deviation is meaningfully lower than the Commodities Focused category average of `22.24`. The risk-adjusted return picture is stellar: the 3-year Sharpe ratio is `1.46` versus the category's `0.58`, and the trailing Sortino ratio is `2.176`, confirming that the volatility it does experience is skewed favorably toward the upside.

Drawdowns are well contained compared to both the category and broad market indices. Over the 10-year window, the fund's worst drawdown was `-17.34%` (occurring between August 2020 and October 2022). This is shallower than the category's `-18.56%` decline and substantially lighter than the broad index's `-30.34%` drop over the same extended period. The 5-year maximum drawdown of `-15.81%` closely mirrors the category average of `-16.02%`, reflecting the asset class's natural boundaries during the 2022 rate shock. Morningstar consistently rates the fund's risk versus its category as Low across 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year periods.

Viewed through the alternative strategies lens, GLD provides exceptional downside protection and capture asymmetry. Over a 5-year horizon, the fund captured `59` of the benchmark's upside but recorded a downside capture of `-34`. Over 3 years, the downside capture reached `-42` compared to the category's `42`. A negative downside capture indicates that the fund structurally gained ground during benchmark down-months. This fulfills the primary reason retail investors allocate to alternative strategies: genuine diversification that acts as a counter-weight when traditional assets sell off.

Strengths include the highly favorable negative downside capture (`-34` over 5 years) and a 10-year Sharpe ratio (`0.78`) that is double its category average (`0.38`). The low beta (`0.20`) provides hard evidence of strong decorrelation. One localized risk is the historically slow recovery time during cyclical lows; the fund's maximum drawdown took `27 Months` to reach its valley between 2020 and 2022, indicating vulnerability to prolonged periods of asset sluggishness. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because it successfully delivers non-correlated returns, materially lower volatility than its peers, and absolute downside protection during broader market stress.

### Factor results

#### `overall_volatility` — Pass

_GLD exhibits much lower standard deviation than its peers and a near-zero beta, perfectly aligning with its non-correlated safe-haven mandate._

The ETF's 5-year beta is `0.20` and its 1-year beta drops to `0.08`, demonstrating that its price swings are largely decoupled from the broad equity market. Its 3-year standard deviation of `16.86` is notably below the Commodities Focused category average of `22.24`. Morningstar assigns it a Low risk rating versus its category. Because the fund delivers the low-correlation, muted-volatility profile expected of a gold asset without taking on excessive internal risk, it passes comfortably.

#### `risk_adjusted_return` — Pass

_The fund produces outstanding risk-adjusted returns, consistently generating Sharpe ratios well above its category median across all measured timeframes._

Across every measured window, GLD rewards investors efficiently for the risk taken. Its 3-year Sharpe ratio of `1.46` is materially higher than the category average of `0.58`, and its 10-year Sharpe of `0.78` easily beats the category's `0.38`. A trailing Sortino ratio of `2.176` confirms that the volatility profile is heavily skewed toward upside gains rather than downside shocks. For an alternative asset class where Sharpe ratios are often dragged down during extended equity bull markets, these metrics are exceptionally strong.

#### `worst_drawdown` — Pass

_GLD's maximum historical drawdowns are shallower than both its category peers and the broad market benchmark._

Over a 10-year window, the ETF's maximum drawdown was `-17.34%`, which developed over a 27-month period between August 2020 and October 2022. This peak-to-trough drop is shallower than the category's `-18.56%` decline and significantly lighter than the index's `-30.34%` plunge. In the 3-year window, the fund's `-11.79%` drawdown also trailed behind the category's `-8.78%` drop, but over the long term, its absolute and comparative loss depths remain strictly within safe bounds for the Commodities Focused space.

#### `capture_ratios` — Pass

_The fund showcases a highly favorable, asymmetric capture profile, often gaining value when the broader benchmark declines._

Over the past 5 years, GLD captured `59` of the benchmark's upside but posted a remarkable downside capture of `-34`. Over 3 years, the downside capture reached `-42`, starkly contrasting the category average of `42`. This negative downside capture proves that the fund structurally acts as a counter-weight during market drops. It successfully satisfies the alternative-strategy mandate of providing decorrelated crisis hedging.

#### `downside_protection` — Pass

_GLD provides exceptional downside protection, effectively buffering portfolios during periods of broad market stress._

The fund's ability to protect capital during stress windows is clearly visible in its drawdown limits relative to the broader market. During the 2022 rate shock window, the fund's 5-year maximum drawdown of `-15.81%` stood against the index's `-22.48%` drop, representing a solid protection ratio of roughly `0.70`. Combined with its consistently negative downside capture ratios (`-9` over 10 years, `-34` over 5 years), GLD definitively fulfills its mandate to act as a portfolio hedge when traditional assets fall.
