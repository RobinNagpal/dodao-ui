# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/MUB

- **Group:** Municipal Bonds (`muni`)
- **Category:** Muni National Interm

## Risk Analysis

### Summary

MUB's risk profile is mixed to strong, anchored by highly stable long-term metrics but showing slight recent volatility relative to its peer group. As an intermediate municipal bond fund, it carries a conservative `15` risk score and low market correlation, with a 5-year beta of `0.25`. The fund weathered the 2022 rate shock effectively, posting an `-11.56%` maximum drawdown that was shallower than the `-12.33%` category average. Overall, MUB delivers the high-quality, tax-exempt ballast expected by retail investors, though its passive structure occasionally trails the risk-adjusted efficiency of actively managed peers in the short term.

### Overall analysis

MUB functions as a core municipal bond holding, sporting a predictably low 5-year beta of `0.25` and a conservative Morningstar risk score of `15`. Over the trailing five years, the fund realized a standard deviation of `5.73%`, slightly above the `5.47%` category average but fundamentally aligned with a low-volatility mandate. Due to the structurally compressed returns of tax-exempt bonds over the recent rate cycle, trailing Sharpe ratios are negative across the board. MUB posted a 5-year Sharpe of `-0.45`, roughly matching the `-0.47` category median, alongside a recent short-term Sortino of `1.38`. The volatility perfectly fits the mandate of a broad, investment-grade tax-exempt allocation.

Drawdown history highlights how the fund behaves in fixed-income stress windows. During the severe 2022 interest rate shock (spanning August 2021 to October 2022), MUB experienced its worst 5-year drawdown of `-11.56%`. Crucially, this loss was structurally driven by the broader asset class rather than fund-specific flaws, and MUB actually suffered a shallower drop than the `-12.33%` average loss of its intermediate muni peers. Over longer horizons (5-year and 10-year), its risk and return both sit at "Average" versus the category, though the 3-year window flags a minor divergence where MUB took "Above Average" risk for "Below Average" return.

For a Muni National Interm fund, the primary risk drivers are interest rate duration and municipal credit quality. The ETF sits squarely in the "High" credit quality and "Moderate" interest rate sensitivity style box, meaning default risk is negligible and price volatility is dictated almost entirely by Treasury and municipal yield curves. Its 5-year downside capture ratio of `89` (versus the category's `85`) indicates it absorbs slightly more of the benchmark's downside drops than active peers, yet its actual peak-to-trough loss limits during the 2022 rate spike proved highly resilient.

Strengths include the fund's conservative credit posture and a 5-year worst drawdown (`-11.56%`) that beat the category average by `0.77` percentage points. The primary risk flag is recent relative efficiency: over the last 3 years, the fund's standard deviation crept up to `5.08%` (versus peers at `4.71%`), leading to a weaker 3-year Sharpe of `-0.42` against the category's `-0.35`. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because it reliably delivers the high-quality, moderate-duration exposure its label promises, keeping severe drawdowns tightly bounded within category norms.

### Factor results

#### `risk_adjusted_return` — Pass

_MUB delivers risk-adjusted returns directly in line with category averages over long horizons, though recent years have shown a slight drag._

In the municipal bond space, Sharpe ratios are structurally compressed. MUB's 5-year Sharpe of `-0.45` and 10-year Sharpe of `-0.06` sit comfortably within the `±0.5` point band of the category averages (`-0.47` and `-0.08`, respectively). However, the 3-year window shows slight underperformance, with a Sharpe of `-0.42` trailing the category's `-0.35`, driven by a marginally higher standard deviation (`5.08%` vs `4.71%`). Despite this near-term lag, the long-term risk-adjusted metrics successfully track the intermediate municipal index without taking uncompensated risks.

#### `worst_drawdown` — Pass

_The fund's maximum drawdown during the 2022 rate shock was noticeably shallower than the category average._

Interest rate spikes are the primary driver of drawdowns for intermediate muni funds. MUB's worst 5-year drawdown occurred between August 2021 and October 2022, bottoming out at `-11.56%`. This compares favorably to the `-12.33%` maximum drawdown suffered by its category peers, showing a `0.77` percentage point advantage. While a double-digit drop in a conservative bond allocation is uncomfortable for retail investors, the loss was purely asset-class driven, and MUB's structural design insulated holders slightly better than the average competitor.

#### `risk_vs_peers` — Pass

_MUB perfectly matches peer risk levels over 5- and 10-year windows, though it flags slightly higher volatility over the 3-year period._

Over the 5-year and 10-year periods, Morningstar rates both MUB's risk and return as exactly "Average" compared to the Muni National Interm category, validating its role as a reliable passive benchmark. Over the 3-year window, it slips slightly, categorized as taking "Above Avg." risk while delivering "Below Avg." return. This is reflected in a 3-year standard deviation of `5.08%` versus the category's `4.71%`. Since passive funds often trail the median of a category where active managers can defensively shorten duration, this minor near-term drift does not break the fund's long-term utility.

#### `interest_rate_sensitivity` — Pass

_Positioned as a moderate-duration fund, MUB handled the extreme 2022 rate shock better than its average category peer._

MUB falls into the "Moderate" interest rate sensitivity style box, meaning its price responds directly to intermediate yield curve shifts. When the rate shock hit in 2022, MUB fell `-11.56%`, outperforming the `-12.33%` drop of its category peers. Over 5 years, it has a downside capture ratio of `89`, closely mirroring the broader municipal benchmark's risk path. The fact that its heaviest losses in the worst modern bond bear market stayed strictly bounded within (and slightly better than) peer norms confirms it is executing its duration mandate safely.

#### `credit_risk` — Pass

_With a portfolio anchored in high-quality municipal issuers, MUB carries negligible default risk and prioritizes capital preservation._

Operating in the "High" credit quality style box, MUB invests primarily in investment-grade state and local debt. During the 2020 COVID credit dislocation (reflected in an all-time high drop of `-9.92%` around March 2020), it experienced standard liquidity-driven pricing pressure but no structural impairment. Its overall Morningstar risk score of `15` and "Conservative" risk level confirm that credit quality is not a hidden danger here. The fund does not reach for yield down the credit spectrum, strictly limiting its volatility to interest rate duration rather than default risk.
