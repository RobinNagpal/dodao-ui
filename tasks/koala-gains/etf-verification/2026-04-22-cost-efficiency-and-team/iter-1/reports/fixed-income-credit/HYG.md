# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/HYG

- **Group:** Fixed Income — Credit & Income (`fixed-income-credit`)
- **Category:** High Yield Bond

## Cost, Efficiency & Team

### Summary

The cost and efficiency profile for the iShares iBoxx $ High Yield Corporate Bond ETF is Mixed. On the positive side, the fund boasts a massive `$16.5B` in AUM and trades with deep liquidity, exchanging over `23.1M` shares daily. It also benefits from solid continuity, with an average manager tenure of `5.4` years. However, its `0.49%` expense ratio acts as a meaningful drag for a passive beta strategy. Overall, while execution and operational stability are top-tier, the ongoing holding costs keep it from earning a purely strong rating for retail investors.

### Overall analysis

The fund's expense ratio sits at the expensive end for a passive index tracker, as modern High Yield Bond alternatives have compressed pricing well below this level. There is no discrepancy between the stated and net prospectus fees, indicating a clean fee structure. From an execution standpoint, the ETF offers massive scale, marked by `206.2M` shares outstanding and an average daily dollar volume of `$1.84B`. However, because underlying high-yield debt is inherently less liquid, this friction mechanically passes through to the secondary market, resulting in a `0.27%` bid-ask spread that makes frequent round-trip trading slightly costly. Moving to trading efficiency, the portfolio turnover of `14%` aligns perfectly with a passive, rules-based approach, minimizing internal friction and avoiding unnecessary tax drag for taxable accounts. For the fixed-income-credit group, however, net asset value execution is a vital lens for hidden costs. ETFs holding illiquid corporate bonds frequently trade at a premium or discount to their true asset value. Because these bonds trade over-the-counter and less frequently, the fund's market price acts as a real-time discovery tool, meaning the deviation can temporarily blow out beyond `2%` during extreme credit market stress—such as March 2020—before rapidly normalizing. This behavior is expected, but retail investors should be cautious with market orders during risk-off events. Operationally, the fund holds a proven track record. Managed by BlackRock, the ETF launched in April `2007`, meaning it has successfully navigated multiple severe credit cycles. The management team features `3` distinct managers with a longest tenure reaching `14.8` years, providing continuity. Although manager tenure is less critical for a passive strategy, this stability ensures zero closure risk. Overall, the ETF's main strengths lie in its dominant market presence and a deep portfolio of `1,325` distinct holdings that has been stress-tested over a `19`-year history. The primary risks are structural: the headline fee creates a persistent performance headwind against cheaper peers, and the secondary market execution spreads penalize tactical traders. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks mixed because its strong operational stability and robust liquidity are offset by above-average expenses for basic fixed-income beta.

### Factor results

#### `expense_ratio` — Fail

_The fund's 0.49% expense ratio is structurally uncompetitive against modern passive high-yield alternatives._

While a `0.49%` fee is not unusual for older, legacy high-yield products, it is materially higher than what retail investors should pay for passive index beta today. Since the fund seeks only to track a benchmark rather than generate active alpha, this fee acts as a persistent, unearned drag on compounding returns. The cost fails the passive value test, as identical exposure can be found in peer ETFs for less than half the price.

#### `fund_size_liquidity` — Pass

_Massive asset scale and intense daily trading volume guarantee tight, reliable execution under normal conditions._

With `$16.5B` in AUM and `$1.84B` in average daily dollar volume, this ETF sits far above any closure-risk thresholds. The `0.27%` market bid-ask spread is perfectly within normal bounds for the high-yield fixed-income category, accurately reflecting the inherent friction of the underlying corporate bond market. Retail investors can trade standard lot sizes without facing any significant slippage.

#### `portfolio_turnover` — Pass

_The fund maintains a very low internal turnover rate, which successfully limits hidden transaction costs and prevents unnecessary tax drag on investor returns._

Evaluating exactly how often a fund trades its internal holdings is crucial for understanding hidden, unlisted costs. This ETF reliably reports an `overviewTurnover` of just `14%` (which perfectly matches its `reportedTurnoverPct` of `14.00%`). This measurement falls comfortably well within the low operational range of `0-30%`, demonstrating significant trading restraint by the BlackRock management team. Low turnover is especially critical in the high-yield corporate bond space, where physically buying and selling the underlying fixed-income securities can involve steep dealer markups and wide transaction spreads. By holding its internal bonds steadily until maturity or structural index changes, the fund successfully minimizes transaction friction and noticeably reduces the likelihood of passing unwanted capital gains taxes onto its shareholders. This highly efficient, cost-conscious approach easily supports a passing grade.
