Management continuity is similarly robust. PFF is overseen by a four-person management team, featuring an impressive longest tenure of `13.7 years` from Jennifer Hsui. This deep bench ensures that mandate continuity is preserved and the passive indexing strategy is executed without style drift.

The fund’s primary strengths are anchored by its massive `$13.4 billion` scale, highly liquid `$73 million` daily dollar volume, and cycle-tested track record dating back to `2007`. Its main operational advantage is providing zero-slippage retail execution for a relatively illiquid asset class. On the risk side, while `0.45%` is in line with or cheaper than category peers, it remains an elevated structural cost compared to plain-vanilla passive indices, meaning investors pay a slight premium for this niche exposure. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks strong because it delivers category-leading liquidity, extremely low trading friction, and seasoned management oversight for the preferred stock market.
