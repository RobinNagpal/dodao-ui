# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/AOK

- **Group:** Allocation & Target-Date (`allocation-target-date`)
- **Category:** Conservative Allocation

## Cost, Efficiency & Team

### Summary

The cost and efficiency profile for AOK is strong. The fund charges a competitive `0.15%` expense ratio while managing over `$744M` in assets. Trading efficiency is supported by a tight `0.02%` bid-ask spread and a highly tax-efficient `3.00%` turnover rate. For retail investors, this ETF provides a structurally cheap and liquid way to hold a multi-asset conservative portfolio.

### Overall analysis

The fund charges a `0.15%` expense ratio, which falls well within the typical `0.10%` to `0.50%` range for allocation and target-date funds. It currently holds roughly `$744M` in assets under management, keeping it safely above industry closure risk thresholds. On the secondary market, the ETF trades with an average daily dollar volume of roughly `$4.3M` alongside a very narrow `0.02%` bid-ask spread. This combination of low holding costs and tight execution spreads ensures that retail investors can complete a round-trip trade cheaply.

As a fund of funds tracking the S&P Target Risk Conservative Index, the underlying portfolio experiences minimal operational churn, evidenced by its `3.00%` reported turnover rate. For the Global Conservative Allocation category, low turnover limits internal trading friction and capital gains distributions, keeping tax drag in check for taxable accounts. Because the ETF leverages an all-in-one allocation structure, the value-for-money test requires assessing whether its fee justifies the structural convenience. At `0.15%`, the pricing offers strong structural value compared to standard active allocation options that frequently charge above `0.60%`.

BlackRock acts as the issuer for this strategy, providing institutional-grade operational scale. The management team has a proven history of continuity, led by a primary manager with a `13.3`-year tenure on the fund. Launched in `2008`, the ETF possesses a track record exceeding `15` years, meaning the core strategy has navigated multiple distinct market environments and interest rate cycles while maintaining a stable mandate.

Key strengths include the highly competitive `0.15%` fee and the `0.02%` bid-ask spread, which minimize both long-term holding and immediate trading costs. The `13.3`-year manager tenure provides operational confidence that is relatively rare in multi-asset allocation sleeves. One minor constraint is the `$4.3M` daily dollar volume; while plenty sufficient for retail trades, it indicates that larger institutional block orders might require limit execution to avoid slipping the spread. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks strong because it delivers a consistently rebalanced multi-asset portfolio at a price point normally reserved for single-asset passive funds.
