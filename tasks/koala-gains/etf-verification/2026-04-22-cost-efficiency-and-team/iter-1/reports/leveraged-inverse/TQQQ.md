# ETF Report — NASDAQ/TQQQ

- **Group:** Leveraged & Inverse Trading (`leveraged-inverse`)
- **Category:** Trading--Leveraged Equity

## Cost, Efficiency & Team

### Summary

TQQQ's cost and efficiency profile is **Strong** for its specialized category, though it requires strict understanding of leveraged mechanics. With an expense ratio of `0.82%`, it is priced competitively against other leveraged equity funds, while its massive `$25.4 billion` in AUM and razor-thin `0.02%` bid-ask spread provide pristine liquidity. However, retail investors must recognize that embedded swap financing costs sit entirely outside the headline fee, making it highly efficient as a short-term trading tool but prohibitively expensive for long-term holds.

### Overall analysis

The ETF charges an expense ratio of `0.82%`, which is firmly **In Line** with the standard benchmark expectations for leveraged equity products. From an execution standpoint, liquidity is exceptional. Backed by `$25.4 billion` in assets under management and an average daily dollar volume of `$2.55 billion`, retail and institutional investors alike can clear large orders with virtually zero market impact. This extreme liquidity translates into a remarkably tight `0.02%` market bid-ask spread, ensuring that round-trip transactions are highly cost-efficient.

While the reported portfolio turnover is exceptionally low at `25%`, this figure obscures the true internal trading dynamics. As a Trading--Leveraged Equity vehicle targeting three times the daily return of the NASDAQ-100, the fund relies heavily on over-the-counter swaps to secure its exposure. These swap agreements carry embedded financing costs—typically tied to prevailing short-term interest rates—that sit completely outside the headline expense ratio. When these hidden financing layers are combined with the mechanical friction of daily rebalancing, the all-in cost to hold this fund for an extended period significantly outpaces the stated sticker fee.

Issued by ProShares, a dominant pioneer in the leveraged and inverse ETF landscape, the fund benefits from deep institutional expertise in complex derivative management. The ETF boasts a seasoned track record, having operated continuously since its inception in `February 2010`. The management team is exceptionally stable for this category, featuring an average tenure of `10.3 years` alongside a longest-serving manager who has guided the portfolio for `12.5 years`. This longevity, paired with steady multi-billion-dollar scale, effectively neutralizes closure risk and guarantees mandate continuity.

TQQQ's primary strengths are its tremendous scale and flawless trading execution, evidenced by billions in daily turnover and a two-basis-point bid-ask spread. The foremost risk is structural: the true cost of exposure is much higher than the headline fee due to invisible swap financing rates and daily rebalancing drag, which quietly erode capital over time. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks strong because it flawlessly delivers its complex daily mandate with industry-leading liquidity, provided it is strictly utilized as a tactical instrument rather than a long-term investment.

### Factor results

#### `expense_ratio` — Pass

_The fund's pricing aligns perfectly with the standard norms for leveraged equity ETFs._

At `0.82%`, the fund's expense ratio falls neatly into the standard `0.80%` to `1.15%` band expected for products utilizing complex derivatives for daily leverage. Both the adjusted and prospectus net expense ratios sit identically at `0.82%`, confirming there are no temporary fee waivers masking the true management cost. While significantly more expensive than a traditional passive benchmark index fund, this fee represents fair compensation for the operational complexity of managing a targeted daily multiple. Still, investors must remember this figure only reflects management compensation, not the fund's total carrying costs.

#### `fund_size_liquidity` — Pass

_Massive scale and pristine execution metrics make this one of the most liquid trading vehicles on the market._

With an enormous `$25.4 billion` in AUM, the fund easily clears any closure-risk thresholds and firmly anchors itself as an industry titan. Its trading efficiency is unmatched, featuring an average daily dollar volume of `$2.55 billion` and share volume well over `113 million` shares. This extreme market depth translates into a razor-tight `0.02%` bid-ask spread. For retail traders, market impact and slippage are practically nonexistent, guaranteeing cheap round-trip trade executions even during periods of elevated volatility.

#### `portfolio_turnover` — Pass

_The reported turnover is unusually low for a leveraged fund, reflecting heavy reliance on swap contracts rather than physical stock trading._

Leveraged funds often exhibit turnover rates exceeding `1000%` due to the mechanical daily resets required to maintain their target leverage. TQQQ's reported `25%` portfolio turnover is a byproduct of its structural methodology, heavily utilizing total return swaps (such as its prominent Nasdaq 100 Index Swap positions) to gain exposure. Because the strategy is executed via these specialized derivatives rather than constantly buying and selling the underlying physical equities, the low reported turnover is completely normal and efficient for its specific mandate.

#### `fund_track_record_and_stability` — Pass

_Backed by ProShares since 2010, the fund offers a bulletproof operational history and highly tenured management._

Operating successfully since `February 2010`, the fund boasts a mature 16-year track record of navigating diverse market environments. ProShares is a premier issuer in the leveraged space, supplying profound institutional backing. The portfolio management team showcases extraordinary stability, with an average tenure of `10.3 years` and the lead manager boasting `12.5 years` at the helm. Combined with its massive asset base, the fund presents zero continuity concerns.

#### `leverage_cost_drag` — Pass

_Execution metrics are flawless for short-term trading, but embedded swap financing costs make long holds expensive._

The fund efficiently delivers its daily mandate, but its actual carrying costs extend far beyond the `0.82%` expense ratio. To achieve triple daily leverage, the issuer utilizes customized swap agreements that contain embedded financing costs tied to prevailing overnight interest rates—acting as a persistent, invisible drag on the NAV. Furthermore, the daily reset mechanism introduces structural volatility drag that compounds negatively. Because the fund boasts top-tier liquidity (a `0.02%` spread) and its daily-targeted nature is transparent, it clearly succeeds as a structured trading instrument. However, retail investors must understand that holding it for multiple weeks embeds silent but significant financial drag.
