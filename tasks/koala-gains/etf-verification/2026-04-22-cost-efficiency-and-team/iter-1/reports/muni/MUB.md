# ETF Report — NYSEARCA/MUB

- **Group:** Municipal Bonds (`muni`)
- **Category:** Muni National Interm

## Cost, Efficiency & Team

### Summary

iShares National Muni Bond ETF (MUB) presents a strong cost and efficiency profile for retail investors seeking tax-exempt income. The fund charges a low 0.05% expense ratio, operating effectively as a low-cost tracking floor for the Muni National Interm category. With $42.9B in assets and a tight 0.01% bid-ask spread, execution costs are minimized. The portfolio maintains a stable 17% turnover rate and a long track record dating back to 2007. Overall, this ETF's cost profile is strong because it pairs massive scale and liquidity with one of the lowest fees in the municipal bond space.

### Overall analysis

The iShares National Muni Bond ETF operates as a highly cost-effective vehicle within the Muni National Interm category, charging a stated expense ratio of 0.05%. The data shows the adjusted and prospectus net expense ratios match this figure, confirming there are no temporary fee waivers masking a higher baseline cost. While broad equity index funds sometimes price under 0.03%, this pricing sits substantially below the typical 0.10% to 0.40% range for municipal bond funds, securing its place as a Strong option for retail exposure. The issuer backs this structural advantage with significant scale, managing roughly $42.9B in assets under management. Market execution remains highly efficient; the fund trades heavily with an average daily dollar volume of $260.6M and a daily share volume exceeding 2,448,550. This deep liquidity drives the market bid-ask spread down to a tight 0.01%. Retail investors can execute trades in and out of this fund without facing meaningful round-trip friction, meaning a standard trade loses virtually nothing to market-maker slippage. This execution quality is a vital feature given the over-the-counter nature of the underlying fixed-income market. Moving to trading efficiency and group-specific cost lenses, the portfolio maintains a stable internal structure, reporting a 17% turnover rate that aligns with its passive mandate tracking the ICE AMT-Free US National Municipal Index. For taxable-account investors, the tax-efficiency framing is the critical lens for evaluating this category. The fund's income is explicitly exempt from federal income taxes and the alternative minimum tax. Evaluated at a standard 32% federal tax bracket, the tax-equivalent yield provides a structural premium over similarly rated taxable core bonds. The strategy captures this advantage through broad diversification, holding 6,346 individual municipal bonds and 86 other holdings. The portfolio avoids concentrating in high-yield debt or isolated single-state risk, instead holding widely dispersed, high-quality issues such as Texas University Revenues (0.20% weight), Atlanta Water and Wastewater Revenues (0.19% weight), and New York State Thruway Authority debt (0.16% weight). Coupon rates on these top holdings range from 3.55% to 5.25%, generating steady cash flow. By spreading its exposure across thousands of issues where individual positions are small, the strategy secures its tax-exempt yield without introducing severe credit risk. Regarding the team and issuer maturity, managed by BlackRock under the iShares banner, the fund benefits from an established fixed-income indexing operation. The ETF boasts a deep operational history with an inception date of Sep 07, 2007, demonstrating nearly two decades of mandate continuity and steady asset accumulation. Although it operates as a passive index fund where individual manager influence is structurally limited, the vehicle features an experienced oversight team. The three-person management roster is anchored by James J. Mauro, who has been on the fund since Jul 01, 2011, providing a longest tenure of 14.8 Years. He is joined by Jonathan Graves and Marcus Tom, who were added to the roster in 2025. This brings the average tenure to 5.4 Years. This combination of a premier issuer, deeply tenured lead oversight, and continuous team refreshment mitigates concerns regarding operational instability, strategy drift, or execution errors in the replication process. Finally, evaluating strengths and risks, the strengths of the fund are concentrated in its pure cost efficiency and market footprint: a bottom-tier fee structure and a category-leading asset base ensure long-term viability and low tracking drag. The negligible bid-ask spread and low annual turnover further shield investors from hidden trading and frictional costs, directly preserving the yield generated by the portfolio. The fund's broad basket of over six thousand bonds mitigates default risk, leaving no notable red flags in the operational or cost profile. The lack of concentrated maturity risk—with top bonds maturing between 2026 and 2055—adds another layer of structural stability. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks strong because it provides frictionless, low-priced access to the investment-grade municipal bond market backed by an established issuer.
