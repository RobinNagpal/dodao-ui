## Report

ETF YYY exhibits higher volatility than typical peers in the Tactical Allocation group. The fund's ten-year standard deviation sits at `13.7%`, noticeably higher than the benchmark's `10.1%`. Despite this elevated volatility, it generally compensates investors over longer cycles, as seen in a ten-year Sharpe ratio of `0.49`, which sits above the category average of `0.43`. The overall beta of `0.73` suggests it is somewhat less volatile than a pure equity index, but its internal swings remain wider than most blended peers. A Sortino ratio of `1.00` indicates that much of its volatility has skewed positive rather than translating entirely into downside variance.

When tested by acute market stress, the fund's downside protection varies significantly by period. During shorter trailing windows, it has held up reasonably well, posting a three-year maximum drawdown of `-6.8%`, which proved slightly better than the category's `-7.4%`. Yet, in deeper historical stress like the `2020 COVID` crash, the fund logged much steeper losses. Its risk positioning reflects this dual nature; over the trailing three-year period, Morningstar scores its risk versus category as `Average` while delivering `Above Avg.` returns, whereas its ten-year lens rates its peer-relative risk as `Above Avg.`, confirming its long-term posture is more aggressive than a standard tactical mix.

As a fund of closed-end funds (CEFs), this ETF carries distinct structural risks tied to closed-end market discounts and embedded leverage. In rising rate environments like the `2022 rate shock`, the underlying fixed-income and leveraged CEF components faced heavy pressure, leading to an `11 Months` drawdown duration. This "fund of funds" structure introduces overlapping layers of volatility, meaning the portfolio absorbs both the net asset value declines of its holdings and the widening of their market price discounts. Short-term momentum indicators like a 14-day RSI of `42` remain neutral, but tactical CEF allocations require monitoring for underlying leverage sensitivity rather than simple daily momentum.

The fund's primary strength is its recent ability to outpace category risk-adjusted metrics, highlighted by a three-year upside capture of `98` paired with a favorable downside capture of `81` versus the benchmark's `100`. Conversely, its historical downside capture during older market cycles and its larger historical drawdowns serve as distinct red flags for risk-averse allocators. Additionally, a five-year beta of `1.08` against the benchmark shows it amplifies benchmark moves rather than buffering them. Given its reliance on inherently leveraged closed-end funds, this strategy fits better as a satellite yield-enhancement slice rather than a core portfolio anchor. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks mixed because its strong recent downside protection masks longer-term structural vulnerability to credit and rate shocks.
