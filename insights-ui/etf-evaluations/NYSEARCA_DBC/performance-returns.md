# DBC (NYSEARCA) — Performance & Returns

_The Invesco DB Commodity Index Tracking Fund (DBC) currently displays a fiercely strong short-term performance profile, though its long-term historical track record remains decidedly mixed. Over the past year, the ETF generated a massive `45.80%` return, easily beating its category by `2.49` percentage points and signaling extreme upward momentum. However, long-term investors should note its intense volatility, extremely overbought weekly RSI of `84.19`, and weak relative performance over the 3-year window. Overall, while the ETF is currently thriving and offers an excellent low-correlation asset with a beta of `0.10`, its history of boom-and-bust cycles means it requires careful timing rather than blind "buy-and-hold" trust._

- **Short Term Returns** _(result: Pass)_: The ETF has delivered explosive short-term growth, marked by consecutive double-digit gains across all recent measurement periods.
- **Returns Consistency** _(result: Fail)_: The fund is highly cyclical, enduring dramatic boom-and-bust periods that destroy its long-term consistency.
- **Category Peer Standing** _(result: Fail)_: The ETF's standing among its category peers fluctuates heavily, demonstrating periods of both leadership and notable underperformance.
- **Income Vs Price Return** _(result: Pass)_: The fund generates a meaningful yield buffer from its cash collateral and rolling futures, boosting total return above pure price appreciation.

## Report

The Invesco DB Commodity Index Tracking Fund has recently performed exceptionally well, stringing together a series of powerful short-term gains that easily overshadow typical market returns. From a relative perspective, the ETF is currently beating both its peer category and its primary benchmark over the past year, though its standing becomes much more mixed when looking over a three-year horizon. Technical indicators are currently flashing intense strength, showcasing a dominant, steep uptrend that is heavily supported by moving averages. However, this explosive upward movement has pushed momentum metrics into highly overbought territory, suggesting that while the immediate picture is incredibly strong, the asset is running very hot right now.

Looking closer at the recent return picture, the ETF has delivered aggressive and accelerating growth across every short-term period. It posted a 1-month return of `11.43%`, a 3-month return of `29.55%`, and an even more impressive 6-month return of `36.30%`. Year-to-date, the fund is already up `32.16%`, which culminates in a spectacular 1-year total return of `45.80%`. These numbers indicate that the ETF is not just experiencing a short-term spike, but rather a persistent and broadening surge in momentum. For retail investors, returns of this magnitude over a single year are extremely high, signaling that the underlying commodity markets tracked by this fund are in a state of rapid appreciation. However, such aggressive vertical moves rarely happen without subsequent cooling-off periods.

When shifting focus to medium- and long-term compounding, the ETF reveals a much more complicated wealth-creation story. Over a 3-year period, the fund achieved a solid Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of `11.75%`, which improved to a `15.34%` CAGR over 5 years, and a respectable `10.53%` CAGR over the past decade. These metrics suggest that investors who bought in a decade ago have seen solid, double-digit annual wealth compounding. However, zooming out further exposes the inherent cyclicality of broad commodities. The 15-year CAGR crashes down to just `0.80%`, and the 20-year CAGR is a mere `2.21%`. This dramatic drop-off highlights that the fund suffered through a grueling "lost decade" prior to its recent multi-year resurgence. Therefore, while medium-term compounding looks highly attractive today, the fund's ultra-long-term record is highly uneven and susceptible to prolonged periods of stagnation.

Evaluating relative performance against the category and benchmark provides crucial context on how the fund's strategy actually stacks up. Over the trailing 1-year period, the ETF posted a `36.40%` Net Asset Value (NAV) return compared to the category average of `33.91%` and the index return of `32.93%`. This places the ETF ABOVE its category by `2.49` percentage points and ABOVE its benchmark by `3.47` percentage points, which qualifies as strong short-term outperformance. However, over the 3-year trailing period, the fund returned `9.84%` annualized, while the category delivered `12.36%` and the index delivered `11.91%`. This means the ETF fell BELOW its category by a wide `2.52` percentage points and BELOW its benchmark by `2.07` percentage points, a definitively weak showing for that specific stretch. Over the 5-year period, the fund earned `13.21%` against the category's `13.16%` and the index's `12.62%`, leaving it firmly IN LINE with both. Similarly, over the 10-year period, the fund's `9.43%` return versus the category's `8.45%` and the index's `7.52%` means it performed basically IN LINE over a decade. Ultimately, while the fund is adding solid value today, it is prone to periods of meaningful underperformance during shifting commodity environments.

The technical and momentum position of the ETF confirms a massive and sustained uptrend, though one that is stretching its limits. With a current price of `29.58`, the ETF is trading significantly above its key moving averages. It sits `12.55%` above its 50-day moving average (`26.25`) and `25.93%` above its 200-day moving average (`23.46`), illustrating absolute dominance by buyers over the medium and long term. Furthermore, it is sitting just pennies away from its 52-week high of `29.60`. However, the Relative Strength Index (RSI) warns of extreme heat. While the daily RSI is elevated but manageable at `66.68`, the weekly RSI has reached a blistering `84.19`, and the monthly RSI sits at `75.83`. In simple terms, RSI values above 70 indicate that an asset is "overbought" and may have risen too far, too fast. While strong momentum supports the recent gains, retail investors should view these RSI levels as a cautionary sign that the market is currently crowded and stretched.

From a risk, volatility, and size standpoint, this ETF behaves very differently from a standard stock fund. Its beta of `0.10` indicates that it has virtually zero correlation with the broader equity markets. This makes it an incredibly powerful diversification tool, as its price movements are largely detached from the daily swings of standard stock indices. The fund is heavily traded and comfortably sized, boasting `$1.67 billion` in total assets and an average daily trading volume of roughly `667,982` shares. This guarantees excellent liquidity for ordinary investors looking to buy or sell without facing large pricing spreads. However, the internal volatility remains high. A 52-week trading range spanning from a low of `19.84` to a high of `29.60` shows that the fund's unit price can move dramatically. With a concentrated portfolio of just `39` futures-based holdings representing various natural resources, the return profile is entirely dependent on global supply-and-demand shocks rather than corporate earnings.

In conclusion, the ETF possesses clear strengths, but also distinct red flags. Its biggest strengths are its massive 1-year absolute return of `45.80%`, its strong 5-year compounding rate of `15.34%`, and its ability to beat its benchmark by `3.47` percentage points over the last 12 months. Its primary risks include a weak 3-year track record where it trailed its category by `2.52` percentage points, a highly overbought weekly RSI of `84.19`, and a historical pattern of massive multi-year drawdowns. Overall, this ETF's performance profile looks mixed; it is an outstanding short-term momentum play and a great portfolio diversifier, but its erratic long-term consistency makes it less dependable as a core, "set-and-forget" holding.
