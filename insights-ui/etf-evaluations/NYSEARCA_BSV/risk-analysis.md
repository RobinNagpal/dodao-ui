# BSV (NYSEARCA) — Risk Analysis

_The risk profile for ETF BSV is Mixed. It offers a very low 5-year equity market beta of 0.09 (lower than the equity market baseline), but its 5-year Sharpe ratio of -0.69 sits below the Short-Term Bond category average of -0.52. The fund experienced a worst 5-year drawdown of -7.8% (worse than the category average of -7.3%), and Morningstar rates its risk profile as Above Avg. (meaning it takes more risk than the typical peer). It is a highly liquid, conservative income sleeve best suited for investors seeking to park cash with slightly more yield than a money market fund, though it remains vulnerable to short-term interest rate shifts._

## Report

The fund delivers the low price volatility expected of a short-duration mandate, carrying a 5-year standard deviation of 2.9 (higher than the category average of 2.6). Its 10-year Sharpe ratio sits at -0.17, lower than the peer group median of 0.00. Because this is a passive index tracker, it fully absorbs market volatility, resulting in a risk-adjusted return that slightly lags active peers who can defensively adjust their holdings during weak environments. Still, the volatility profile fits the stated mandate of a conservative fixed-income allocation without carrying equity-like downside. When evaluating historical stress, the fund's worst multi-year drop occurred during the 2022 rate shock, falling from 08/01/2021 to 10/31/2022. Over a 5-year window, it exhibits a downside capture ratio of 36, which is higher than the category norm of 23. This dynamic explains why the fund's multi-year risk metrics look elevated compared to peers, while its return vs category ranks as Below Avg. (lower than typical peers). Passive short-term bond funds often look somewhat weaker on peer-relative downside simply because they cannot shorten duration to hide from rising rates the way active managers can. Interest-rate sensitivity is the single dominant macro driver for this portfolio. While the one-to-five year maturity constraint restricts the large duration risk found in long-term bonds (which routinely lost 25.0% in the same cycle, far worse than this fund's short-term limits), it is not immune to aggressive Federal Reserve hiking cycles. The portfolio is built entirely of U.S. government and high-quality corporate credit, so structural risks like credit drift, reaching into high-yield segments, or opaque yield-smoothing are not present here. The structural headwind is purely the drag of continuous bond rolling and fee costs in a low-return asset class. The fund's core strength is its role as an equity diversifier; a 1-year beta of -0.02 (lower than the broad equity baseline) shows it provides pure decorrelation during stock market swings. Additionally, its maximum historical losses remain comfortably within the single digits, providing a stable floor for a broader portfolio. The main red flag is its peer-relative drag: despite a 5-year upside capture of 54 (above the category's 49), the strategy structurally absorbs more rate pain than flexible active alternatives. When compared to a prime money market fund, this short-term bond ETF carries real principal risk during rate hikes, but offers the benefit of locking in yields for longer periods. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks mixed because it successfully delivers a low-volatility bond exposure, but structurally captures slightly more downside than active category peers during acute rate shocks.
