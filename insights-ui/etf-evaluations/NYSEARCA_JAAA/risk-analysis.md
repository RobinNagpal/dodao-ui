# JAAA (NYSEARCA) — Risk Analysis

_This ETF presents a Strong risk profile for the fixed-income-core category. It delivers a 3-year Sharpe ratio of `2.28`, better than the securitized bond category average of `1.60`. Over the same period, its standard deviation was a notably low `0.9%`, well below the category's `2.2%`. The fund also registered a 3-year maximum drawdown of just `-0.1%`, significantly outperforming the index's `-7.0%` drop. This makes it a highly effective capital-preservation sleeve for conservative portfolios seeking to avoid duration-driven losses._

- **Worst Drawdown** _(result: Pass)_: The strategy successfully sidesteps the deep losses that periodically hit the broader bond market.

## Report

Looking at multi-year volatility and risk-adjusted return, the portfolio provides a very calm ride. Its 5-year standard deviation sits at `1.6%`, lower than the broader index's `7.0%`. It paired this low volatility with a 5-year Sharpe ratio of `0.77`, stronger than the category average of `0.08`. Downside protection is reinforced by a Sortino ratio of `3.62`, indicating far better than average risk-adjusted returns during negative stretches. The trailing 5-year beta of `0.07` is far below the index mark of `1.08`, confirming that this fund's volatility perfectly fits the mandate of a AAA-rated collateralized loan obligation strategy.

When examining extreme stress windows, the strategy's resilience becomes obvious. During the 2022 rate shock, its worst 5-year drawdown was restricted to `-2.3%` between `02/01/2022` and `06/30/2022`, shallower than the category's `-8.3%` drop and the index's `-16.5%` decline. Morningstar assigns it an overall risk score of `6`, classifying it as `Conservative` and safer than average. Over the trailing 5-year window, its `Below Avg.` risk versus category peers paired favorably with an `Above Avg.` return profile, demonstrating highly efficient downside management.

As a securitized bond fund focused on CLOs, the primary group-specific risk driver is duration and rate sensitivity. Because these underlying loans are floating-rate instruments, the fund carries near-zero interest rate duration. In an environment of spiking yields, this structural feature prevents the principal decay that hits traditional fixed-rate bonds. This dynamic is perfectly captured by its 5-year downside capture ratio of `-24`, vastly superior to the category's `12`. The trade-off is a 5-year upside capture of `29`, lower than the category's `41`, which is normal for floating-rate assets and limits upside in falling-rate environments.

The ETF's primary strengths are its immunity to interest-rate shocks and its ability to deliver peer-beating risk-adjusted returns, highlighted by a 5-year alpha of `1.21`, higher than the category's `0.03`. The main risk is credit spread widening; while AAA tranches rarely default, panic selling in credit markets can cause temporary liquidity-driven price drops. Compared to a short-term Treasury fund, this ETF takes slightly more credit spread risk but eliminates almost all rate risk while managing volatility effectively. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks strong because its floating-rate nature successfully neutralizes interest-rate shocks while delivering favorable risk-adjusted metrics.
