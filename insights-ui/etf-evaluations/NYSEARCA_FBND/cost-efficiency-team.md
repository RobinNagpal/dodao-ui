# FBND (NYSEARCA) — Cost, Efficiency & Team

_The cost and efficiency profile for Fidelity Total Bond ETF is **Mixed**. The fund charges a moderate active fee that sits above passive benchmarks, but compensates with a massive asset base and exceptionally deep liquidity. Investors accept higher portfolio rotation and an active management premium in exchange for a well-run core-plus mandate with seasoned leadership._

## Report

FBND charges an expense ratio of `0.36%`, which sits well above the near-zero cost of passive index trackers but aligns with the typical baseline for actively managed core-plus bond ETFs. The fund is massive and deeply liquid, managing `$25.09B` in AUM with huge daily trading activity, ensuring a retail round-trip is structurally cheap to execute. The portfolio provides broad exposure across government, corporate, and securitized debt, using its active mandate to allocate up to `20.00%` in lower-quality high yield bonds to boost income. Portfolio turnover is very high at `246.00%`, which is a mechanical consequence of its active fixed-income strategy and frequent sector rotations. For yield-driven investors, this active positioning translates to a trailing yield of `~4.70%`. Because the fund's income is generated from standard taxable corporate and government bonds, distributions are treated as ordinary income, making it best suited for tax-advantaged accounts to avoid ongoing tax drag. Fidelity is a premier institutional asset manager with extensive fixed-income research capabilities. The fund benefits from strong team stability, utilizing a nine-person management roster with an average tenure of `5.90 years`. The longest-serving manager has been at the helm since the fund's inception in `October 2014`, providing over a decade of continuous active management history and a reliable track record for investors to evaluate. The main strengths here are vast scale and the deep institutional backing of Fidelity's fixed-income desk. The primary drawback is its structural cost: the actively managed fee acts as a persistent hurdle against the absolute cheapest market options. For a direct retail alternative, the Vanguard Total Bond Market ETF (BND) offers standard intermediate core bond exposure for just `0.03%`. Choosing the Fidelity product means accepting higher pricing in exchange for active duration management and out-of-benchmark credit exposure. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks mixed because the fund delivers excellent trading execution and team pedigree, but at a clear premium in a heavily commoditized category.
