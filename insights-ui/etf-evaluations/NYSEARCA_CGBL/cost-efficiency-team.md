# CGBL (NYSEARCA) — Cost, Efficiency & Team

_The Capital Group Core Balanced ETF (CGBL) presents a strong cost and efficiency profile for investors seeking active multi-asset exposure. Its `0.33%` expense ratio is highly competitive for active management, though it carries a slight premium over static passive index allocations. Bolstered by massive scale with `$5.46B` in AUM and `19%` turnover, the fund minimizes both execution friction and internal drag. Overall, retail investors get access to a premier active manager at a very reasonable price point._

## Categories

- **Expense Ratio Vs Competition** _(result: Pass)_: The 0.33% fee is highly competitive against active peers, though slightly higher than passive benchmark equivalents.
- **Expense Ratio Vs Expected Returns** _(result: Pass)_: With under three years of history, long-term net returns are unproven, but the low structural fee gives active management a feasible hurdle to clear.
- **Bid Ask Spread And Trading Cost** _(result: Pass)_: Massive daily trading activity ensures frictionless execution for retail investors.
- **Management Quality And Track Record** _(result: Pass)_: Despite being less than three years old, the fund benefits from absolute manager continuity and a tier-one issuer.
- **Tax Efficiency** _(result: Pass)_: The 19% turnover limits capital gains, but the structural bond exposure makes it better suited for tax-deferred accounts.

## Report

At `0.33%`, the fund's expense ratio is favorably priced compared to the `~0.50%` median of active allocation peers, though it sits above the `~0.15%–0.25%` range expected for pure passive models. With a massive `$5.46B` in AUM and `$23.39M` in daily dollar volume, market liquidity is deep and retail bid-ask spreads are structurally tight, making round-trip trading cheap. As a Moderate Allocation fund, its defining exposure is a balanced mandate targeting `50%–75%` in equity securities and at least `25%` in debt instruments.

Portfolio turnover is remarkably low at `19%`, falling well beneath the `30%–60%` typical of active multi-asset managers and severely limiting internal trading drag. From a tax perspective, the fund's asset mix dictates its efficiency. Because the portfolio fundamentally relies on its minimum `25%` debt sleeve, it generates a steady stream of ordinary interest income rather than exclusively qualified dividends. Consequently, this target-mix structure is modestly less tax-efficient than pure equity and is structurally optimized for tax-advantaged retirement accounts.

Capital Group is a premier institutional issuer, bringing deep operational scale and stability to the fund. The ETF is technically unseasoned with an inception date of September 26, 2023, placing it under the standard three-year threshold for full historical evaluation. However, the longest manager tenure sits at `2.7 years`, which exactly matches the fund's age, indicating absolute continuity and zero turnover risk since launch. While the track record is strictly young, the issuer's established pedigree mitigates operational uncertainty.

The fund's primary strengths are its tremendous `$5.46B` asset base and highly efficient `19%` turnover rate, which collectively ensure a low-friction holding experience. The main tradeoff is the active management premium; its `0.33%` fee is a drag for investors who just want market beta. A retail investor could opt for the iShares Core Moderate Allocation ETF (AOM) at `0.25%` or the Vanguard Moderate Growth Fund (VSMGX) at `0.14%`, accepting a static, passive index glide-path in exchange for a lower baseline cost. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks strong because it prices a proven active management methodology at near-passive levels while executing with exceptional liquidity.
