# GUNR (NYSEARCA) — Cost, Efficiency & Team

_The cost and efficiency profile for this global commodity fund is **Strong**. The `0.46%` expense ratio is priced reasonably against the category average for specialized thematic exposure, while its massive `$7.6B` in assets under management completely eliminates closure risk. Supported by an exceptionally liquid `$24.9M` in daily dollar volume, retail investors can execute trades with negligible market friction. Finally, the low `28%` portfolio turnover confirms an efficient, passive tracking approach that minimizes internal drag. Overall, this vehicle provides deeply liquid and cost-effective access to global upstream resource producers._

## Report

The fund's headline expense ratio sits fairly within the expected band for a globally diversified thematic basket, reflecting the naturally higher curation costs compared to a plain-vanilla domestic sector tracker. Rather than isolating a single resource, the index buys a rules-based mix of global upstream producers, resulting in a reasonably diversified top end where the top three holdings—Corteva, Exxon Mobil, and BHP Group—combine for just `14.69%` of the portfolio. Supported by its multi-billion-dollar asset base and robust daily trading activity, the underlying liquidity easily handles tight execution, making retail round-trips highly cost-efficient without punishing spread slippage. The fund's portfolio turnover is perfectly aligned with the expectations for a passive index tracker that relies on market-cap weighting rather than active trading. From a structural tax standpoint, because this vehicle invests strictly in standard corporate equities rather than Master Limited Partnerships or physical commodities, it shields investors from partnership reporting friction and the punitive collectibles tax rate. Distributions come largely from cash-generative producers and are treated as standard qualified or ordinary dividends, though the actual payouts can swing noticeably with underlying commodity cycles and corporate buyback trends. Backed by FlexShares and its parent Northern Trust, the ETF benefits from top-tier institutional infrastructure and operational scale. The strategy was launched in September 2011, establishing a live track record of roughly `14.7 years` that has successfully navigated multiple severe commodity booms and busts. The management team features excellent continuity, highlighted by a longest manager tenure of `12.6 years`, ensuring stable oversight of the index methodology even though the passive mandate inherently minimizes traditional key-person risk. The fund's primary strengths are its exceptional trading liquidity and an intelligent index design that spreads exposure across energy, metals, and agriculture to cushion the blow when a single commodity sub-sector crashes. The main drawback is that its headline cost, while fair for a global thematic basket, is structurally more expensive than buying a hyper-cheap U.S. materials ETF. For a direct retail alternative, investors could consider the SPDR S&P Global Natural Resources ETF (GNR), which charges a slightly lower `0.40%` fee; however, choosing the cheaper peer means giving up this fund's explicit focus on upstream producers and its dedicated water and timber sleeves. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks strong because its pricing is competitive for its specialized global reach, and its deep institutional scale makes it a highly efficient core holding.
