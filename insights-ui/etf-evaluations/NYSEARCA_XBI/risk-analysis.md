# XBI (NYSEARCA) — Risk Analysis

_The risk profile of this ETF is Mixed. Morningstar assigns it a `Very Aggressive` risk level compared to similar funds, driven by an elevated 3-year beta of `1.28` that sits well above the category median of `0.97`. Despite the bumpier ride, it compensates investors effectively in favorable recent conditions, posting a 3-year upside capture ratio of `129` that is significantly better than the category's `78`. The fund also delivers a 3-year Sharpe ratio of `0.64`, higher than the category's `0.20`, rewarding the extra risk over this specific window. Ultimately, this is a tactical short-horizon trading tool or a small aggressive portfolio slice, not a core buy-and-hold asset._

## Categories

- **Overall Volatility** _(result: Pass)_: The fund experiences large price swings, consistently running far more volatile than its healthcare category peers.
- **Risk Adjusted Return** _(result: Fail)_: The fund fails to provide superior long-term risk-adjusted returns to compensate for its elevated volatility.
- **Worst Drawdown** _(result: Fail)_: The fund has suffered deep drops that are nearly double the magnitude of the average healthcare peer.
- **Risk Vs Peers** _(result: Fail)_: The ETF consistently carries a much higher risk profile than its category without delivering reliably superior returns.
- **Concentration Risk** _(result: Pass)_: The fund strongly limits single-stock risk through an equal-weight strategy, efficiently diversifying capital across its specific theme.

## Report

The fund's volatility runs extremely hot, purposefully delivering the amplified swings expected of an equal-weighted biotechnology mandate. Its 5-year standard deviation of `25.3%` is substantially higher than the broader healthcare category average of `18.3%`. Similarly, the 5-year beta of `0.94` sits higher than the category median of `0.83`, confirming that the fund reacts more aggressively to broader market movements. The volatility fits the stated objective of capturing early-stage price action, but it creates a relentlessly bumpy ride for investors.

During prolonged market stress, the fund has experienced steep pullbacks that go far beyond standard healthcare corrections. Its 5-year maximum drawdown reached `-55.2%`, plunging far deeper than the category's `-29.3%` decline over the same period. In this medium-term window, Morningstar grades the fund's risk versus category as `High`, while its return versus category registers as `Below Avg.`. The comparative gap in downside protection highlights that the structural focus on smaller capitalization companies magnifies losses when the overarching sector falls out of favor.

As a thematic equity fund, the primary group-specific risk driver is within-theme concentration and the resulting small-cap bias. Rather than anchoring the portfolio with mega-cap pharmaceutical names, the equal-weight methodology continuously shifts capital into smaller, highly volatile research firms. This structural trait causes the fund to fall harder during broad sell-offs, evidenced by its 3-year downside capture ratio of `150` against the benchmark, which is worse than the category's `130`. The complete lack of defensive, revenue-generating anchors leaves the portfolio fully exposed to the high-beta dynamics of the biotechnology industry.

The risk profile contains distinct trade-offs. On the positive side, the fund captures sector rallies efficiently, delivering a 10-year upside capture of `111` that is better than the category's `90`. Additionally, its 10-year alpha of `-0.14` performs better than the category average of `-0.28`. However, the long-term downside vulnerability remains a glaring red flag, as the 10-year downside capture of `129` is worse than the category's `98`. Because the fund utilizes an equal-weight approach, it completely sidesteps the single-stock concentration risks found in market-cap-weighted alternatives, but this same structure increases its vulnerability to broad funding dry-ups. Given the thematic-sleeve nature of the exposure, single-sector concentration means this asset typically belongs as a `5%` to `10%` portfolio slice. Overall, this ETF's risk profile looks mixed because its strict diversification effectively prevents isolated stock disasters, but its small-cap bias guarantees heavy sector-wide drawdowns.
