# TLT (NASDAQ) — Cost, Efficiency & Team

_The cost and efficiency profile for the iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF (TLT) is Strong. The fund charges a low 0.15% expense ratio, which provides affordable access to the long end of the U.S. government debt market. Execution is highly frictionless, anchored by substantial scale with $42.2B in AUM and nearly $943.4M in daily dollar trading volume. The portfolio avoids hidden trading drag through a modest 19.00% annual turnover rate over a track record dating back to July 2002. Ultimately, this ETF is a highly liquid and structurally sound vehicle for targeting Treasury yields._

## Report

The fund's expense ratio lands comfortably inside the 0.05–0.30% range expected for core fixed-income index trackers, though it carries a slight premium compared to ultra-cheap competitors. The portfolio offers deep liquidity, easily clearing the $500M safe-asset threshold with its large asset base. Retail investors can trade this ETF with practically zero slippage, as evidenced by an exceptionally tight 0.01% bid-ask spread, making round-trips highly cost-efficient. Because this is a broad long-government fund tracking a plain-label index, the exposure is straightforward and requires no active-fee justification.

Portfolio turnover sits at the lower end of the 30–80% expected band for core bond funds, reflecting disciplined tracking and avoiding unnecessary internal transaction costs. As a fixed-income product, yield is a primary driver for retail investors; the portfolio currently delivers a ~4.91% 30-day SEC yield. Because U.S. Treasury bonds trade over-the-counter, NAV execution reliability is an important consideration. Fortunately, the narrow spread and extensive secondary-market depth fully insulate retail buyers from underlying-market friction, ensuring fair-value pricing during normal conditions.

Issued by BlackRock under the iShares brand, the fund benefits from a broad operational footprint. The vehicle's lengthy history spans multiple interest-rate cycles without any concerning mandate changes, proving its structural resilience. Given the substantial asset base, the AUM trajectory remains exceptionally stable, meaning closure risk is effectively zero. Because this is a passively managed index fund, the longest continuous manager tenure of 14.8 years simply confirms a stable human-oversight layer rather than serving as a critical performance driver.

The ETF's main strengths are its heavy daily trading volume and tight execution, making it highly efficient to enter and exit. Its low churn further minimizes internal friction. The primary drawback is a fee that is slightly elevated for a basic Treasury index fund. For a cheaper alternative, long-term buy-and-hold investors might prefer the Vanguard Long-Term Treasury ETF (VGLT) at 0.04% or the SPDR Portfolio Long Term Treasury ETF (SPTL) at 0.06%, accepting less active options chains in exchange for the fee savings. Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks strong because its scale and trading efficiency fully offset the modest premium in its expense ratio for retail investors prioritizing liquidity.
