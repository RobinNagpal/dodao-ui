## Report

**Positioning snapshot.** The Avantis U.S. Mid Cap Value ETF (AVMV) targets mid-sized companies using a rules-based systematic approach that screens for both deep value and high profitability. This profitability overlay is the most critical quality lever for this category, ensuring the fund avoids low-quality "zombie" companies that often litter generic value indexes. The resulting portfolio of `287` holdings is cheap, cyclical, and tilted heavily toward classic value sectors, including Financial Services (`23.39%`), Consumer Cyclical (`17.47%`), Industrials (`15.16%`), and Energy (`13.95%`). Conversely, it is deeply underweight in defensive Utilities (`0.51%`) and interest-rate-sensitive Real Estate (`0.96%`), positioning it as a pro-cyclical vehicle rather than a defensive yield play. **Macro regime fit.** The current macro environment—defined by resilient economic growth and sticky inflation—is a structurally strong setup for this ETF's exposure profile over both the short and long horizons. As of May 2026, the ISM Manufacturing PMI (a key gauge of industrial activity) sits in expansionary territory at `52.7` (Institute for Supply Management, May 2026). Meanwhile, stubbornly high Core PCE inflation (the Fed's preferred price metric) at `3.3%` (Bureau of Economic Analysis, April 2026) has forced the central bank to hold rates steady at `3.50%–3.75%`, with futures markets pricing in renewed odds of a hike (CME FedWatch, May 2026). Over the next `6–12` months, this "higher for longer" rate regime provides a tailwind for AVMV's heavy financials weighting (via sustained net interest margins) and its energy sleeve, while the profitability screen protects its cyclical holdings from elevated borrowing costs. Near-term catalysts include the June FOMC meeting and upcoming monthly CPI prints; any signs of re-accelerating inflation will likely steepen the yield curve, further benefiting this value-heavy mix. Over a `3–5` year secular horizon, a persistent regime of higher structural inflation and robust capital expenditure broadly favors the asset-heavy, cash-producing businesses concentrated in this fund. **Valuation and cycle position.** The fund is currently in a mature markup phase of its cycle, having rallied `36.59%` over the trailing year and trading within `5.4%` of its all-time highs. Despite this strong momentum, valuations have not stretched into distribution-phase extremes. The portfolio trades at a reasonable forward P/E of `14.46x`—a notable discount to the broader S&P 400 and large-cap benchmarks—offering a credible margin of safety. While the headline `1.08%` dividend yield is modest, a remarkably low payout ratio of `17.42%` indicates that the underlying companies are generating robust earnings and aggressively retaining cash to fund operations or execute share buybacks. The fundamental trajectory remains supportive, as the fund's holdings demonstrate genuine cheapness backed by strong operating cash flows rather than deteriorating fundamentals. **Verdict.** The forward outlook is Favorable because the fund pairs a highly effective profitability-and-value selection screen with a macro regime that explicitly rewards cyclical, cash-flowing businesses in a higher-rate environment. This ETF fits long-horizon equity allocators seeking a robust domestic alternative to concentrated large-cap exposure, though its aggressive cyclical tilt means the fund could underperform if the economy tips into an unexpected recession. Flip your view to Mixed if the ISM Manufacturing PMI rolls over decisively below `50.0` or if high-yield credit spreads (the premium paid by risky corporate borrowers) widen past `450 bps`, as an industrial recession would quickly erode the earnings power of its cyclical holdings.
