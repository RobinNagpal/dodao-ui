# BLOK (NYSEARCA) — Cost, Efficiency & Team

_The cost and efficiency profile for the Amplify Blockchain Technology ETF is Mixed. The fund offers a deeply tenured, actively managed approach to digital assets, supported by a healthy $932M in AUM and an 8.3-year track record that spans multiple crypto cycles. However, its 0.70% expense ratio represents a structural premium over newer passive alternatives, and its equity-heavy strategy introduces indirect operating risks compared to holding spot assets. Overall, the fund is a well-scaled and professionally executed thematic vehicle, but cost-conscious retail investors have cheaper paths to blockchain exposure._

## Report

Amplify Blockchain Technology ETF (BLOK) is an actively managed thematic fund where the top three holdings (Hut 8, Cipher Digital, and TeraWulf) combine for roughly 13.69% of the portfolio. Rather than holding spot coins, the fund provides exposure through the equities of crypto-exposed businesses like miners, exchanges, and technology providers. The fund charges a 0.70% expense ratio, which sits predictably above the ~0.10–0.35% range of broad passive S&P 500 sector ETFs but aligns with the standard cost stack for active curation in a fast-moving niche. Supported by a healthy $932M in AUM, the fund trades an average daily volume of 241K shares or roughly $5.44M in dollar terms. The median bid-ask spread of 0.15% (Amplify ETFs, May 2026) is elevated compared to the sub-0.05% spreads of mainstream S&P 500 sectors, but remains entirely reasonable for retail investors executing routine round-trips in a specialized theme. The portfolio turns over at 50% annually, a moderate rate that sits comfortably within the expected band for an actively managed, fundamentally driven equity strategy without incurring excessive hidden trading drag. Because the underlying basket consists of crypto miners and growth-stage digital asset companies, total return is driven purely by price appreciation rather than dividend yield. Crucially, as an equity ETF rather than a physical commodity wrapper or a futures-based trust, the fund avoids the tax-time frictions of K-1 partnership reporting and the higher 28% collectibles tax rate applied to physical metals. The underlying structure relies on standard in-kind creation and redemption, keeping it largely tax-efficient, though the active turnover means taxable investors should monitor for occasional capital-gain distributions. Issued by Amplify Investments, the fund is supported by a boutique but established operator in the thematic ETF space. The mandate was launched 8.3 years ago, meaning the strategy has survived multiple highly volatile crypto winters and demonstrated long-term operational resilience. Manager continuity is exceptionally strong; the three-person management team features an average tenure of 7.3 years, with the longest-serving manager matching the fund's entire 8.3-year lifespan. This ensures the active security selection and risk management remain consistent with the original vision. The fund's primary strengths are its proven $932M scale and a deeply tenured management team that provides clean, well-curated exposure without the structural roll costs of synthetic crypto futures. However, the 0.70% fee is a persistent hurdle, and the concentrated equity basket acts as a high-beta proxy to Bitcoin, layering operating and balance-sheet leverage that can magnify drawdowns far beyond the underlying coin. For a direct retail alternative, the VanEck Digital Transformation ETF (DAPP) offers similar blockchain equity exposure at a lower 0.50% fee, though investors must accept a passive index approach rather than active curation. Alternatively, those wanting clean coin exposure without the equity risk could opt for a spot product like IBIT (0.25%). Overall, this ETF's cost profile looks mixed; it provides a highly seasoned, efficiently managed active strategy, but retail investors pay a premium price for indirect asset exposure.
