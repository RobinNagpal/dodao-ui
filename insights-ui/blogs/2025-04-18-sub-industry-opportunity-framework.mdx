We will be building an **AI‑driven reporting engine** that will systematically scan the entire GICS universe to identify the sub‑industries most likely to **benefit from U.S.–China tariff actions**. By feeding trade flows, company financials, policy updates, and alternative‑sourcing data into our AI Agent, we can generate a consistent, scalable, and continuously updated set of deep‑dive reports.

To ensure each AI output meets the **highest consulting standards**, we selected a **McKinsey/BCG/Bain‑inspired blueprint**. This framework guarantees that every section is:
1. **Top‑down & hypothesis‑driven**—so the narrative flows logically from big-picture questions to data‑backed conclusions.
2. **MECE**—so there is no overlap or omission across sections.
3. **Actionable**—each sub‑heading comes with 5–6 precise data inputs, 4–5 simple but powerful charts, and a crystal‑clear “so‑what” that ties back to investment decisions.

Below is the consultant‑grade blueprint you can reuse for **any GICS sub‑industry** affected by the U.S.–China tariff dispute.
For every sub‑heading you get exactly **5–6 data ideas, 4–5 exhibit ideas, and a clear “so‑what”** that ties the analysis to an investment decision.

### **How to Deploy This Framework**

1. **Populate Section 1** with publicly available data (10‑K, Capital IQ, UN Comtrade).  
2. **Break down product P&L** for Section 2 using segment notes and management disclosure.  
3. **Build a unified 5‑yr model** for every company; feed outputs to Section 3 exhibits.  
4. **Score and rank** using Section 4; overlay qualitative judgement to avoid quant‑only traps.  
5. **Update quarterly** as tariffs, FX, and company guidance shift.

Following these four sections yields a **60–80‑page comparative deck** that meets MBB standards and ends with a single, defensible investment call.

## How to Use This Blueprint

1. **Pick one GICS sub‑industry** (e.g., “Industrial Machinery”).  
2. Populate the **Tariff‑Impact Dashboard** with HS‑code duties and cost stacks.  
3. Build the **Market & Competitive Landscape** using UN Comtrade, Capital IQ, and industry reports.  
4. **Screen public companies** (Section 3.4) but *defer* the deep dives until you next request Section 5.2.  
5. Run the **financial model & scenario analysis**; feed results back into 1.2 and 5.3.  
6. Assemble appendices for audit trail and committee credibility.

Following this structure guarantees a report that is **MECE, hypothesis‑driven, visually compelling, and investment‑ready**—fully aligned with McKinsey, BCG, and Bain standards.
