**1.1 Tariff‑Impact Dashboard**

**Effective duty rates on HS 39 (Plastics & Articles) – Pre vs. Post**
Before the new measures, the average Most‑Favored‑Nation (MFN) duty on imported plastics under HS Chapter 39 was approximately **3.1%** according to the U.S. International Trade Commission’s Harmonized Tariff Schedule (HTS) ([hts.usitc.gov/chapter/39](https://hts.usitc.gov/chapter/39)). After Executive Order 14257 (“Liberation Day”), Chinese‑origin plastics face an additional **125%** reciprocal tariff, for an **effective duty of ~128.1%** ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/markets/us-excludes-smartphones-computers-reciprocal-tariffs-2025-04-12/)).

**Import reliance ratio (% of COGS)**
In February 2025, the U.S. plastics and rubber products manufacturing subsector recorded shipments of **\$24.9 billion** (annualized) in the Census Bureau’s Monthly Manufacturing Report ([census.gov/manufacturing/m3](https://www.census.gov/manufacturing/m3)). In 2023, total U.S. imports of plastics and rubber were **\$100.4 billion**, of which **\$20.7 billion** (20.6%) came from China, per the Peterson Institute for International Economics ([piie.com blog](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2024/no-trade-tax-free-trumps-promised-tariffs-will-hit-large-flows)).

**Incremental cost per unit (USD/kg)**
North American polypropylene (PP) prices averaged **\$1.19/kg** in April 2025 ([BusinessAnalytiq](https://businessanalytiq.com/procurementanalytics/index/polypropylene-price-index/)). A 125% tariff adds **\$1.49/kg**, nearly doubling landed cost to **\$2.68/kg**, or **\$0.75** extra for a 0.5 kg part.

**EBITDA margin delta (bps)**
Westlake Corporation’s full‑year 2024 report shows **\$12.1 billion** in net sales and **\$2.3 billion** in EBITDA, implying a **19.0%** margin ([StockTitan](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/WLK/westlake-corporation-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2024-80dqwtgazpi8.html)). With polymer feedstock ~15% of COGS, doubling resin cost raises total COGS by roughly **1.9%**, compressing EBITDA margins by about **190 bps** before any mitigation.

**Abnormal share‑price return (announcement ± 3 days)**
Shares of LyondellBasell (NYSE: LYB) fell from **\$70.95** on April 3, 2025, to **\$59.83** on April 9, 2025—a **15.8%** drop over five trading days ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/LYB/history?p=LYB)).

**Management quote on mitigation actions**
“PLASTICS remains deeply concerned about the tariffs on Canada, Mexico, and China, and their impact on U.S. plastics manufacturing and jobs. We urge policymakers to support balanced trade policies that enhance U.S. competitiveness, reinforce supply chains, and drive continued innovation,” said Matt Seaholm, President & CEO of the Plastics Industry Association ([PLASTICS newsroom](https://www.plasticsindustry.org/newsroom/plastics-industry-association-shares-concern-about-tariffs-urges-trade-policies-that-strengthen-u-s-manufacturing/)).

**Unique insight**
A **> 125 percentage‑point** jump in duties effectively eliminates Chinese cost advantage, structurally benefiting domestic producers with integrated production and distribution.

## 1. Feedstock & Monomer Production

**Process**
– **Crude oil and natural gas liquids (NGLs)** are fractionated and then **steam‑cracked** to produce light olefins—predominantly **ethylene** and **propylene**.
– These monomers are the building blocks for most commercial thermoplastics.

**Key U.S. Players**
– **ExxonMobil Chemical** (part of XOM)
– **Chevron Phillips Chemical** (joint venture of Chevron & Phillips 66)
– **Shell Chemical** (part of Shell plc)
– **Sasol** (via their U.S. Gulf Coast cracker)



- No direct plastics imports at this stage—crude oil and NGLs are processed in huge petrochemical complexes._

– Olefins are polymerized into pelletized **resins**:

**Key U.S. Players**
– **Dow Inc.** (NYSE: DOW)
– **LyondellBasell Industries** (NYSE: LYB)
– **Westlake Chemical** (NYSE: WLK)

## 3. Compounding & Masterbatch

**Process**
– Base resins are blended with **additives** (colorants, UV stabilizers, flame retardants) and **fillers** to produce **masterbatches** and **specialty compounds** tailored for specific end‑use properties.

**Key U.S. Players**
– **Avient Corp.** (NYSE: AVNT)
– **Eastman Chemical** (NYSE: EMN)
– **Cabot Corp.** (NYSE: COG)

**Import Notes**
Most feedstock comes from domestic resin producers. Imports of specialized masterbatch components are minimal (< 1 % of raw‐resin imports) and largely sourced from North America/Europe.

**Tariff Impact**: **Positive.**
With Chinese‐sourced specialty compounds negligible, U.S. compounders rely on domestic resins and additive suppliers, benefiting from stable input costs.

_Resins + additives → tailor‑made color, UV, flame‑retardant concentrates._

## 7. Recycling & Re‑Use

**Process**
– **Mechanical recycling**: grinding, pelletizing post‑consumer scrap
– **Chemical recycling**: depolymerization back to monomers
– **Key Players**: Waste Management (NYSE: WM), Veolia (EPA: VEOEY), KW Plastics (private)

**Import Notes**
– U.S. does not import recycled plastic (exports a small stream of mixed scrap).
– Tariffs do not directly affect domestic recycling flows.

**Tariff Impact**: Neutral.

_Mechanical & chemical recycling; very little imports of recycled resin._

## 1. Upstream Feedstock: Oil & Gas Extraction and Refining

### 1.1 Process Overview
The plastic value chain begins with the extraction of hydrocarbons—crude oil and natural gas liquids (NGLs)—from onshore and offshore wells. These feedstocks are processed in refineries and fractionation plants to yield intermediates such as naphtha, ethane, and propane, which serve as the monomer precursors for most commercial polymers.

### 1.2 Raw Materials & Sourcing
- **Crude oil** (liquid) and **natural gas** (gaseous), obtained via drilling and hydraulic fracturing.
- **NGLs** (ethane, propane, butane) recovered at gas‐processing plants.
- Transport via pipelines, rail, and tanker ships to downstream petrochemical sites.

### 1.3 Outputs
- **Naphtha** (C₅–C₁₀ hydrocarbons)
- **Ethane/Propane** (C₂–C₃)
- **Refinery by‐products** (Coke, fuel gases)

### 1.4 Customers
- Steam‐cracker plants of integrated chemical companies
- Merchant NGL buyers
- Independent petrochemical firms

## 2. Monomer & Resin Production

### 2.1 Steam Cracking & Polymerization
Refined feedstocks undergo **steam cracking** at 800–900 °C to yield light olefins (ethylene, propylene). Subsequent **polymerization** (via Ziegler–Natta or metallocene catalysts) produces base resins: polyethylene (HDPE, LDPE, LLDPE), polypropylene (PP), polystyrene (PS), PVC, etc.

### 2.2 Raw Materials & Sourcing
- Ethane, propane, naphtha from upstream refiners.
- Catalysts and co‐monomers.

### 2.3 Outputs
- **Resin pellets** (granules) in base grades for conversion.

### 2.4 Customers
- Compounders (masterbatch & additives)
- Distributors
- Large converters

- **Overall U.S. plastics industry** shipped **$519 B** in 2023 and grew ~ 2.2 % CAGR over five years citeturn0search1.

- **Key systemic headwinds**: decarbonization regulations, circular‑economy requirements, recycled content quality, labor & CapEx pressure.
- **Key systemic tailwinds**: reshoring, bio‑based & recycling technologies, digitalization, ongoing demand in packaging, medical, & automotive sectors.
